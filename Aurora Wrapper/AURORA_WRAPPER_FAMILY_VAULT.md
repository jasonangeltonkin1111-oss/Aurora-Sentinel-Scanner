This vault compiles the active family-first doctrine from the family registry, family system map, core family files, family cards, and family competition protocol/schema/example.
It gives the wrapper enough family doctrine to reason faithfully without loading every source family file.

## What this vault excludes

- conditional, preserved, or future family classes as hot-path doctrine
- raw source-card duplication beyond the compiled essentials below
- pattern-local doctrine, which belongs in the pattern vault

## Family-layer law

Aurora is family-first.
Family competition sits after context interpretation and before pattern competition, opportunity classification, deployability, geometry, and card decisions.
Family doctrine must be consumed, not replaced by generic trend/range abstractions.

## Family competition laws

- no forced family winner
- no numeric-confidence scoring
- preserve candidate visibility under partial context
- allow `DEFERRED_CLASSIFICATION` when the candidate set is real but insufficiently resolved
- allow `NO_VALID_FAMILY` and `INVALID_INPUT` when required
- compare by habitat fit, anti-habitat conflict, state alignment, regime compatibility, contradiction load, and dependence on missing surfaces

Canonical wrapper-visible outcomes:
- `CLEAR_PRIMARY`
- `CONTESTED_PRIMARY`
- `MULTIPLE_LIVE_CANDIDATES`
- `DEFERRED_CLASSIFICATION`

- `INVALID_INPUT`

## Core system structure C-01 through C-05

- `C-01` continuation after accepted directional authority already exists
- `C-02` continuation-through-retracement when prior directional authority remains intact but must be restored through pullback logic
- `C-03` breakout / compression-release transition from containment into expansion
- `C-04` failed-break / trap-reversal transition where attempted continuation loses authority and reclaim logic takes over
- `C-05` balance rotation / range mean-reversion where accepted value and rotational trade dominate over directional migration

The family layer answers: which organizing engine best explains the case now, which competitors remain alive, and which missing surfaces would change that answer.

## Stage-aware family ecology

Use family stage logic as a family-specific timing language, not a universal scorecard.
Remember:
- continuation families often stay legitimate into mature, late, and re-entry states
- breakout-release families are often strongest earlier and can expire faster
- failed-break families often require later confirmation before they become trustworthy
- balance-rotation families may become more legitimate after the range is already established
- bucket and regime context can expand or compress a stage's usable life

### C-01 — Trend Continuation
- **Canonical thesis:** continuation of already established directional discovery or trend expansion.
- **Native habitat:** clean directional structure, strong discovery posture, post-acceptance continuation, low exhaustion evidence.
- **Anti-habitat:** unstable balance, unresolved compression, failed-break conditions, mixed overlap, late exhaustion.
- **Required surface:** `S1_CHART_STRUCTURE`.
- **High-value supporting surfaces:** `S2_ASC_INTERNAL_FEATURES`, `S3_LIVE_EXECUTION_MICROSTRUCTURE`.
- **Typical suppressors:** low-trust continuation, degraded directional performance, responsive opposition taking control, exhausted extension.
- **Main competitors:** C-03 for fresh release, C-02 for genuine pullback-resumption, C-04 for failure, C-05 when rotation retakes control.
- **Anti-confusion boundary:** do not treat a merely attempted direction or unresolved breakout as true continuation.

### C-02 — Trend Pullback / Pullback Continuation
- **Canonical thesis:** established directional bias resumes through a constructive retracement rather than a fresh breakout.
- **Native habitat:** intact directional discovery with orderly pullback and restoration signals.
- **Anti-habitat:** full failed-state logic, reforming balance, widening instability, very late extension, deterioration disguised as retracement.
- **Required surface:** `S1_CHART_STRUCTURE`.
- **High-value supporting surfaces:** `S2_ASC_INTERNAL_FEATURES`, `S3_LIVE_EXECUTION_MICROSTRUCTURE`.
- **Typical suppressors:** opposing acceptance grows, pullback ceases to be subordinate, reversal logic takes over.
- **Main competitors:** C-01 if no real pullback exists, C-04 if reclaim/failure logic dominates, C-05 if balance re-forms.
- **Anti-confusion boundary:** do not misclassify structural deterioration, unstable overlap, or full failed state as healthy pullback continuation.

### C-03 — Breakout / Compression Release
- **Canonical thesis:** stored structure resolves through release from compression, balance edge, or boundary pressure.
- **Native habitat:** compression, containment, pressure build, balance resolving into directional discovery.
- **Anti-habitat:** wick-only excursion, unresolved false commitment, unstable overlap, cleanly holding balance, late mature extension.
- **Required surface:** `S1_CHART_STRUCTURE`.
- **High-value supporting surfaces:** `S2_ASC_INTERNAL_FEATURES`, `S3_LIVE_EXECUTION_MICROSTRUCTURE`, `S6_CALENDAR_SESSION_TIMEBOX`.
- **Typical suppressors:** weak hold quality, immediate failure/reclaim, low-trust expansion, absence of real prior compression.
- **Main competitors:** C-01 for healthy continuation, C-04 for failed release, C-05 when price is still fundamentally rotational.
- **Anti-confusion boundary:** do not label a one-bar spike or unresolved excursion as genuine compression release.

### C-04 — Failed Break / Trap Reversal
- **Canonical thesis:** directional attempt fails, reclaims, and reverses as trapped participation unwinds.
- **Native habitat:** failed break, false commitment, rejected breakout, reclaim after rejection, unstable transition turning against prior direction.
- **Anti-habitat:** unresolved breakout, fresh accepted release, trap-on-trap chaos with no clear hierarchy, pure balance rotation.
- **Required surface:** `S1_CHART_STRUCTURE`.
- **High-value supporting surfaces:** `S2_ASC_INTERNAL_FEATURES`, `S3_LIVE_EXECUTION_MICROSTRUCTURE`, `S6_CALENDAR_SESSION_TIMEBOX`.
- **Typical suppressors:** lack of visible break attempt, reclaim not yet structurally secured, continuation authority restored, unstable whipsaw.
- **Main competitors:** C-03 if release is still valid, C-01 if original direction remains healthy, C-05 if the case is accepted rotation rather than trap unwind.
- **Anti-confusion boundary:** do not confuse ordinary pullback or unresolved breakout drift with a true failed-break hierarchy.

### C-05 — Balance Rotation / Range Mean-Reversion
- **Canonical thesis:** value-dominant rotation inside balance where excursion rejects and price returns toward acceptance/value.
- **Native habitat:** balance, auction rotation, meaningful range extremes, accepted rotational trade.
- **Anti-habitat:** unstable balance, emerging breakout acceptance, widening chaos, transition into strong directional discovery, failed break that is no longer merely rotational.
- **Required surface:** `S1_CHART_STRUCTURE`.
- **High-value supporting surfaces:** `S2_ASC_INTERNAL_FEATURES`, `S3_LIVE_EXECUTION_MICROSTRUCTURE`, `S6_CALENDAR_SESSION_TIMEBOX`.
- **Typical suppressors:** directional authority growing, range stability degrading, rejection quality weak, migration stronger than return-to-value logic.
- **Main competitors:** C-03 when balance is preparing to resolve, C-04 when rejection is really trap reversal, C-01/C-02 when directional structure dominates.
- **Anti-confusion boundary:** do not call unstable overlap or early breakout acceptance a healthy rotational family.

## Family preservation fields

For each live family candidate, the wrapper should preserve:
- canonical thesis
- habitat and anti-habitat
- state/surface dependencies
- required versus supporting data surfaces
- structural entry logic at high level
- structural invalidation logic at high level
- anti-confusions
- common failure modes
- competitor relationships
- candidate visibility even when not primary

## Family competition implications the wrapper must remember

- weaker but still plausible families stay visible if rejection would depend on missing surfaces
- deployability can modify family confidence posture but cannot retroactively rewrite the family read into a different family
- symbol shells must not become family doctrine; family logic is broader than one illustrative ticker
- family competition should still name what would reduce ambiguity, not just what currently wins
