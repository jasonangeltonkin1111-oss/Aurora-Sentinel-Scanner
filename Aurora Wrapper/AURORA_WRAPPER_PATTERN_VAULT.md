## Root pattern law

Pattern routing is not chart-label matching.
A pattern is the local structural role expressed inside surviving family posture.
Patterns inherit meaning from family posture, stage posture, regime posture, and anti-confusion pressure.
No pattern should be treated as valid just because the local shape looks familiar.

## Pattern competition law

A wrapper-only GPT must:
- route only after family posture is valid enough
- compare local candidates inside the surviving family ecology
- preserve ambiguity when local structure is still transitional
- allow deferred, contested, multiple-live, and no-valid-pattern outcomes
- avoid hidden numeric confidence

Competition dimensions to preserve:
- parent-family compatibility
- structural role fit
- anti-confusion pressure
- state alignment
- regime compatibility
- contradiction load
- dependence on missing surfaces
- evidence not yet present
- deployability compatibility modifier

## Pattern routing questions

1. What surviving family posture owns the local structure?
2. What local structural role is actually being expressed?
3. What opportunity stage is the pattern in now?
4. What anti-confusions matter most here?
5. What keeps the pattern alive, deferred, or invalid?

## Pattern structure G1 through G5

### G1 — Accepted Break and Hold
**Parent families:** mainly C-01 continuation and C-03 breakout/release.
**Local meaning:** structure is not just crossing a boundary; it is accepting and holding beyond it.
**Strong stages:** confirmed and early continuation.
**Main anti-confusions:** spike-through mistaken for acceptance, late exhausted extension mistaken for healthy holding, reclaimed excursion mistaken for persistence.
**Routing clue:** if truth depends on whether the move is genuinely holding outside prior structure, G1 stays live.

### G2 — Orderly Continuation Pullback
**Parent families:** mainly C-02, sometimes C-01.
**Local meaning:** retracement remains subordinate and constructive inside ongoing directional authority.
**Strong stages:** developing, continuation, re-entry.
**Main anti-confusions:** deterioration mistaken for healthy pullback, reversal logic mistaken for restoration, balance reform mistaken for continuation.
**Routing clue:** if the local question is whether retracement is still constructive, G2 deserves weight.

### G3 — Compression Release
**Parent families:** mainly C-03.
**Local meaning:** meaningful containment/compression is resolving into actual expansion.
**Strong stages:** emergent, developing, confirmed, sometimes early continuation.
**Main anti-confusions:** random volatility mistaken for release, expansion with no real compression parent, weak non-persistent break mistaken for real release.
**Routing clue:** if stored pressure resolution is the local story, G3 remains near the top.

### G4 — Failed Break / Reclaim
**Parent families:** mainly C-04.
**Local meaning:** prior release or directional attempt has failed and reclaim hierarchy now gives the case meaning.
**Strong stages:** developing, confirmed, sometimes salvage/re-entry.
**Main anti-confusions:** unresolved breakout drift mistaken for failure, ordinary pullback mistaken for reclaim, unstable chop mistaken for clean failed-break structure.
**Routing clue:** if the case only makes sense after acknowledging failure of the prior move, G4 deserves real weight.

### G5 — Range Rejection / Return to Value
**Parent families:** mainly C-05.
**Local meaning:** excursion from meaningful balance rejects and points back toward accepted value.
**Strong stages:** confirmed balance conditions.
**Main anti-confusions:** edge touch mistaken for rejection, emerging breakout mistaken for rotational return, fake balance assumed where migration pressure dominates.
**Routing clue:** if the local story is rejection back into accepted value rather than discovery away from it, G5 should stay live.

## Pattern-card logic the wrapper must preserve

From the atlas and current pattern-card posture, preserve that pattern cards exist to capture:
- parent-family anchor
- pattern role
- confirmation requirements
- invalidation cues
- anti-confusions
- stage profile
- downstream geometry implications

Pattern cards do not overrule family law.
They sharpen local expression inside that law.

## Transitional and ambiguous pattern posture

Wrapper-only operation must preserve transitional cases such as:
- release versus reclaim not yet resolved
- pullback versus deterioration not yet resolved
- rejection versus breakout acceptance not yet resolved

Use deferred or contested wording rather than forcing a winner for elegance.

## Pattern-to-later-layer law

Patterns influence:
- which opportunity stage is honest
- what confirmation is still needed
- which revisit triggers matter
- what geometry style is even eligible
- whether a card should stay blocked despite an interesting family read

Patterns do not independently authorize deployability or geometry.
