# AURORA WRAPPER PATTERN VAULT

## What this vault compiles

This vault compiles the active pattern doctrine from the pattern atlas, pattern cards, and pattern competition protocol/schema/example.
It preserves pattern meaning as family-attached structural roles rather than detached labels.

## What this vault excludes

- family-selection logic that belongs upstream in the family vault
- raw duplication of every pattern source field when compiled essentials are enough
- unsupported pattern expansion beyond the current active set G1-G5

## Pattern-layer law

Pattern competition is downstream of family competition.
Patterns must consume the surviving family posture and must not silently reopen family selection.
Pattern meaning remains owned by the atlas and cards, not by ad hoc wrapper paraphrase.

## Pattern competition laws

- family-first law
- no forced pattern winner
- no numeric-confidence scoring
- preserve missingness and ambiguity
- allow `DEFERRED_PATTERN_COMPETITION` when the family route is real but still under-resolved
- compare by parent-family compatibility, structural role fit, anti-confusion pressure, state alignment, regime compatibility, contradiction load, and dependence on missing surfaces

Canonical wrapper-visible outcomes:
- `CLEAR_PRIMARY`
- `CONTESTED_PRIMARY`
- `MULTIPLE_LIVE_CANDIDATES`
- `DEFERRED_PATTERN_COMPETITION`
- `NO_VALID_PATTERN`
- `INVALID_INPUT`

## Current pattern canon

### G1 — Accepted Break and Hold
- **Parent families:** mainly C-01 and C-03.
- **Structural role:** successful acceptance outside prior containment; release is already holding.
- **Native habitat:** directional discovery, trend expansion, balance-to-expansion transition with credible hold.
- **Main anti-confusion:** do not label a failed excursion or weak hold as accepted break.

### G2 — Orderly Continuation Pullback
- **Parent families:** mainly C-02, sometimes C-01.
- **Structural role:** constructive retracement inside intact directional structure.
- **Native habitat:** trend pullback with orderly restoration potential.
- **Main anti-confusion:** do not misread messy reversal/exhaustion as orderly continuation pullback.

### G3 — Compression Release
- **Parent families:** mainly C-03.
- **Structural role:** local breakout-release expression from meaningful compression.
- **Native habitat:** compression resolving into expansion or balance-to-break transition.
- **Main anti-confusion:** do not use when expansion never truly confirms or compression was never real.

### G4 — Failed Break / Reclaim
- **Parent families:** mainly C-04.
- **Structural role:** failed release reverting through initiating structure with reclaim confirmation.
- **Native habitat:** failed break/trap state, false release, rejection then reclaim.
- **Main anti-confusion:** do not misclassify unresolved breakout drift as confirmed failed-break reclaim.

### G5 — Range Rejection / Return to Value
- **Parent families:** mainly C-05.
- **Structural role:** rejection from range edge or failed excursion back toward accepted value.
- **Native habitat:** balance/range, stable accepted rotational trade, meaningful range extremes.
- **Main anti-confusion:** do not use when price is actually leaving balance into trend or breakout discovery.

## Pattern preservation fields

For each candidate pattern, preserve:
- thesis and structural meaning
- parent family compatibility
- habitat and state/surface dependencies
- confirmation logic
- invalidation logic
- anti-confusions and false-positive risks
- candidate visibility when not yet decisive

## Canonical example anchor

The active pattern competition example and the C-04 / G4 lane show why Aurora preserves ambiguity before geometry:
`G4 Failed Break / Reclaim` can survive while unresolved breakout-versus-reclaim tension remains explicit instead of being rewritten away.

Replace this vault when pattern competition law, atlas posture, or the current G1-G5 canon changes materially.
