# AURORA WRAPPER FAMILY ROUTING MANUAL V1

## PURPOSE

This file deepens the wrapper's family knowledge so a GPT using only `Aurora Wrapper/` can reason more faithfully without relying on hidden Blueprint access.

It is not a replacement for:
- `AURORA_WRAPPER_FAMILY_VAULT.md`
- `AURORA_WRAPPER_EXECUTION_PACK.md`

It is a densification layer for wrapper-only use.

Its job is to preserve the kinds of family-routing knowledge that often get lost when source doctrine is compiled too aggressively:
- what family questions to ask first
- what family confusion patterns matter most
- how stage and bucket context change legitimacy
- how to preserve multiple live family candidates without flattening the case
- what should remain unresolved instead of forced

---

## ROOT LAW

Family routing is not label assignment.
It is the act of preserving the most truthful organizing engine for the case while keeping plausible competitors alive when the context still supports them.

A wrapper-only GPT should therefore treat family routing as:
- structural
- comparative
- stage-aware
- bucket-aware
- uncertainty-preserving

not as a fast pattern label or a trend/range shortcut.

---

## FAMILY ROUTING QUESTIONS

When reasoning from wrapper files alone, the model should ask these family questions in order.

### 1. What is the dominant structural regime now?
Possible answers include:
- accepted directional authority already exists
- directional authority exists but is being tested through retracement
- containment/compression pressure is resolving
- prior break attempt is failing and reclaim logic is taking over
- accepted value/balance/rotation dominates
- no family is yet honest enough to promote

This is the first family-routing layer.
Do not jump to pattern names before this is answered.

### 2. What is the most honest family habitat?
Ask:
- where does the current structure naturally belong?
- which family habitat best fits the observable state?
- which family anti-habitats are already triggered?

### 3. What is the current opportunity stage inside that family?
Ask:
- is this emergent, developing, confirmed, mature, continuation, re-entry, salvage, late, or exhausted?
- is the stage native for this family or only weakly tolerated?
- would a later stage still be valid for this family even if it would be weak in another family?

### 4. What bucket or regime pressure changes the family answer?
Ask:
- does this environment support this family cleanly?
- is this family valid here but burdened?
- does this bucket compress the useful life of this family's current stage?
- does this bucket make a competitor family more likely?

### 5. Which competing families remain honestly alive?
Ask:
- what else could this still be?
- what missing surface would change the family winner?
- does this deserve contested or deferred family posture instead of a forced primary?

## CANONICAL FAMILY ECOLOGY

## C-01 — Trend Continuation

### Best use case
Use when directional authority is already accepted and the main question is whether continuation still deserves preservation.

### Native structure
- already-established directional discovery
- continuation after acceptance
- directional authority remains dominant over rotation or failure
- post-confirmation continuation rather than fresh release from compression

### Strong stage legitimacy
Most legitimate in:
- `STAGE_CONFIRMED`
- `STAGE_MATURE`
- `STAGE_CONTINUATION`

Still potentially legitimate in:
- `STAGE_LATE`
- `STAGE_REENTRY`

Usually weak in:
- `STAGE_EMERGENT`
- `STAGE_EXHAUSTED`

### Common family confusions
- confused with C-03 when the move is actually a fresh release, not established continuation
- confused with C-02 when the structure is really a constructive retracement rather than direct continuation
- confused with C-04 when the prior directional authority is actually failing and reclaim logic is beginning

### Wrapper-only routing clue
If the move already has accepted directional authority and the main challenge is whether the continuation still has enough path left, C-01 is usually the first serious candidate.

---

## C-02 — Trend Pullback / Pullback Continuation

### Best use case
Use when directional authority exists, but the case depends on whether a retracement remains subordinate rather than becoming deterioration.

### Native structure
- prior directional authority is real
- pullback is visible and constructive
- the case depends on restoration/resumption rather than fresh break initiation
- retracement has not yet become accepted reversal or balance reform

### Strong stage legitimacy
Most legitimate in:
- `STAGE_DEVELOPING`
- `STAGE_CONFIRMED`
- `STAGE_CONTINUATION`

Still potentially legitimate in:
- `STAGE_REENTRY`
- `STAGE_MATURE`

Usually weak in:
- `STAGE_LATE` when pullback quality has degraded into failure
- `STAGE_EXHAUSTED`

### Common family confusions
- confused with C-01 when the retracement is too shallow to matter structurally
- confused with C-04 when the restoration attempt is really failed-state logic
- confused with C-05 when balance has actually re-formed instead of trend resuming

### Wrapper-only routing clue
If the trend still looks structurally alive but the local case depends on whether the retracement is healthy or deteriorating, C-02 should stay active.

---

## C-03 — Breakout / Compression Release

### Best use case
Use when the structure is transitioning from containment into release and the core question is whether expansion is real.

### Native structure
- compression
- containment
- pressure build
- boundary resolution
- transition from held structure into directional release

### Strong stage legitimacy
Most legitimate in:
- `STAGE_EMERGENT`
- `STAGE_DEVELOPING`
- `STAGE_CONFIRMED`

Still potentially legitimate in:
- early `STAGE_CONTINUATION`

Usually weak in:
- `STAGE_LATE`
- `STAGE_EXHAUSTED`

### Common family confusions
- confused with C-01 when the breakout is actually just ongoing continuation inside an already accepted move
- confused with C-04 when the release is weak and reclaim/failure logic takes over quickly
- confused with C-05 when price is still fundamentally rotational and has not truly resolved

### Wrapper-only routing clue
If the move's identity comes mainly from release out of pressure rather than already-established directional authority, C-03 should stay near the top.

---

## C-04 — Failed Break / Trap Reversal

### Best use case
Use when prior directional or breakout logic is failing and the real opportunity may now live in reclaim and unwind structure.

### Native structure
- failed break
- false commitment
- reclaim after rejection
- directional attempt loses authority
- trap unwind or failed continuation transition

### Strong stage legitimacy
Most legitimate in:
- `STAGE_DEVELOPING`
- `STAGE_CONFIRMED`

Still potentially legitimate in:
- `STAGE_SALVAGE`
- `STAGE_REENTRY`

Usually weak in:
- `STAGE_EMERGENT`
- `STAGE_EXHAUSTED`

### Common family confusions
- confused with C-03 when the release has not actually failed yet
- confused with C-02 when ordinary retracement is mistaken for full failure logic
- confused with C-05 when rejection is rotational rather than trap-unwind structural reversal

### Wrapper-only routing clue
If the prior move must first be judged as having failed before the current case makes sense, C-04 deserves serious weight.

---

## C-05 — Balance Rotation / Range Mean-Reversion

### Best use case
Use when accepted value and rotational trade dominate over migration and directional discovery.

### Native structure
- accepted balance
- value rotation
- range-edge rejection
- return-to-value logic
- excursion that is subordinate to accepted rotational structure

### Strong stage legitimacy
Most legitimate in:
- `STAGE_CONFIRMED`
- `STAGE_MATURE`

Still potentially legitimate in:
- `STAGE_LATE`
- `STAGE_REENTRY`

Usually weak in:
- `STAGE_EMERGENT`
- `STAGE_EXHAUSTED`

### Common family confusions
- confused with C-04 when rejection at the edge is actually failed-break reversal rather than orderly rotation
- confused with C-03 when balance is preparing to resolve and the environment is no longer truly rotational
- confused with C-01/C-02 when directional authority already dominates over rotational return logic

### Wrapper-only routing clue
If the structure makes most sense as accepted rotation inside value rather than directional migration, C-05 should remain primary even when directional temptation exists locally.

## MULTI-CANDIDATE PRESERVATION LAW

A wrapper-only GPT must not erase plausible competing families too early.

Common honest multi-candidate cases include:
- C-01 vs C-02 when continuation exists but pullback status is not yet fully resolved
- C-03 vs C-04 when release quality and failure/reclaim hierarchy are both still alive
- C-04 vs C-05 when rejection is visible but it is not yet clear whether the case is trap-unwind or orderly rotation
- C-03 vs C-05 when balance-edge pressure exists but full release legitimacy is still contested

When this happens:
- preserve contested posture
- preserve what would resolve the contest
- do not force one winner just to simplify downstream logic

## FAMILY STAGE AND BUCKET LAW

Stage and bucket context change family legitimacy.

### Directional families
Continuation families often tolerate:
- mature structure
- late but still usable continuation
- re-entry after interruption

### Release families
Release families often demand:
- earlier stage legitimacy
- cleaner confirmation timing
- less tolerance for late chased structure

### Failed-break families
Failed-break families often require:
- more developed evidence before trust becomes high
- later confirmation than early breakout families
- better hierarchy between break attempt and reclaim

### Balance families
Balance families often become more legitimate after the range is already proven,
not less.

### Bucket pressure
Buckets/regimes can:
- shorten usable stage life
- increase fake-break risk
- reduce late-stage continuation honesty
- support rotational families better than directional ones
- make salvage/re-entry more or less legitimate

Wrapper-only use must preserve that family truth is not bucket-blind.

## FAMILY REFUSAL AND DEFER LAW

Return `DEFERRED_CLASSIFICATION` when:
- the candidate set is honest but not resolved
- the family answer would depend on missing surface truth
- stage legitimacy is too unclear to force promotion
- breakout/failure or continuation/reversal hierarchy is still structurally open

Return `NO_VALID_FAMILY` when:
- no organizing engine is currently honest enough
- the structure is too broken or too incomplete for family preservation

Return `INVALID_INPUT` when:
- minimum structure/context identity is too broken for even bounded routing
