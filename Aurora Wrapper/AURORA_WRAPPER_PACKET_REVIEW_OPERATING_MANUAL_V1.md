# AURORA WRAPPER PACKET REVIEW OPERATING MANUAL V1

## PURPOSE

This file deepens the wrapper's packet, example, and review-side knowledge so a GPT using only `Aurora Wrapper/` can reason more faithfully without relying on hidden Blueprint access.

It is not a replacement for:
- `AURORA_WRAPPER_PACKET_EXAMPLE_VAULT.md`
- `AURORA_WRAPPER_EXECUTION_PACK.md`
- `AURORA_WRAPPER_FAMILY_VAULT.md`
- `AURORA_WRAPPER_PATTERN_VAULT.md`

It is a densification layer for wrapper-only use.

Its job is to preserve the packet/review intelligence that often gets over-compressed when source doctrine is compiled too aggressively:
- what packets are really for
- how examples should and should not be used
- how review packets diagnose without rewriting doctrine
- how illustrative and real-case anchors differ
- how packet lanes preserve continuity without forcing premature certainty
- how no-card-valid, degraded, and observe-only endings remain valid outputs

---

## ROOT LAW

Packets and examples do not exist to make Aurora look concrete.
They exist to make Aurora's staged reasoning auditable, reusable, and diagnosable.

A wrapper-only GPT should therefore treat packet and review material as:
- continuity structures
- shape anchors
- diagnosis anchors
- anti-fabrication anchors
- non-retroactive learning structures

not as collections of nice examples to imitate blindly.

---

## WHAT A PACKET IS PROTECTING

The packet layer protects Aurora from five common failures.

### 1. Abstract doctrine with no operational shape
Without packets, doctrine can sound right while remaining too vague to reuse safely.

### 2. Example misuse
Without packet law, one illustrative case can get mistaken for universal doctrine.

### 3. Retroactive doctrine rewriting
Without review law, outcomes can be used to rewrite history instead of diagnosing where the chain actually failed or held.

### 4. Hidden stage collapse
Without packet discipline, sessions drift toward early-entry-only thinking and forget observe-only, continuation, re-entry, salvage, degraded, or no-card-valid endings.

### 5. Symbol-shell drift
Without group-context and family-lane discipline, a single case can get mistaken for the doctrine itself.

---

## THE FOUR PACKET FUNCTIONS

## 1. Workflow packets

### What they are for
Workflow packets define the full staged route a case is allowed to take.

### What they must preserve
- input requirements
- stage order
- object chain
- stop conditions
- output expectations
- operator notes

### What they must not do
- invent family or pattern truth not earned in the chain
- skip downgrade logic
- imply that a card is the default endpoint

### Wrapper-only reminder
A workflow packet is the execution grammar of a case, not just a checklist.

---

## 2. Family-lane packets

### What they are for
Family-lane packets define a reusable operational route inside one family/pattern lane.

### What they must preserve
- family habitat and anti-habitat
- main competitors
- required inputs
- stage-aware checks
- stop/go boundaries
- lane-specific caution points

### What they must not do
- replace the family registry
- replace the pattern atlas
- imply that one lane is universal doctrine

### Wrapper-only reminder
A lane packet is a reusable route, not a declaration that all future cases should look the same.

---

## 3. Worked-example packets

### What they are for
Worked-example packets show a full staged interpretation chain from ASC intake through downstream judgment.

### What they must preserve
- honest intake quality
- group-context construction
- interpretation steps
- family/pattern narrowing
- opportunity posture
- deployability and geometry gating
- whether the case did or did not reach card-valid status

### What they must not do
- imply that a worked example proves live deployability in general
- hide ambiguity that survived the chain honestly
- rewrite missingness into certainty for the sake of neatness

### Wrapper-only reminder
A worked example proves that the chain can be carried honestly.
It does not prove that the same lane always deserves a card.

---

## 4. Review packets

### What they are for
Review packets diagnose what happened **after** the original thesis and packet existed.

### What they must preserve
- target reference
- original state summary
- actual outcome summary
- review classification
- learned changes
- stability notes

### What they must not do
- mutate the original packet retroactively
- convert one outcome into sweeping doctrine rewrite
- erase what was genuinely correct in earlier layers

### Wrapper-only reminder
A review packet is a diagnosis object.
It is not a backdoor to pretend the original state knew more than it did.

---

## EXAMPLE-TYPE DISCIPLINE

A wrapper-only GPT should preserve three example classes.

### Illustrative examples
Purpose:
- prove routing
- prove object shape
- prove honesty posture

Limit:
- do not create live certainty by themselves

### Real-intake examples
Purpose:
- prove bridge behavior with real ASC-derived input
- prove missingness handling
- prove stop-before-invention behavior

Limit:
- do not authorize invention beyond the actual intake quality

### Review examples
Purpose:
- prove diagnosis discipline
- prove non-retroactive learning

Limit:
- do not authorize outcome-based mythology

---

## GROUP-CONTEXT DISCIPLINE

The wrapper should preserve why group-context examples matter.

Group context exists so Aurora does not reason from:
- one ticker shell
- one local pattern snapshot
- one-symbol storytelling

A proper group-context packet/object should preserve:
- identity
- membership
- bucket posture
- regime posture
- routing posture
- boundary posture

Wrapper-only reminder:
If group context collapses, family reasoning becomes too local and doctrine drifts toward symbol-specific anecdotes.

---

## NO-CARD-VALID HONESTY

The wrapper must explicitly preserve that packets and examples may end in:
- `OBSERVE_ONLY`
- watch-only
- `ELIGIBLE_DEGRADED`
- `EXECUTION_INVALID`
- `STRUCTURE_INVALID`
- `GEOMETRY_INVALID`
- `GEOMETRY_UNRESOLVED`
- stage exhaustion
- `CARD_BLOCKED_*`
- no-card-valid outcomes

This is not a weakness in the packet layer.
It is proof that the packet layer is honest.

---

## REVIEW DIAGNOSIS MAP

A wrapper-only GPT should use review packets to ask:

### Was the structure wrong?
- family read bad?
- pattern read bad?
- anti-confusion boundary ignored?

### Was the stage wrong?
- too early?
- too late?
- exhausted but treated as live?
- mid-progress or continuation legitimacy misunderstood?

### Was deployability wrong?
- execution burden ignored?
- friction/continuity burden underestimated?
- case structurally valid but practically unusable?

### Was geometry wrong?
- entry law too weak?
- invalidation dishonest?
- timebox unrealistic?
- explicit field set not truly earned?

### Was upstream truth too thin?
- did missing ASC truth block honest downstream continuation?
- was bridge weakness hidden instead of preserved?

Review should isolate the failing layer rather than flattening everything into “bad trade” or “good trade.”

---

## FAMILY-LANE AND PATTERN-LANE HUMILITY

A lane packet can be very useful without becoming universal doctrine.

The wrapper should preserve:
- one lane proving a reusable route does not mean that lane dominates the whole family
- a strong C-04 / G4 anchor does not make Aurora a failed-break-only system
- future lanes should widen the packet library without retroactively overstating current lane breadth

This protects the wrapper from overgeneralizing from a few well-developed anchors.

---

## WRAPPER-ONLY REFUSAL GUIDE

A wrapper-only GPT should refuse or downgrade when packet/example use would otherwise:
- turn an illustrative anchor into production certainty
- turn a real-intake excerpt into permission to invent missing downstream truth
- treat a review outcome as retroactive proof of original certainty
- treat a lane packet as universal doctrine
- erase observe-only or no-card-valid endings because they look less exciting

The correct fallback is to:
- preserve the example class honestly
- preserve missingness honestly
- preserve degraded or blocked status honestly
- keep doctrine separate from one specimen case
