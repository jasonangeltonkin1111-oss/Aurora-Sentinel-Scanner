============================================================
1. SYSTEM IDENTITY
============================================================

SYSTEM NAME
-----------
Aegis Forge Scanner

SHORT NAME
----------
AFS

PLATFORM
--------
MetaTrader 5 (MT5)

SYSTEM TYPE
-----------
Market intelligence scanner and opportunity environment builder

WHAT THE SYSTEM IS
------------------
AFS is a scanner EA that runs on one MT5 terminal and analyzes the broker
universe visible in that terminal.

It builds a structured market-intelligence environment by:

- loading the broker universe
- normalizing symbol identity
- classifying symbols using a user-owned map
- running a light surface scan across the broad universe
- selecting which symbols deserve deeper work
- validating economics sanity
- building ATR, history, activity, and friction context
- forming final market-truth and tradability status
- ranking eligible symbols
- selecting Top 5 symbols per PrimaryBucket
- showing shortlist correlation context on the final selected set
- delivering clean operator-facing output

WHAT THE SYSTEM IS NOT
----------------------
AFS is not:

- a signal bot
- an entry engine
- an auto-trader
- a prediction engine
- a giant dashboard framework
- a full portfolio optimizer in Phase 1

PRIMARY OUTPUT GOAL
-------------------
Top symbols per PrimaryBucket, with enough context to be genuinely useful
during live trading workflow.

SECONDARY OUTPUT GOAL
---------------------
A clean symbol-level scan table showing:

- identity
- classification
- economics trust
- volatility context
- friction/activity context
- tradability truth
- ranking
- final-set correlation context

============================================================
2. TRADING MISSION ALIGNMENT
============================================================

REAL USE CASE
-------------
AFS supports:

- scalp trading
- intraday trading

TARGET HOLD WINDOW
------------------
- minimum intended hold: 20 minutes
- maximum intended hold: 8 hours

PRACTICAL TRADING ENVIRONMENT
-----------------------------
- around 10 trades per day
- low correlation between trades
- around 0.1% risk per trade

IMPORTANT RULE
--------------
AFS does not generate buy/sell signals.
AFS does not place trades.
AFS does not decide entries.

AFS only improves the trade-catching environment by helping identify:

- usable symbols
- active symbols
- relevant symbols
- early opportunity context
- low-quality symbols to ignore
- low-uniqueness symbols to avoid over-focusing on

3. HARD-LOCKED DECISIONS
============================================================

DECISION 1
----------
One terminal = one firm / one broker-server context.

DECISION 2
----------
One EA instance scans only the universe visible in its terminal.

DECISION 3
----------
Multiple terminals may write to one shared Common Files root.

DECISION 4
----------
Output partitioning must use SERVER NAME, not account login.

DECISION 5
----------
Broker sector classification is never trusted as system truth.

DECISION 6
----------
A separate maintained classification and universe mapping source is the truth for:

- canonical symbol
- asset class
- primary bucket
- sector
- industry
- theme labels

DECISION 7
----------
Menu/settings, HUD, mode controls, and testing controls are part of the foundation.
They are not optional polish.

DECISION 8
----------
The menu/settings block must remain at the top of the wrapper.

DECISION 9
----------
The wrapper owns:

- inputs/menu
- lifecycle
- mode
- timer
- scheduling
- rotation
- call order
- HUD
- top-level logging
- export policy

DECISION 10
-----------
Grouped major modules do mechanics only.
They do not own global orchestration or global UI.

DECISION 11
-----------
Phase 1 is not a skeleton.
Phase 1 must already be a complete usable scanner.

DECISION 12
-----------
Trader Mode is invalid until Phase 1 is complete.

DECISION 13
-----------
Growth must be additive:
new features must add capability without forcing architectural repair.

DECISION 14
-----------
ScanRecord is the master row contract.

DECISION 15
-----------
Step 8 market-truth status is the final truth gate before ranking.

No new downstream gate layer may be introduced after Step 8.

DECISION 15A
------------
After Step 8, the remaining pipeline is:

- selection
- finalist correlation
- output / HUD / trader delivery

These downstream stages may score, sort, group, annotate, or present,
but they must not introduce a new independent eligibility gate family.

DECISION 15B
------------
If Step 8 shows WEAK symbols, that is valid runtime truth.

WEAK does not mean excluded.
WEAK means usable but not fully proven.
WEAK symbols may still be ranked lower and may still appear if they remain the
best available names inside their bucket.

DECISION 16
-----------
Correlation is included in Phase 1, but only on the final selected set,
not across the full universe.

DECISION 17
-----------
Final shortlist target is:
Top 5 per PrimaryBucket

DECISION 18
-----------
The system must use a two-depth scan model:
- surface scan on the broad universe
- deeper scan on promoted symbols only

DECISION 19
-----------
Universe processing must use staged rotation and update logic to avoid overloading MT5.

Rotation remains wrapper-owned orchestration behavior and does not become a separate
late-phase gate step.

DECISION 20
-----------
Rotation means update focus moves across symbol sets over time.
Rotation does NOT mean dropping prior symbols from memory or forgetting previous state.

DECISION 21
-----------
Each major stage may track its own freshness/update timestamp.
Scheduler decisions must prefer stage freshness over vague generic recency.

DECISION 22
-----------
Legacy EAs may be mined for broker-handling patterns, field coverage,
validation ideas, and anomaly ideas, but their wrapper, persistence,
and output architecture must not be transplanted into AFS unless the
governing grouped-module design is formally revised.

DECISION 23
-----------
Identity, market state, economics, friction, and selection are separate concerns.
They must remain separated in both stage ownership and code ownership.

DECISION 24
-----------
Surface scan is a lightweight truth-gathering stage, not a final truth engine.

Step 8 is the final tradability-truth stage for Phase 1.

DECISION 25
-----------
Broken broker specs must be identified as broken or suspicious inputs before
they are allowed to masquerade as downstream symbol failure.

DECISION 26
-----------
Reason codes after Step 8 are explanatory labels, not new gate engines.

Reject codes and weak codes exist to explain truth already established by earlier stages.
They must not create a second eligibility wall after Step 8.

============================================================
4. CORE ARCHITECTURAL PHILOSOPHY
============================================================

RULE 1
------
Runtime is memory-first.
Most working data stays in memory, not in many always-on files.

RULE 2
------
Files are for:

- permanent mapping data
- final production output
- selective debug artifacts
- validation evidence
- health/anomaly logs

RULE 3
------
Dev Mode must be selective, not noisy.

RULE 4
------
Trader Mode must be clean, readable, and low-noise.

RULE 5
------
Only major responsibility groups get their own modules/files.

RULE 6
------
The architecture must be interlinked through stable records.
Not a monolith. Not a fragmented pile.

RULE 7
------
Every phase must have:
- clear steps
- clear build target
- clear tests
- clear success criteria

RULE 8
------
Broker reality is hostile until proven otherwise.

RULE 9
------
Asset classes behave differently and must be handled through internal behavior policies.

RULE 10
-------
Future growth must mean adding capability, not fixing structure.

RULE 11
-------
Selection must never compensate for upstream truth failures.

Selection also must not re-litigate upstream truth through a second hidden gate system.

RULE 12
-------
The scanner must prefer coarse-but-honest truth over fake precision.

This applies especially to Step 8:
- PASS = tradable enough now
- WEAK = not fully proven yet, but still usable/promotable
- FAIL = not tradable now

RULE 13
-------
Timing and scan cadence must be test-tuned live.
Do not hard-code premature timing assumptions into the architecture.

RULE 14
-------
Unknown or incomplete upstream information must not be silently treated as good,
but it also must not be prematurely treated as final failure when the stage is
supposed to remain cautious.

This rule is hard-locked for Step 8:
- stale does not automatically mean fail
- quiet does not automatically mean fail
- rebuilding evidence does not automatically mean fail
- active tick truth must override misleading coarse tags where appropriate

RULE 15
-------
Stage-level observability is part of the architecture.
A stage is not considered healthy merely because the timer runs; its own freshness,
coverage, and update state must be inspectable.

RULE 16
-------
Persistence must be intentional.
A new cycle is not a justification for discarding healthy existing state.

RULE 17
-------
From Step 8 onward, the scanner must become lighter, not more gate-heavy.

Step 9, Step 10, and Step 11 must not add a new rejection architecture.

5. OPERATING MODES

AFS has two operator-facing modes:

MODE A: DEV MODE
----------------

- build the next phase
- hard test a function
- inspect a selected scope
- validate calculations
- inspect anomalies
- validate broker behavior
- tune rules by asset class or bucket

DEV MODE BEHAVIOR
-----------------
- selective scope controls
- selective pipeline controls
- selective exports
- richer HUD
- validation visibility
- anomaly visibility
- debug summaries
- review debt visibility where useful

MODE B: TRADER MODE
-------------------
Purpose:
- use the scanner during actual trading workflow
- receive clean final shortlist context
- avoid debug clutter

TRADER MODE BEHAVIOR
--------------------
- clean HUD
- minimal logs
- compact final shortlist context
- compact context only
- degraded-but-honest provisional markers when some downstream context is pending

IMPORTANT RULE
--------------
Mode must not be controlled internally by two ambiguous booleans.

Use one effective internal mode state:

- MODE_DEV
- MODE_TRADER

============================================================
6. MODE LEGALITY RULES
============================================================

MODE LEGALITY
-------------
- MODE_DEV is always allowed.
- MODE_TRADER is only allowed after Phase 1 completion.

If the user tries to enable Trader Mode before Phase 1 completion:
- the EA must refuse cleanly
or
- degrade safely to Dev Mode with a clear message

NO AMBIGUITY RULE
-----------------
The system must never allow:
- both modes active
- neither mode active without fallback
- mixed dev exports while claiming Trader Mode

DEGRADED HONESTY RULE
---------------------
If a downstream stage is incomplete, late, or pending,
the system should degrade honestly rather than hide output or fake certainty.

Examples:
- correlation unavailable -> CORR_PENDING
- friction thin -> FRICTION_THIN
- history incomplete -> HISTORY_THIN

TRADER MODE FILE BEHAVIOR

Keep output minimal, clean, and operator-facing.

RUNTIME SERVER FOLDERS
----------------------
Per-server runtime folders remain valid and unchanged for:
- debug
- dev
- logs
- runtime
- trader
- warm-state files
- other internal server-specific artifacts

These folders are runtime/internal partitions and must continue to use server name.

FINAL OPERATOR OUTPUT RULE
--------------------------
Final operator-facing output must also be written to a shared root-level folder:

- AFS/FINAL_OUTPUT/

This folder contains one text file per server:

- AFS/FINAL_OUTPUT/<ServerName>.txt

Examples:
- AFS/FINAL_OUTPUT/Upcomers-Server.txt
- AFS/FINAL_OUTPUT/TopOneTrader-MT5.txt

FINAL OUTPUT CONTENT RULE
-------------------------
The final output text file must contain the final selected symbols for that server
with all materially useful symbol details needed by the operator.

It must be human-readable and compact, not raw debug spam.

FINAL OUTPUT FORMAT RULE
------------------------
The final output file must be a text file, not the primary production CSV.

CSV or additional review exports may still exist in Dev Mode where useful,
but the core final operator-facing output is the per-server TXT file.

OVERWRITE RULE
--------------
The final per-server text output should be rewritten on refresh so it reflects
current scanner truth, not appended indefinitely.

RUNTIME RESET RULE
------------------
A full intelligence cycle is not the same as a full memory reset.

Fields should reset only when correctness requires it.

In particular:
- structural identity and classification data should survive normal cycles
- stage-runtime fields may reset on startup, manual refresh, server change,
  or clear invalidation conditions
- stages must not erase healthy prior state on every timer tick

SURFACE STATE PERSISTENCE RULE
------------------------------
Surface runtime fields must remain memory-first and survive ordinary timer cycles.

Surface update counts, last-seen timestamps, freshness clues, and promotion context
must accumulate across scan cycles unless correctness requires invalidation.

16. RAW / DERIVED / VALIDATED DISTINCTION
============================================================

This distinction must remain visible at all times.

RAW
---
Direct broker fields from MT5.

DERIVED
-------
Values calculated by AFS.

VALIDATED
---------
Values or behavior checked via:
- OrderCalcProfit
- OrderCalcMargin
- other explicit MT5 verification paths if used later

RULE
----
Never hide the distinction.
If they disagree, log the disagreement instead of collapsing it into a fake final truth.

WHY
---
Because live broker hardening depends on seeing disagreements clearly.

STEP 6 VALIDATION CHAIN
-----------------------
Step 6 must treat the sequence as:

1. spec integrity check
2. economics derivation
3. economics validation
4. trust grading

Broken broker inputs must be identified first as bad inputs,
not silently collapsed into bad-symbol conclusions.

============================================================
17. TRUST / STATUS MODEL
============================================================

Phase 1 must stay coarse-but-honest.

Do not create false precision.

PHASE 1 TRUST / STATUS GRADES
-----------------------------
Use coarse states like:
- PASS
- WEAK
- FAIL

Use these for:
- economics trust
- history sufficiency
- friction usability
- final tradability truth through Step 8

FINAL STEP-8 STATUS INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------
Step 8 must answer:

"Is this symbol tradable enough right now, based on compact and honest evidence?"

Interpret states as:

- PASS = tradable enough now, with enough recent evidence
- WEAK = not fully proven yet, but still usable, rankable, and promotable
- FAIL = not tradable now

IMPORTANT HARD-LOCK
-------------------
Step 8 FAIL means not tradable now.
It does not mean permanent exile.
It does not mean never reconsider.
It does not mean remove from future passes.

Step 8 WEAK means current truth is incomplete, thin, cautious, or borderline.
It must not be silently converted into rejection by later stages.

MISSING INFORMATION RULE
------------------------
Missing information must not silently become weak or pass.

Separate:
- missing
- weak
- fail

SPEC INTEGRITY GRADES
---------------------

- SPEC_OK
- SPEC_SUSPICIOUS
- SPEC_BROKEN

NORMALIZATION STATUS VALUES
---------------------------
- NORMALIZATION_OK
- NORMALIZATION_PARTIAL
- NORMALIZATION_FAIL
- NORMALIZATION_NOT_REQUIRED

PRACTICALITY STATUS VALUES
--------------------------
- PRACTICAL

- IMPRACTICAL

18. FINAL TRUTH-THEN-RANK RULE
============================================================

RANKING NEVER SEES STEP-8 FAIL SYMBOLS
--------------------------------------
That rule remains.

HOWEVER
-------
No new independent eligibility engine step is introduced after Step 8.

The pipeline after Step 8 is:

- ranking
- shortlist selection
- finalist correlation
- output

These stages must not create a second gate family.

ROLE OF REJECT CODES
--------------------
Reject codes exist to explain why a symbol is not currently usable.

They are explanatory truth labels.
They are not a separate downstream gate engine.

ROLE OF WEAK CODES
------------------
Weak codes exist to explain why a symbol is usable but not fully trusted.

They are explanatory truth labels.
They are not a second-stage hidden filter.

STEP-8-TO-SELECTION RULE
------------------------
The selection stage may:
- score
- sort
- bucket-rank
- down-rank weak names
- choose top names per bucket

The selection stage must not:
- reclassify broad market truth through a new eligibility wall
- invent a second rejection regime after Step 8
- convert ordinary weak evidence into silent exclusion

CORE RULE
---------
From Step 8 onward:
- PASS may rank normally
- WEAK may rank lower
- FAIL does not rank

That is the only downstream truth constraint.

19. TWO-LAYER SCAN MODEL
============================================================

This is a hard-locked runtime principle.

LAYER 1: SURFACE SCAN
---------------------
Purpose:
touch the broad universe lightly and cheaply.

Surface scan should focus on fields that can be gathered or refreshed
without heavy MT5 cost.

Typical surface-scan concerns:
- symbol visibility / availability
- classification state
- basic tradability visibility
- lightweight quote presence
- raw spread snapshot
- session high / low
- bid high / low
- ask high / low
- session open / close
- tick timestamp
- basic tick age / quote freshness clue
- daily change percent
- basic surface flags
- cheap promotion clues

Typical MT5 surface sources include:
- SYMBOL_BID
- SYMBOL_ASK
- SYMBOL_SESSION_HIGH
- SYMBOL_SESSION_LOW
- SYMBOL_SESSION_OPEN
- SYMBOL_SESSION_CLOSE
- SYMBOL_BIDHIGH
- SYMBOL_BIDLOW
- SYMBOL_ASKHIGH
- SYMBOL_ASKLOW
- SYMBOL_TIME

Typical lightweight derived surface fields include:
- spread snapshot
- tick age
- daily change percent
- QuoteState
- SessionState

LAYER 2: DEEP SCAN
------------------
Purpose:
apply heavier calculations only to symbols that matter.

Deep scan should focus on:
- economics sanity
- validation behavior
- history sufficiency
- ATR and baseline movement
- friction samples
- liveliness
- tradability truth
- ranking
- final selection context

RULE
----
Do not run full heavy calculations across all 1200 symbols every cycle.

============================================================
20. PROMOTION LOGIC
============================================================

The system must decide which symbols graduate from surface scan to deep scan.

PROMOTION PURPOSE
-----------------
Avoid wasting MT5 resources on low-value symbols while still avoiding
wrong hard gates.

PROMOTION RULE
--------------
Promotion must be cautious.
The system must avoid prematurely excluding symbols because of weak surface data.

SURFACE PROMOTION SHOULD PREFER
-------------------------------
- symbols with live enough quotes
- symbols with acceptable early spread clues
- symbols already known as important or active
- symbols recently ranked well
- symbols recently in top selections
- symbols in user-selected scope
- symbols that surface anomalies suggest deserve inspection
- symbols lacking confidence and needing verification

ANTI-FALSE-GATE RULE
--------------------
Surface scan must not act like a final truth engine.

Surface scan may:
- defer
- promote for verification
- mark uncertain

Surface scan must not aggressively kill potentially good symbols
based on shallow data alone.

NEVER BLOCK BECAUSE UNKNOWN RULE
--------------------------------
If surface information is missing, stale, thin, or uncertain,
the symbol should default to cautious non-final handling such as:

- DEFERRED
- PROMOTED_FOR_VERIFICATION
- SURFACE_CANDIDATE

and not immediate hard rejection unless a separate stage has the right
to make that stronger decision.

LOCKED PROMOTION STATE IDEA
---------------------------
Promotion logic should use stable compact states rather than scattered booleans.

Recommended coarse states:
- NOT_REVIEWED
- SURFACE_COLD
- SURFACE_CANDIDATE
- PROMOTED
- DEFERRED
- BLOCKED

============================================================
21. ROTATION / UPDATE MODEL
============================================================

AFS must use staged rotational updating to avoid overloading MT5.

ROTATION PURPOSE
----------------
- distribute load across time
- keep broad universe coverage alive
- keep top candidates refreshed more often
- avoid freezing on only one subset
- avoid rescanning the full heavy universe every cycle

ROTATION OWNERSHIP RULE
-----------------------
Rotation is wrapper-owned runtime orchestration.

It is not a standalone late-phase selection gate.
It is not a separate post-ranking architecture step.

MEMORY RULE
-----------
Previously scanned symbols remain in memory with their latest known state,
timestamps, and confidence context.

REFRESH RULE
------------
More important symbols should refresh more often.

Examples:
- recent top selections
- recent deep-scan survivors
- recently promoted symbols
- symbols with degrading freshness
- symbols with unresolved confidence
- symbols currently weak and still hydrating

LOWER-PRIORITY RULE
-------------------
Less important symbols may refresh more slowly,
but they must still remain part of the global scan rotation.

SURFACE RUNTIME RESET RULE
--------------------------
Surface scan fields must be reset only when:

- EA startup occurs
- manual refresh is triggered
- server context changes
- correctness clearly requires invalidation

Surface fields must NOT reset during normal timer cycles.

Surface runtime state must remain memory-first
and accumulate update counts and timestamps across scan cycles.

SHORTLIST STICKINESS RULE
-------------------------
Recent shortlist membership may influence refresh priority,
but must not directly fake score quality.

Good use:
- refresh priority
- verification priority
- revisit priority

Bad use:
- automatic ranking bonus
- hidden score inflation

============================================================
22. SCHEDULING PHILOSOPHY
============================================================

The scheduling model must be adaptive and test-tuned.

DO NOT HARD-LOCK PREMATURE CADENCE
----------------------------------
Do not assume in the architecture that:
- 3 seconds is always right
- 50-symbol batches are always right
- one rotation strategy fits all brokers/universes

INSTEAD
-------
The blueprint hard-locks the scheduling philosophy, not the exact timing numbers.

SCHEDULING MUST SUPPORT
-----------------------
- surface batches
- deep batches
- rotation cursors
- priority refresh
- adaptive timing tests
- broker/load-aware tuning
- stage freshness awareness
- different refresh cadence by stage

EXAMPLE CONCEPT ONLY
--------------------
Possible practical behavior:
- surface scan first 50 symbols
- after a few seconds, next 50
- continue until full universe has fresh surface state
- then continue cycling
- meanwhile deep scan promoted candidates in smaller heavier batches
- refresh top selected symbols more often than cold symbols

This is an example concept, not a mandatory fixed timing rule.

SPEC READ COOLDOWN RULE
-----------------------
Specification and economics data should usually refresh less often than surface data.

Spec/economics refresh should accelerate only when:
- symbol is newly promoted
- spec integrity is suspicious
- symbol is in active shortlist
- broker/server changed
- validation disagreement exists
- operator is explicitly testing economics

============================================================
23. CYCLE MODEL
============================================================

The scanner runs as a repeating intelligence cycle.

FIXED EXECUTION ORDER
---------------------
The practical pipeline order is:

1. universe load
2. classification
3. surface scan
4. promotion update
5. market/spec/economics sanity
6. history / ATR
7. Step 8 market truth and tradability
8. selection and shortlist
9. finalist correlation
10. output / HUD

IMPORTANT
---------
Step 8 is the final truth stage before selection.

No new independent eligibility gate stage exists after Step 8.

HIGH-LEVEL CYCLE
----------------
1. refresh surface batch
2. update promotion candidates
3. deep-scan selected batch
4. update spec/history/friction truth for affected symbols
5. refresh Step 8 tradability truth
6. rebuild ranking and Top 5 per PrimaryBucket where needed
7. recompute final-set correlation where needed
8. refresh HUD/output
9. advance rotation state
10. continue until the full universe has been covered
11. start the cycle again without forgetting prior state

IMPORTANT
---------
"Start the cycle again" does NOT mean reset all knowledge to zero.
It means begin a new full coverage pass while keeping current memory state
and updating it intelligently.

not on 1200 symbols.

PURPOSE
