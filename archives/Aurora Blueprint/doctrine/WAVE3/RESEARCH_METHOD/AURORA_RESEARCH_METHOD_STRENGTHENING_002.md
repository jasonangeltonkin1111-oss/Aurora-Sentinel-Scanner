# 2. RESEARCH-METHOD STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Testing should respect family class differences

### Strengthening statement
Research should not test mean reversion, momentum, and statistical arbitrage as if they were interchangeable forms of the same signal logic.

### Why this is justified
Chan’s strategy organization and Pole’s stat-arb framing both preserve clear differences in how families are constructed and evaluated.

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s research method should preserve distinct test logic for:
- trend/momentum families
- mean-reversion families
- stat-arb / relative-value families

### What this changes
This strengthens family-specific evidence thinking and later quant-translation design.

---

## 2.2 Model calibration risk must be treated as first-class evidence risk

### Strengthening statement
Research should treat calibration fragility and model-choice sensitivity as major risks in systematic relative-value and reversion work.

### Why this is justified
Pole’s framing emphasizes modeling, reversion assumptions, and multiple interacting components rather than naive plug-and-play signals.

### Canonical implication
Research should ask:
- how sensitive is the edge to parameter changes?
- what assumptions about reversion are load-bearing?
- does performance depend on fragile spread definitions or pair selection rules?

### What this changes
This makes the research layer less naive about model-driven edge claims.

---

## 2.3 Implementation realism belongs inside research, not after it

### Strengthening statement
Research should not treat execution and implementation as cleanup work after a strategy is already “proven.”

### Why this is justified
Chan places backtesting, automated execution, and practical implementation near the center of real strategy development.
Pole’s work also implies that impact, event risk, and portfolio interaction matter to systematic reversion logic.

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s research method should include explicit prompts for:
- execution assumptions
- automation assumptions
- event-risk vulnerability
- market-impact and liquidity realism

### What this changes
This pulls research closer to actual deployability instead of abstract signal scoring.

---

## 2.4 Simplicity should outrank decorative complexity

### Strengthening statement
Research should prefer simple interpretable systems until additional complexity proves it creates real discrimination.

### Why this is justified
Chan’s practical algorithmic framing strongly favors robust implementable logic over ornamental complexity.

### Canonical implication
Aurora research should default toward:
- simpler transparent hypotheses first
- complexity only when it adds measurable leverage
- explicit anti-overfitting caution around feature or rule proliferation

### What this changes
This strengthens the anti-overfitting stance already present in the research backbone.

---

## 2.5 Relative-value families require cross-asset and portfolio awareness

### Strengthening statement
Research for stat-arb and relative-value families must preserve portfolio interaction and cross-asset dependence rather than pretending each pair is a standalone trade.

### Why this is justified
Pole’s framing points toward multiple bets, selection interactions, and broader portfolio/risk-control logic.

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s future stat-arb research method should preserve:
- group/pair selection discipline
- exposure interaction logic
- portfolio-level risk realism

### What this changes
This prevents later stat-arb family work from being reduced to simplistic pair anecdotes.

---

## 2.6 Reflexive and non-equilibrium context should challenge naive test transfer

### Strengthening statement
Research should remain open to the possibility that historical tests can degrade when participant beliefs and environments change reflexively.

### Why this is justified
Soros’s anti-equilibrium and reflexive framing implies that strategy validity may not transfer cleanly across changing historical contexts.

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s research layer should preserve a warning that:
- historical tests are not neutral timeless truths
- reflexive environment shifts can alter family behavior and validity

### What this changes
This adds a high-value caution against over-trusting static historical fit.
