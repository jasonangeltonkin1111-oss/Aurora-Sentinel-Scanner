# 2. FAMILY-LAYER STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Family interpretation should allow reflexive feedback loops

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should preserve the possibility that some market structures are partly shaped and reinforced by participant beliefs, not only by independent external reality.

### Why this is justified
Soros explicitly frames markets through reflexivity, participant bias, and anti-equilibrium thinking rather than static equilibrium assumptions.

### Canonical implication
Family interpretation should remain open to the possibility that:
- trend persistence can be amplified by belief loops
- breaks and transitions can be self-reinforcing
- apparent structure can degrade quickly when the belief loop turns

### What this changes
This deepens:
- trend maturity / exhaustion logic
- Breakout / Compression Release caution
- Failed Break / Trap Reversal interpretation when belief-supported moves unwind

---

## 2.2 Non-equilibrium thinking strengthens transition-state realism

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should avoid treating all market states as clean equilibrium categories with stable boundaries.

### Why this is justified
The Alchemy of Finance treats markets as historically evolving and prone to reflexive, unstable transitions rather than timeless balance.

### Canonical implication
Transition and instability logic should remain central where:
- structure is changing faster than classification confidence
- participant belief is amplifying moves
- ecological fit is deteriorating

### What this changes
This strengthens the broader doctrine around unstable states, late-stage continuation caution, and family competition during transitions.

---

## 2.3 Statistical arbitrage should remain a preserved primary family branch

### Strengthening statement
Statistical arbitrage / relative-value logic should remain a real Aurora family branch, not a minor side note.

### Why this is justified
Pole treats statistical arbitrage as a substantial algorithmic domain with reversion models, selection logic, and risk-control realism.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should continue preserving a non-chart-first family branch for:
- spread / reversion logic
- pair or group selection logic
- relative-value distortion logic
- cross-asset or relational family expansion later

### What this changes
This strengthens the existing preserved-family architecture and justifies future dedicated stat-arb family cards.

---

## 2.4 Mean reversion, momentum, and stat-arb must remain distinct machine families

### Strengthening statement
Aurora’s machine-side family architecture should preserve separate systematic families for:
- momentum
- mean reversion
- statistical arbitrage / relative value

### Why this is justified
Chan’s algorithmic family treatment and Pole’s stat-arb framing both preserve meaningful strategy-class separation.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should not flatten machine-side family logic into one generic algorithmic signal family.

### What this changes
This strengthens:
- the quant/machine lane
- family-card future quant-translation fields
- later family evidence work

---

## 2.5 Event risk and market-impact realism must accompany stat-arb family preservation

### Strengthening statement
Relative-value and stat-arb family logic should not be preserved as frictionless model abstractions.

### Why this is justified
Pole’s framing points toward event-risk, market-impact, and risk-control realism as central rather than optional.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should later preserve stat-arb family warnings around:
- event risk
- market impact
- portfolio interaction
- calibration fragility

### What this changes
This protects a future stat-arb branch from being imported naively.
