# 2. STRATEGY FAMILY STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Habitat-first family rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora strategy families should be built first from market habitats, not from isolated named patterns.

### Why this is justified
Grimes structures market action around trends, trading ranges, and the interfaces between them, while Murphy’s classical TA framing reinforces trend/range distinction.

### Canonical implication
The registry should group families under habitats such as:
- trend
- range
- transition

### What this changes
This prevents Aurora from becoming a flat catalog of setups.

---

## 2.2 Trend continuation family

### Strengthening statement
Trend continuation is a core family and should include continuation entries, pullbacks, and base-break continuation structures within a directional habitat.

### Why this is justified
Grimes devotes substantial structure to trends, pullbacks, and trend-continuation templates. Murphy provides broad trend doctrine support.

### Family notes
Context requirements:
- directional habitat already exists
- pullback or base context should respect state structure

Main anti-error:
- do not treat every strong bar as continuation quality

---

## 2.3 Pullback continuation family

### Strengthening statement
Pullbacks are important enough to stand as their own family-level branch rather than being treated as a minor subcase of continuation.

### Why this is justified
Grimes calls the pullback the quintessential trend trading pattern and develops multiple pullback templates.

### Family notes
Context requirements:
- existing directional state
- retracement remains structurally healthy
- not full structural failure

Main anti-error:
- do not confuse pullback entry logic with blind dip buying

---

## 2.4 Range rotation family

### Strengthening statement
Range rotation should remain a core family because ranges are functional structures, not merely periods of inactivity.

### Why this is justified
Grimes explicitly treats trading ranges as functional structures. Murphy’s support/resistance framing also supports bounded rotational trade.

### Family notes
Context requirements:
- balance / range habitat
- location quality matters strongly
- middle-of-range opportunity should often be discounted

Main anti-error:
- do not overtrade the center of accepted trade

---

## 2.5 Breakout / release family

### Strengthening statement
Breakout / release is a distinct family anchored in range-to-trend transition, not just “price went through a line.”

### Why this is justified
Grimes explicitly frames breakout trade as trading range to trend, and Bulkowski preserves a broad breakout-like taxonomy across patterns.

### Family notes
Context requirements:
- transition from containment into directional expansion
- trust must be filtered through Wave 1 state/surface doctrine

Main anti-error:
- do not let mere penetration of structure count as breakout quality

---

## 2.6 Failed breakout / trap reversal family

### Strengthening statement
Failed breakout / trap reversal should be a first-class family, not just an error case.

### Why this is justified
Grimes treats failure tests and failed breakouts explicitly. Bulkowski also preserves failure as a major pattern dimension.

### Family notes
Context requirements:
- apparent breakout attempt exists
- breakout-quality trust collapses
- reversal / return logic becomes structurally dominant

Main anti-error:
- do not delay recognition of failure after trust has already broken

---

## 2.7 Trend reversal / transition family

### Strengthening statement
Trend reversal belongs as a transition family, not as a generic “opposite trend” label.

### Why this is justified
Grimes devotes specific space to trend-to-opposite-trend and failure-of-reversal interfaces.

### Family notes
Context requirements:
- prior trend habitat exists
- structural deterioration of the old trend is visible
- transition quality matters more than one-bar reversal drama

Main anti-error:
- do not force reversal family logic before the transition is structurally credible

---

## 2.8 Support-resistance reaction family

### Strengthening statement
Support-resistance reactions can form a family, but must remain context-bound rather than becoming a catch-all doctrine.

### Why this is justified
Grimes uses support/resistance in practical templates and within range/trend logic; Murphy provides the classical vocabulary.

### Family notes
Context requirements:
- meaningful location
- appropriate habitat
- trust from Wave 1 state/surface logic

Main anti-error:
- do not let “at support” or “at resistance” replace actual family logic

# 3. FAILURE AS FAMILY LOGIC

## 3.1 Failure is not secondary

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should preserve failure-derived families because they are often more discriminating than idealized textbook successes.

### Why this is justified
Grimes and Bulkowski both treat failures seriously rather than as footnotes.

### Canonical implication
The registry should explicitly preserve families built around:
- failed breakouts
- failed reversals
- failed continuation
