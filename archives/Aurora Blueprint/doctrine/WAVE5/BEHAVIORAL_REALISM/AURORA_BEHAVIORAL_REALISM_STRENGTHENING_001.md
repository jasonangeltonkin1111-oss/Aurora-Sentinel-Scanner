# 2. BEHAVIORAL-REALISM STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Two-systems rule

### Strengthening statement
Human judgment should be modeled as the interaction of fast, automatic processing and slower, effortful control rather than as one seamless rational engine.

### Why this is justified
Thinking, Fast and Slow explicitly organizes judgment around System 1 and System 2. fileciteturn101file2

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve a structured language for:
- fast intuition
- slow checking
- where fast intuition helps
- where it misleads

---

## 2.2 Bias-pattern rule

### Strengthening statement
Recurring decision errors should be treated as patterned bias families rather than as isolated mistakes.

### Why this is justified
Thinking, Fast and Slow catalogs recurring biases such as availability, anchoring, framing, law of small numbers, and illusion of validity. fileciteturn101file2

### Canonical implication
Behavioral realism should preserve named bias classes that can later be referenced in review and adaptation prompts.

---

## 2.3 Anti-Homo-Economicus rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should not model the operator or the market participant as a perfectly rational optimizer.

### Why this is justified
Misbehaving explicitly critiques the Econ / Homo Economicus model and documents predictable deviations in real human behavior. fileciteturn101file3

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s doctrine should begin from realistic human behavior, not from idealized rational fiction.

---

## 2.4 Mental-accounting rule

### Strengthening statement
People do not treat money, gains, and losses as one neutral pool; they segment and label them in ways that distort choices.

### Why this is justified
Misbehaving emphasizes mental accounting, transaction utility, sunk costs, house money, and break-even effects. fileciteturn101file3

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve behavioral warnings around:
- sunk-cost attachment
- house-money looseness
- break-even risk escalation
- transaction-utility seduction

---

## 2.5 Physiology-of-risk rule

### Strengthening statement
Biology is part of decision quality under risk and pressure, not a background detail.

### Why this is justified
The Hour Between Dog and Wolf explicitly links body chemistry, stress, confidence, and risk appetite. fileciteturn101file0

### Canonical implication
Behavioral realism should include:
- stress-state degradation
- overconfidence loops after success
- fear / cortisol contraction after losses
- risk-appetite instability under pressure

---

## 2.6 Ecology-sensitive bias rule

### Strengthening statement
Some behaviors and biases should be interpreted relative to environment and adaptation pressure rather than only as static irrationality.

### Why this is justified
Adaptive Markets reframes behavior through competition, adaptation, and changing ecological fit. fileciteturn101file1

### Canonical implication
Behavioral realism should leave room for:
- heuristics that are adaptive in one environment and poor in another
- environment-specific performance of human judgment
