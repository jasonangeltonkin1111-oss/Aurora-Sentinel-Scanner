# 2. STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS TO EXECUTION-CONTEXT DOCTRINE

## 2.1 Feasible Opportunity Rule

### Strengthening statement
Not every visible market opportunity corresponds to a feasible or implementable opportunity under the governing institutional rules.

### Why this is justified
The guided tour emphasizes implementability constraints such as incentive compatibility and individual rationality.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should continue strengthening the distinction between:
- visible opportunity
- feasible opportunity
- deployable opportunity

---

## 2.2 Participation-Sensitive Deployability

### Strengthening statement
Deployability depends partly on who is entering, under what incentives, and under what participation structure.

### Why this is justified
The paper treats entry as endogenous and outcome-relevant.

### Canonical implication
Weak or distorted participation conditions should lower trust in visible opportunity even when structure looks attractive.

---

## 2.3 Information-Asymmetry Trust Caution

### Strengthening statement
A move may look strong while still being lower-trust if the environment is shaped by correlated information or asymmetric informational advantage.

### Why this is justified
The guided tour explicitly highlights asymmetry and correlated information as central complications.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should continue to separate:
- visible strength
- trustworthy strength

### What this changes
This strengthens low-trust interpretation for moves that may be shaped by uneven information quality.

---

## 2.4 Institution-Sensitive Deployability

### Strengthening statement
Practical deployability depends partly on the institution and rules that generate interaction, not only on the chart event itself.

### Why this is justified
The paper treats rules of trade as central and extends auction theory toward market institutions.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should remain institution-sensitive and should resist chart-shape-only evaluation.

# 3. SURFACE-STATE STRENGTHENING

## 3.1 Discovery-with-Trust Surface

### Strengthening
This surface should now require stronger confidence not only in directional structure, but also in the feasibility and information-quality environment supporting that structure.

### Upgraded rule
Visible directional movement is not enough; the surrounding institution and information conditions must not sharply reduce trust.

---

## 3.2 Structurally Valid but Low-Trust Surface

### Strengthening
This state gains stronger support from implementability limits, participation sensitivity, and information asymmetry.

### Upgraded rule
A market can be structurally coherent and still be low-trust because the surrounding institution or participation environment makes the visible opportunity less feasible or less reliable.

---

## 3.3 Nonconviction / Stay-Out Surface

### Strengthening
This surface now gains additional support from the fact that poor deployability may arise from institutional and informational weaknesses even before pure structural confusion is obvious.

### Upgraded rule
Stay-out quality can be justified not only by poor structure, but also by weak feasibility, weak participation, or poor trust conditions.
