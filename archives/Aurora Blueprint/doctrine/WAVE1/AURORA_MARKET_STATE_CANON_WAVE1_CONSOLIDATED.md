# 3. ROOT INTERPRETIVE LAW

Aurora should interpret markets first as **auctions in motion shaped by structured interaction rules**, not as static price labels or pure geometric chart events.

That means:
- price is a result of ongoing competitive interaction
- the market reveals information through its own behavior
- the trading structure that produces observed price behavior matters
- the key question is not only “where is price?” but “how is the auction behaving here, and how trustworthy is that behavior?”

This root law sits under the entire Market State Canon.

# 4. PRIMARY WAVE 1 CANONICAL CONCEPTS

## 4.1 Auction Process

### Canonical definition
The market is an auction process that explores, tests, facilitates, and occasionally rejects trade through price over time.

### Why Aurora needs it
This is the root concept behind:
- balance
- directional discovery
- acceptance
- rejection
- excess
- failed auction
- value migration

### Canon law
Every Aurora market-state label must be interpretable as a condition of the auction process.

---

## 4.2 Market-Generated Information

### Canonical definition
Market-generated information is the present-tense information revealed by the market’s own price, time, and participation behavior as the auction unfolds.

### Aurora use
Aurora should privilege current market-generated information over stale narrative when classifying present-tense state.

### Canon law
External story may support context, but it must not override current structural evidence.

---

## 4.3 Mechanism Sensitivity

### Canonical definition
Observed price behavior is mechanism-sensitive rather than mechanism-neutral.
The structure of the market and the rules of interaction influence what price movement means.

### Aurora use
Aurora should avoid assuming that every visible move emerged from the same strategic environment or carries the same interpretive weight.

### Canon law
Chart geometry is not self-explanatory.
A visible move must be interpreted with awareness that the trading structure producing it matters.

---

## 4.4 Balance

### Canonical definition
Balance is an accepted rotational auction condition where trade is being facilitated around a relatively stable value area and directional discovery is not currently dominant.

### Closely related source language
- balance
- bracket
- rotational trade
- accepted trade around value

### Structural markers
- repeated return through an accepted area
- poor sustained directional escape
- two-sided trade
- weak directional migration

### Canon law
Use “balance” as the broader canonical term.
Use “bracket” when the source situation specifically emphasizes a contained rotational range.

### Strengthened caution
Not every departure from balance deserves equal trust as discovery.
Movement away from balance must be checked for whether it reflects durable migration or only fragile, context-shaped excursion.

---

## 4.5 Directional Discovery / Imbalance

### Canonical definition
Directional discovery is a directional auction condition in which the market is migrating away from prior balance with enough asymmetry, initiative, and directional performance to suggest genuine search for new value rather than mere probing.

### Structural markers
- directional displacement from prior balance
- stronger hold away from prior value
- evidence of migration rather than simple excursion

### Canon law
Not every breakout print is directional discovery.
Discovery requires more than excursion.
It requires signs that the auction is functioning directionally.

### Strengthened caution
Directional discovery should also be read with awareness that:
- price discovery is institutionally shaped
- visible strength is not automatically trustworthy strength
- linked-market or mechanism effects can distort local interpretation

A move is not high-confidence discovery merely because it displaced.
It must survive stronger scrutiny around trust and context-of-formation.

---

## 4.6 Acceptance

### Canonical definition
Acceptance is sustained trade quality at or beyond a reference area sufficient to indicate that price is being facilitated there rather than instantly denied.

### Structural implications
- acceptance is revealed over time
- acceptance increases confidence that migration is real
- acceptance separates genuine discovery from cheap excursion

### Canon law
Do not reduce acceptance to a one-print or one-close event.
Acceptance must reflect sustained structural behavior.

---

## 4.7 Rejection

### Canonical definition
Rejection is the market’s denial of sustained trade at a location after a probe or attempted migration.

### Structural implications
- rejection is the negative image of acceptance
- rejection often precedes return to balance or reversal of the attempted move
- rejection must be judged in context, not confused with ordinary pullback

### Canon law
A retracement is not automatically rejection.
Rejection requires structural failure of facilitation at the tested location.

---

## 4.8 Excess

### Canonical definition
Excess is structural evidence that an auction has run too far relative to available participation and is exhausting rather than gaining durable acceptance.

### Aurora use
Excess is a structural diagnostic concept that supports:
- end-of-auction interpretation
- rejection interpretation
- failed directional continuation interpretation
- transition from trend to balance or instability

### Canon law
Excess is not itself a trade trigger in the canon.
It is evidence about auction condition.

---

## 4.9 Value / Accepted Value Area

### Canonical definition
Value is the area where the market is facilitating enough trade that price is functioning as accepted rather than exceptional.

### Aurora use
- balance occurs around value
- directional discovery is movement away from prior value
- value migration is movement toward a new accepted area

### Canon law
In this module, value is an auction-accepted structural area, not an abstract fundamental “true price.”

---

## 4.10 Value Migration

### Canonical definition
Value migration is the shift of accepted trade away from a prior value area toward a newly accepted area.

### Aurora use
This is one of the clearest structural signals that the market is moving from a balance condition toward a genuine directional discovery condition.

### Canon law
Temporary excursion is not value migration.
Migration requires evidence that acceptance itself is shifting.

---

## 4.11 Initiative Activity

### Canonical definition
Initiative activity is behavior that pushes the auction away from accepted value toward new discovery.

### Aurora use
This concept aligns strongly with:
- directional discovery
- clean break attempts
- continuation authority

---

## 4.12 Responsive Activity

### Canonical definition
Responsive activity is behavior that reacts against excursion and pushes trade back toward accepted value.

### Aurora use
This concept aligns strongly with:
- balance defense
- rejection
- return-to-value logic
- failed directional movement

### Canon law
Aurora must not collapse initiative and responsive activity into one vague “movement” category.
They are structurally different auction behaviors.

---

## 4.13 Attempted Direction

### Canonical definition
Attempted direction is where the auction is trying to go.

## 4.14 Directional Performance

### Canonical definition
Directional performance is how well the market is actually doing in sustaining that directional attempt.

### Aurora use
The distinction between attempted direction and directional performance is critical for separating:
- real directional discovery
- weak breakout attempts
- failed auctions
- unstable transitions

### Canon law
Aurora must always separate directional intention from directional success.

---

## 4.15 Apparent Strength vs Trustworthy Strength

### Canonical definition
Apparent strength is movement that looks directional or authoritative on the chart.
Trustworthy strength is movement that deserves greater confidence after considering acceptance, directional performance, mechanism sensitivity, and information-quality caution.

### Aurora use
This concept sharpens the canon by preventing visible strength from being overrated when the environment may be low-trust.

### Canon law
Aurora should not collapse visible strength into trustworthy strength.

---

## 4.16 Failed Auction / Failed Break

### Canonical definition
A failed auction or failed break is an attempted directional move that cannot gain or maintain acceptance and instead reverts back through the initiating structure.

### Aurora use
This is a first-class structural event.
It is not noise.
It is one of the main transition sources between:
- directional discovery
- rejection
- return to balance
- opposite-direction opportunity later in other modules

### Canon law
Failed directional performance should be interpreted structurally, not dismissed as random whipsaw.

### Strengthened support
Failed movement may be especially relevant when the earlier move carried weak trust from the outset because of information asymmetry, mechanism sensitivity, or poorly grounded directional authority.

---

## 4.17 Trend vs Bracket Distinction

### Canonical definition
A trending market is a directional auction with stronger migration and directional performance.
A bracketed market is a rotational auction with contained trade and repeated return through accepted value.

### Aurora use
This is one of the main parent distinctions in the canon.
Many later families and patterns inherit from this split.

---

## 4.18 Inventory Imbalance

### Canonical definition
Inventory imbalance is a short-horizon directional distortion or overextension that may require correction without implying durable higher-timeframe regime change.

### Aurora use
This helps Aurora distinguish:
- genuine regime or state transition
- short-horizon liquidation / correction
- trap / excess behavior

### Canon law
Not every sharp move implies durable discovery.
Some moves are inventory distortions that need correction.

---

## 4.19 Other-Timeframe Control

### Canonical definition
Other-timeframe control is structural evidence that participation from a larger or different timeframe is dominating the current auction behavior.

### Aurora use
This concept helps explain:
- stronger directional authority
- unusual excess behavior
- sharper break quality
- why some extremes matter more than others

### Canon law
Other-timeframe control is a support concept.
It should inform state interpretation without replacing the main state label itself.

---

## 4.20 Linked-Market Caution

### Canonical definition
Linked-market caution is the rule that present structural behavior may be shaped by influences beyond the immediate local auction being observed.

### Aurora use
Aurora should remain open to the possibility that local structure is being informed or distorted by broader interactions rather than a closed self-contained process.

### Canon law
Closed-system interpretations should be applied cautiously, especially in mixed or unstable conditions.

---

## 4.21 Divisible-Good Translation Caution

### Canonical definition
Elegant auction intuitions do not transfer cleanly into every market context without checking structural comparability.

### Aurora use
Aurora should prefer translated structural truth over direct transplantation of neat theory.

### Canon law
Do not rigidify doctrine by forcing simplified auction intuition into every market form.

# 5. PRIMARY WAVE 1 STATES — CONSOLIDATED VERSION

## 5.1 Balance State

### Canonical definition
Balance State is a rotational accepted auction condition where the market is facilitating two-sided trade around value and directional migration is not currently dominant.

### Structural markers
- repeated return through accepted area
- poor sustained directional escape
- bracket-like behavior
- responsive trade stronger than directional follow-through

### Compatible later family habitats
- range rotation
- selective failed-break reversal

### Main risks
- false directional conviction
- overtrading the middle of accepted trade
- mistaking temporary excursion for genuine discovery

### Strengthened rule
Balance remains valid, but departures from balance must be checked more carefully for whether they reflect durable discovery or fragile context-shaped excursion.

---

## 5.2 Directional Discovery State

### Canonical definition
Directional Discovery State is a directional auction condition in which the market is migrating away from prior balance with enough initiative, asymmetry, and directional performance to suggest search for new value.

### Structural markers
- displacement from prior balance
- better hold away from prior value
- signs of acceptance beyond old structure
- stronger directional performance than rotational behavior

### Main risks
- mistaking attempted direction for real discovery
- chasing late expansion after clean discovery is already spent
- overrating visually directional movement that does not deserve equal trust

### Strengthened rule
Directional Discovery State should now be read with stronger caution around:
- mechanism-sensitive price formation
- institutionally shaped price discovery
- linked-market influence
- apparent strength that may not deserve equal trust

A move is not high-confidence discovery merely because it displaced.

---

## 5.3 Failed Break / Trap State

### Canonical definition
Failed Break / Trap State is the condition in which an attempted directional break loses acceptance, directional performance collapses, and the move reverts back through the initiating structure.

### Structural markers
- visible attempt away from prior structure
- insufficient sustaining trade beyond the break
- rejection back through the initiating area
- evidence of failed directional performance

### Main risks
- entering before the failure is structurally clear
- overcalling failed breaks in ordinary pullbacks

### Strengthened rule
Failed movement should not be dismissed as random chop when the earlier move was weak-trust from the outset because of information asymmetry, mechanism sensitivity, or poorly grounded authority.

---

## 5.4 Transitional / Unstable State

### Canonical definition
Transitional / Unstable State is a mixed auction condition where the previous state is degrading but the next stable state is not yet convincingly established.

### Structural markers
- mixed evidence
- weak directional performance
- uncertainty between balance, discovery, and failure
- inconsistent acceptance profile

### Main risks
- forcing a preferred label too early
- narrative overfitting

### Strengthened rule
Aurora should remain open to the possibility that ambiguity is real rather than a labeling failure, especially when linked-market or mechanism effects may be distorting local structure.

# 7. CORE STATE-DIAGNOSTIC RULES

## Rule 1 — Auction before label
Interpret the market as an auction condition before naming the state.

## Rule 2 — Present-tense structural evidence outranks stale narrative
Market-generated information has priority in present-tense state reading.

## Rule 3 — Discovery requires more than excursion
A move away from prior structure must show directional performance and/or acceptance before it earns the discovery label.

## Rule 4 — Acceptance and rejection are core structural separators
Acceptance and rejection are not cosmetic terms.
They are central to state classification.

## Rule 5 — Attempted direction must be separated from directional performance
The market can try to move somewhere without actually performing well enough to justify the state label.

## Rule 6 — Initiative and responsive activity are structurally distinct
These must remain separate in interpretation.

## Rule 7 — Excess is evidence of auction termination pressure
Excess strengthens end-of-move and rejection interpretation.

## Rule 8 — Value migration matters more than one-off displacement
The market is only truly changing state if accepted trade itself is relocating.

## Rule 9 — Observed price behavior is mechanism-sensitive
Price movement should not be treated as if it emerged from a uniform strategic environment.

## Rule 10 — Visually directional movement is not automatically trustworthy discovery
Visible strength must be checked against trust, acceptance, and context-of-formation.

## Rule 11 — Ambiguity may be real
Mixed or unstable conditions may reflect actual linked-market or mechanism distortion rather than mere labeling weakness.

## Rule 12 — Translate theory, do not transplant it blindly
Aurora should prefer translated structural truth over elegant but overly direct theoretical import.
