# 2. STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS TO THE MARKET-STATE CANON

## 2.1 Mechanism Sensitivity Rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should assume that observed price behavior is **mechanism-sensitive**, not mechanism-neutral.

### Why this is justified
The auction sources make clear that different auction rules change behavior and outcomes. Market microstructure also makes clear that trading rules shape prices rather than simply transmitting them neutrally.

### Canonical implication
When Aurora interprets a state, it should avoid treating every visible move as if it emerged from the same strategic environment.

### What this changes
This strengthens caution around:
- overgeneralizing discovery
- assuming one market’s structure maps cleanly to another
- treating chart geometry as self-explanatory

---

## 2.2 Price Discovery Is Institutionally Shaped

### Strengthening statement
Directional discovery should be interpreted as a product of institutional trading structure and interaction rules, not just as free-floating directional geometry.

### Why this is justified
Auction Theory frames auctions as price-formation mechanisms, and Auction Theory2 shows that institutional auction form affects bidding behavior and revealed outcomes.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should keep a stronger distinction between:
- visually directional movement
- institutionally credible price discovery

### What this changes
This increases caution before labeling any move as genuine discovery purely because it displaced from a prior area.

---

## 2.3 Linked-Market Caution

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should allow for the possibility that present structural behavior is being shaped by linked-market influences rather than a self-contained local auction.

### Why this is justified
Auction Theory2 explicitly discusses forward-market and secondary-market linkage around Treasury auctions.

### Canonical implication
Transitional and unstable states should remain more open to the possibility that local structure is being distorted or informed by broader interaction outside the immediate observed slice.

### What this changes
This strengthens:
- Transitional / Unstable State interpretation
- caution around oversimplifying apparent state shifts
- skepticism toward closed-system interpretations of price behavior

---

## 2.4 Winner's-Curse / Adverse-Selection Caution

### Strengthening statement
Not all visible strength deserves equal trust when the environment may reward better-informed participation and disadvantage less-informed participation.

### Why this is justified
Auction Theory2 highlights winner's-curse dynamics and bidder concentration concerns.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should strengthen the distinction between:
- apparent strength
- trustworthy strength

### What this changes
This reinforces the existing Q1 distinction between:
- attempted direction
- directional performance

and adds a further caution layer:
- not all successful movement is equally trustworthy from an information-quality perspective

---

## 2.5 Divisible-Good Translation Caution

### Strengthening statement
Aurora must not transfer elegant, clean auction intuitions into every market context without checking structural comparability.

### Why this is justified
Auction Theory2 explicitly warns that divisible-good auction settings differ meaningfully from indivisible-good intuition.

### Canonical implication
Aurora doctrine should continue to prefer translated structural truth over direct transplantation of theory.

### What this changes
This strengthens the system’s general anti-naivety rule and supports keeping doctrine flexible rather than over-rigid.

---

## 2.6 Market-Structure-Dependent Meaning

### Strengthening statement
The meaning of a move depends partly on the market structure that produced it.

### Why this is justified
Market Microstructure Theory argues that explicit and implicit trading rules influence price formation.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should treat state interpretation as partly context-of-formation dependent.

### What this changes
This supports a more realistic reading of:
- discovery
- excess
- failed breaks
- unstable transitions

because the same shape can mean different things under different market structures.

# 3. STATE-SPECIFIC STRENGTHENING

## 3.1 Directional Discovery State

### Strengthening
Directional Discovery State should now be read with stronger caution around:
- mechanism-sensitive price formation
- linked-market influence
- apparent strength that may not deserve equal trust

### Upgraded rule
A move is not high-confidence discovery merely because it displaces.
It should also survive stronger scrutiny around trust and context-of-formation.

---

## 3.2 Transitional / Unstable State

### Strengthening
This state should now be treated as even more important when linked-market influence or mechanism sensitivity may be distorting local structure.

### Upgraded rule
If the auction looks mixed, unstable, or difficult to read, Aurora should remain open to the possibility that the ambiguity is real rather than a labeling failure.

---

## 3.3 Failed Break / Trap State

### Strengthening
This state gains added support from the idea that weak-trust discovery attempts may fail because of information asymmetry, mechanism sensitivity, or poorly grounded directional authority.

### Upgraded rule
Failed movement should not be dismissed as random chop when the context suggests trust in the move was weak from the outset.

---

## 3.4 Balance State

### Strengthening
Balance remains a valid accepted auction state, but Source Set 002 adds extra caution against assuming that all departures from balance are equally meaningful.

### Upgraded rule
The move away from balance must now be checked more carefully for whether it reflects durable discovery or only a fragile, context-shaped excursion.
