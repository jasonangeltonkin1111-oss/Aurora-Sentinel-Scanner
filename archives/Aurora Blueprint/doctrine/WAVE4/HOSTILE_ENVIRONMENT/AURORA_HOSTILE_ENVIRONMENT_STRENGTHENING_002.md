# 2. HOSTILE-ENVIRONMENT STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Macro-hostility rule

### Strengthening statement
Hostile environments should not be modeled only as local execution or volatility problems.
They can also be driven by debt-cycle stress, policy distortion, political instability, and order-transition pressure.

### Why this is justified
How Countries Go Broke is organized around the Big Debt Cycle and a broader Overall Big Cycle linking debt, political, and geopolitical dynamics.

### Canonical implication
Wave 4 should preserve a broader hostility map that includes:
- debt pressure
- policy distortion
- late-cycle fragility
- order-shift instability

---

## 2.2 Context-overrides-local-cleanliness rule

### Strengthening statement
A locally clean structure can still sit inside a globally fragile backdrop that degrades follow-through, liquidity conditions, or payoff asymmetry.

### Why this is justified
Debt-cycle and order-shift framing emphasize regime context and pressure progression rather than isolated local events.

### Canonical implication
Wave 4 should preserve the rule that hostile context can reduce confidence in otherwise valid local structures without declaring those structures logically invalid.

---

## 2.3 Cross-asset pressure rule

### Strengthening statement
Hostility can express through cross-asset expected-return and premia shifts, not only through the traded chart in isolation.

### Why this is justified
Expected Returns on Major Asset Classes is centered on equity, bond, and credit premia and on the time variation of expected returns across them.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve the idea that changing premia across major asset classes can signal backdrop pressure, opportunity competition, and relative-attractiveness distortion.

---

## 2.4 Distortion-without-collapse rule

### Strengthening statement
Hostile environments do not always appear as obvious collapse.
They can appear as persistent distortion, unreliable transmission, or unusual policy-shaped behavior.

### Why this is justified
Macro debt-cycle framing implies that fragility can build and mutate across stages rather than appearing only at the terminal break.

### Canonical implication
Wave 4 should preserve earlier-warning vocabulary for degraded but not yet catastrophic environments.
