# 2. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 3 RESEARCH / COMPARISON DISCIPLINE

## 2.1 Active claims need benchmark pressure

### Extracted truth
A Random Walk Down Wall Street repeatedly defends low-cost broad indexing and efficient-market logic as a baseline challenge to active stock selection, while also acknowledging behavioral-finance critiques and limits to perfect rationality.

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that active-trading and active-selection claims need a benchmark challenge, not just internal storytelling.

### Why this matters
This keeps Aurora from treating every plausible edge narrative as automatically superior to passive or simpler alternatives.

---

## 2.2 Simple baselines matter

### Extracted truth
The Little Book That Beats the Market argues for a simple, disciplined ranking-style method rather than ornate discretionary complexity.

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that simple baseline methods deserve respect as comparison anchors.
A complicated framework should have to beat simple disciplined alternatives, not just sound more intelligent.

---

## 2.3 Source of edge matters

### Extracted truth
One Up On Wall Street emphasizes amateur observational edge, institutional lag, and the fact that professionals are often constrained, slow, or herd-like.

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that edge can come from source-of-observation advantages and institutional blind spots, not only from abstract formal models.

### Why this matters
This broadens Aurora’s idea-generation worldview without forcing it to imitate long-horizon stock-picking.

---

## 2.4 Qualitative edge exists, but not as free-form storytelling

### Extracted truth
Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits is a classic expression of qualitative business-quality and management-quality reasoning.

### Aurora translation
Aurora should preserve the rule that qualitative insight can matter, but it must still be bounded, explicit, and not allowed to become hand-wavy narrative rescue.

### Why this matters
This gives Wave 3 a more balanced worldview: not everything real is purely quantitative, but not every qualitative story deserves trust.

---

## 2.5 Prudence and margin-of-safety pressure still matter

### Extracted truth
The Intelligent Investor is preserved here as a discipline and prudence comparison source, even though deeper direct-text extraction from the uploaded MOBI copy is not yet cleanly preserved.

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve a pressure rule: active claims should be stress-tested against conservative discipline and downside awareness, not only against upside excitement.
