# 2. CORE EXTRACTIONS — MONEY MANAGEMENT / RISK SIZING SIDE

## 2.1 Quantity choice is a first-class strategic decision, not an afterthought

### Extracted truth
Ralph Vince explicitly argues that every trade contains not just a directional decision but also a quantity decision, and that this quantity decision is inseparable from account equity and perceived worst-case loss.

### Aurora translation
Aurora should preserve the rule that:
- quantity / sizing is not cosmetic
- position size is part of system logic
- risk governance should be treated as a structured design problem, not as a vague trader preference

### Why this matters
This strengthens Aurora’s resistance to naive sizing overlays and supports later wrapper-level risk-governance comparison work.

---

## 2.2 Geometric growth and drawdown trade off against each other nonlinearly

### Extracted truth
Vince frames optimal f and fixed fractional logic around geometric growth, drawdown severity, and the nonlinear cost of overbetting rather than simple straight-line risk/reward intuition.

### Aurora translation
Aurora should preserve the rule that:
- size increase is not linearly rewarded
- overbetting can destroy long-run geometric growth
- drawdown severity must remain central in risk realism

### Why this matters
This is high-value support for Wave 3 testing/risk discipline and Wave 4 hostile-environment caution.

---

## 2.3 Worst-case thinking belongs at the start of risk design

### Extracted truth
Vince repeatedly emphasizes building strategy from worst-case scenarios and preparing for catastrophic loss rather than assuming orderly price behavior.

### Aurora translation
Aurora should preserve the principle that:
- risk design begins with worst-case thinking
- catastrophic scenario awareness is part of real strategy design
- market price behavior cannot be assumed continuous or orderly under stress

### Why this matters
This cross-links naturally into Wave 4 volatility/hostile-environment realism.

---

## 2.4 Simple money-management myths should be rejected explicitly

### Extracted truth
Vince directly rejects common misconceptions such as:
- more risk means linearly more reward
- diversification solves drawdown more than it really does
- price behaves rationally and orderly tick-by-tick

### Aurora translation
Aurora should preserve explicit warnings against:
- straight-line risk/reward assumptions
- naive diversification confidence
- continuity assumptions that ignore gap or leap behavior

### Why this matters
This strengthens Aurora’s realism around drawdown, fragility, and execution/risk assumptions.
