## 2.2 Bias diagnosis needs a richer language

### Extracted truth
Kahneman explicitly says the goal is to improve the vocabulary used to identify and understand systematic errors of judgment and choice, and he presents biases as recurring diagnostic patterns rather than one-off quirks. fileciteturn115file3

### Aurora translation
Wave 5 should preserve the rule that review and adaptation work need explicit bias labels and diagnostic vocabulary, not only vague “discipline” language.

## 2.3 Fast versus slow cognition matters in review

### Extracted truth
Kahneman presents System 1 as fast, automatic, associative, and low-effort, while System 2 is slower, effortful, deliberate, and attention-demanding. fileciteturn115file3

### Aurora translation
Wave 5 should preserve the rule that some failures arise from automatic System 1 substitution and others from weak System 2 oversight.

### Why this matters
This supports more precise post-trade and post-decision diagnosis.

---

## 2.4 Risk appetite is physiologically unstable under stress and success

### Extracted truth
The uploaded Dog/Wolf summary explicitly describes Coates’s core claim that testosterone can reinforce confidence and risk taking after success, while cortisol rises during stress and failure and suppresses risk appetite. fileciteturn115file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 5 should preserve a comparison rule that operator state and physiology can shift risk appetite in cyclical ways after wins and losses.

# 3. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 3 RESEARCH / EVALUATION DISCIPLINE

## 3.1 Rationality and behavioral error are not binary opposites

### Extracted truth
Lo argues that many behavioral deviations can be understood as adaptive heuristics responding to changing environments, rather than as simple proof that markets are irrational. fileciteturn115file1

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that apparently irrational behavior may still reflect adaptive fit or maladaptive carryover from prior environments.

---

## 3.2 Intuition can be valid, but only under the right conditions

### Extracted truth
Kahneman distinguishes expert intuition that arises from long practice and valid recognition from heuristic substitution that answers easier questions instead of the real one. fileciteturn115file3

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that intuition should neither be romanticized nor dismissed wholesale; it should be judged by domain validity and feedback conditions.

---

## 3.3 Statistical thinking is harder than causal/story thinking

### Extracted truth
Kahneman explicitly emphasizes that System 1 thinks associatively and causally, while statistical thinking is harder and often neglected, helping explain why people jump to conclusions. fileciteturn115file3

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve stronger pressure against causal-story overreach when statistical structure is weak or ignored.
