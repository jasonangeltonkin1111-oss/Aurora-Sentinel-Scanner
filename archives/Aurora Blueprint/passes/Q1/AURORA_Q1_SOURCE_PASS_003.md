# 2. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 1 MARKET-STATE STRENGTHENING

## 2.1 Auction theory is institution-first

### Extracted truth
The paper emphasizes that auction theory begins from the rules of trade and the institution that organizes bidding, rather than treating price as a free-floating abstract variable.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- Aurora should continue strengthening its rule that market state is shaped by institutional mechanism, not just by visible price geometry

### Why this matters
This deepens the system’s mechanism-sensitivity doctrine and reinforces that state labels are interpretations of structured interaction, not just candle shapes.

---

## 2.2 Auction theory applies to markets, not just single-sale events

### Extracted truth
The paper explicitly argues that auction theory can be used to study two-sided bidding and market institutions, not only one-off seller/bidder formats.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- Aurora can legitimately use auction-theory language to reason about ongoing market conditions
- auction-process doctrine should not be treated as metaphor only; it is structurally relevant to market interpretation

### Why this matters
This strengthens the root Wave 1 law that markets should be interpreted as auctions in motion.

---

## 2.3 Feasible outcomes depend on implementability constraints

### Extracted truth
The paper emphasizes that feasible allocation rules are constrained by incentive compatibility and individual rationality.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- not every elegant theoretical outcome is operationally feasible
- visible market opportunity must be separated from feasible actionable opportunity

### Wave 1 implication
This strengthens caution around overconfident interpretation of state transitions as if they guaranteed realizable opportunity.

---

## 2.4 Entry and participation conditions matter

### Extracted truth
The paper discusses entry as an endogenous feature of auction environments and shows that participation conditions affect outcomes.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- observed market states may depend strongly on who is participating and under what incentives
- state interpretation should remain open to participation-sensitive distortions

### Wave 1 implication
This strengthens:
- Transitional / Unstable State
- trust caution around thin or uneven participation

---

## 2.5 Asymmetry and correlated information matter

### Extracted truth
The paper explicitly highlights asymmetry, correlated information, and richer information structures as complications for auction outcomes.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- not all visible state transitions deserve equal trust when information asymmetry may be shaping outcomes
- apparent directional clarity may still be low-trust when the information environment is uneven

### Wave 1 implication
This strengthens the distinction between:
- visible discovery
- trustworthy discovery

---

## 2.6 Double-auction and market boundary support

### Extracted truth
The paper discusses double auctions and the way auction logic extends into market settings.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- market state should continue to be interpreted with awareness that two-sided interaction and institutional matching rules matter
- auction-process doctrine should remain central rather than optional in Aurora’s market-state language

# 3. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 1 EXECUTION-CONTEXT STRENGTHENING

## 3.1 Implementability vs visible opportunity

### Extracted truth
Because the paper stresses feasibility constraints, it supports the distinction between what appears achievable in theory and what is actually implementable under the institutional rules.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- execution-context doctrine should continue to separate visible opportunity from implementable opportunity

### Why this matters
This deepens the existing execution-context rule that structural validity is not the same as practical deployability.

## 3.2 Participation and entry shape deployability

### Extracted truth
Entry and participation conditions influence the environment in which prices and allocations emerge.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- deployability should be treated as partly participation-sensitive
- weak, concentrated, or distorted participation can reduce trust in apparent opportunity

---

## 3.3 Information asymmetry lowers trust quality

### Extracted truth
Asymmetry and correlated information are highlighted as important complications in auction environments.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- visible strength may deserve reduced practical trust when the environment implies uneven information quality
- execution-context doctrine should continue to distinguish between visible strength and deployable trustworthy strength

## 3.4 Institution-sensitive execution realism

### Extracted truth
Because the paper treats auction rules and mechanisms as central, it supports the broader execution-context doctrine that deployability depends on the structure of the institution that generates the opportunity.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- execution-context doctrine should remain institution-sensitive, not chart-shape-only
