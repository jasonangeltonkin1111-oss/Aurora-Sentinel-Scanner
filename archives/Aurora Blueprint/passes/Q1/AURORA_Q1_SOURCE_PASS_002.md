# 2. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 1 MARKET-STATE STRENGTHENING

## 2.1 Auction mechanism matters

### Extracted truth
Auction Theory and Auction Theory2 both support that the auction mechanism changes bidder behavior and observed prices. Different auction rules create different strategic responses.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- state interpretation should remain cautious about assuming that observed price behavior is mechanism-neutral
- identical-looking outcomes can emerge from different strategic environments

### Why this matters for Aurora
Aurora should not over-read raw price movement as if market structure and mechanism were irrelevant.

---

## 2.2 Price discovery is mechanism-dependent

### Extracted truth
Auction Theory explicitly frames auctions as models of price formation. Auction Theory2 treats auctions as mechanisms for price discovery and highlights that institutional form matters.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- directional discovery and value migration should be read with awareness that price formation depends on the underlying trading mechanism
- market state is not just a geometric pattern; it emerges from structured interaction rules

---

## 2.3 Winner's-curse caution and weak-trust discovery

### Extracted truth
Auction Theory2 highlights the winner's-curse problem and the way certain auction forms can discourage relatively uninformed bidders and concentrate bidding.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- apparent directional authority may be low-trust when the environment encourages adverse-selection pressure or concentrated informed participation
- this supports caution against overrating visible discovery without considering the information environment

### Wave 1 implication
This strengthens the boundary between:
- visible directional movement
- trustworthy directional movement

---

## 2.4 Divisible-good caution

### Extracted truth
Auction Theory2 stresses that intuition from indivisible-good auctions does not transfer cleanly into divisible-good auctions like Treasury auctions.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- Aurora must avoid carrying clean textbook auction intuitions into all market states without checking whether the market environment is structurally comparable

### Why this matters
It reinforces a broader Aurora rule:
- do not blindly transfer elegant theory into practical doctrine without structural translation

---

## 2.5 Linked-market caution

### Extracted truth
Auction Theory2 highlights both a forward market before auction and a secondary market after auction, and explains that these linked markets affect bidding behavior and price discovery.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- present market behavior may reflect interactions beyond the immediate local mechanism
- state interpretation should remain open to linked-market influence rather than assuming a closed local process

### Wave 1 implication
This supports stronger caution in transitional and unstable conditions, where apparent structure may be reflecting broader linkage rather than clean local auction logic.

# 3. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 1 EXECUTION-CONTEXT STRENGTHENING

## 3.1 Trading rules shape prices

### Extracted truth
Market Microstructure Theory explicitly says the explicit or implicit trading rules governing a mechanism result in the formation of prices, and that the actual mechanism matters rather than being a neutral pipe. fileciteturn81file2

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- deployment quality cannot be judged from chart shape alone
- the trading mechanism and its rules influence what price movement means and how trustworthy it is

---

## 3.2 Immediacy has a cost

### Extracted truth
Market Microstructure Theory explains that traders who demand immediate execution face costs, and that the price process depends on immediacy, transaction cost, and the structure of trading. The early Demsetz discussion specifically ties transaction costs and immediacy to price formation. fileciteturn81file2

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- visible opportunity must be discounted when execution requires immediacy under poor conditions
- execution-context doctrine should explicitly distinguish between a structurally valid move and a move that is expensive or fragile to access

---

## 3.3 Spread is structural, not cosmetic

### Extracted truth
Market Microstructure Theory’s inventory-model discussion shows bid/ask prices and spread are tied to risk, order arrival, dealer survival, and market structure rather than being incidental noise. fileciteturn81file2

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- spread burden reflects structural conditions of the market and should be treated as meaningful context, not as a minor nuisance
- spread and deployment friction belong in the execution-context layer as first-class conditions

---

## 3.4 Order flow and inventory shape deployment quality

### Extracted truth
Market Microstructure Theory explicitly frames market-maker pricing and viability in terms of order arrival, inventory position, and the danger of failure. The dealer’s quotes and spread reflect protection against order-flow and inventory risk. fileciteturn81file2

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- low-trust execution environments often arise from order-flow and inventory conditions, not just from visible chart messiness
- apparent directional moves may be inventory-distortion artifacts rather than durable opportunities

---

## 3.5 Market structure changes interpretation

### Extracted truth
The early chapters of Market Microstructure Theory emphasize that the same abstract price process can behave differently under different trading mechanisms and market organizations. fileciteturn81file2

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- context quality depends on how the market is organized and how trade is being facilitated
- execution-context doctrine should remain aware that not all markets or venues transmit the same meaning through similar-looking moves

---

## 3.6 Orders are the building blocks of execution

### Extracted truth
Trading and Exchanges explicitly frames orders as trade instructions and the fundamental building blocks of trading strategies. It emphasizes that order submission strategy is one of the main determinants of trading success and that order properties define how execution happens.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- execution-context doctrine should later connect market quality to orderability, not only to state reading
- practical opportunity is inseparable from the ability to express that opportunity with the right kind of order under the right market conditions

---

## 3.7 Trader roles and venue structure matter

### Extracted truth
Trading and Exchanges explains that who trades, where they trade, and under what venue rules materially affects execution and market behavior.

### Aurora translation
Canonical strengthening:
- execution-context doctrine should remain venue-aware and role-aware at the conceptual level
- deployability is partly a function of the specific market setting, not only the chart event
