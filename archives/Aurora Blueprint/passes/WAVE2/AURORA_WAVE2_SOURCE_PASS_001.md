# 2. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR STRATEGY FAMILY REGISTRY

## 2.1 Trend, range, and transition are the primary family habitats

### Extracted truth
Grimes explicitly structures much of the book around trends, trading ranges, and the interfaces between them, including breakout from range to trend, trend to range, trend reversal, and failure of reversal.
Murphy provides broad support for trend/range thinking through trend analysis, Dow Theory, and support/resistance framing.

### Aurora translation
Primary family habitats should remain anchored in:
- trend continuation
- range rotation
- breakout / release from range to trend
- transition / reversal conditions

### Why this matters
Aurora strategy families should inherit from state habitats first, not from isolated visual setups.

---

## 2.2 Grimes' Four Trades are high-value family anchors

### Extracted truth
Grimes frames the market cycle and the Four Trades early in the book and then develops practical templates around pullbacks, breakouts, failure tests, and related structures.

### Aurora translation
The Strategy Family Registry should likely treat these as family-level anchors or close relatives:
- pullback continuation
- breakout / release
- failed breakout / failure test
- support / resistance reaction trades

### Why this matters
This is one of the cleanest bridges from state doctrine into usable family buckets.

---

## 2.3 Failure is a first-class family dimension

### Extracted truth
Grimes devotes explicit attention to failure tests and failed breakouts.
Bulkowski also treats failures as a central analytic dimension inside pattern studies and trading tactics.

### Aurora translation
Aurora should not define families only by successful pattern archetypes.
It should preserve failure-derived families, especially:
- failed breakout / trap reversal
- trend failure / reversal transition
- failed pullback continuation

### Why this matters
Failure structures are often more discriminating than idealized textbook successes.

---

## 2.4 Support and resistance are family context, not standalone doctrine

### Extracted truth
Murphy and Grimes both use support/resistance extensively. Grimes specifically places support/resistance inside trading ranges and pullback logic.

### Aurora translation
Support and resistance should feed family context and pattern location, not become a vague all-purpose doctrine.

### Why this matters
This keeps Aurora from collapsing everything into generic level trading.

---

## 2.5 Risk and trade management are secondary at this stage

### Extracted truth
Grimes includes practical trading templates, risk management, and trader development, but these are not the first Wave 2 deliverables.

### Aurora translation
Preserve these as residue for later:
- Research / Testing Method
- Strategy Review Protocol
- trader-development / review logic

### Why this matters
We do not want to dilute the Wave 2 family/pattern build with premature risk-process doctrine.

# 3. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR PATTERN ATLAS

## 3.1 Pattern atlas must be taxonomy-first, not performance-first

### Extracted truth
Bulkowski's contents and introduction show a broad encyclopedia approach with identification, failures, statistics, and trading tactics by pattern.
Nison provides a large candlestick naming system.
Murphy provides classic TA vocabulary.

### Aurora translation
The Pattern Atlas should first preserve:
- pattern name
- aliases
- context required
- family relationship
- common failure mode
- state habitat

Statistics and tactics should be preserved as notes, not allowed to dominate the atlas structure.

---

## 3.2 Bulkowski is a pattern catalog, not final truth

### Extracted truth
Bulkowski organizes an enormous set of patterns and discusses results, failures, and tactics.

### Aurora translation
Aurora should use Bulkowski as a taxonomy and failure-source, but should not import the encyclopedia as active doctrine without state/context filtering.

### Why this matters
The atlas must be context-sensitive, not a flat encyclopedia dump.

---

## 3.3 Nison is naming and nuance support

### Extracted truth
Nison provides a candlestick system and naming structure rooted in Japanese candlestick analysis.

### Aurora translation
Use Nison primarily for:
- candlestick pattern names
- reversal/continuation nuance
- pattern-language support

Do not let candlestick names become standalone strategy doctrine.

---

## 3.4 Murphy provides universal TA vocabulary, but must be filtered

### Extracted truth
Murphy's contents show broad coverage: philosophy of technical analysis, Dow Theory, chart construction, trend concepts, continuation and reversal patterns, moving averages, oscillators, volume, and futures-specific tools.

### Aurora translation
Use Murphy to stabilize broad vocabulary and classical categories, but heavily filter indicator-heavy or too-general content.

### Why this matters
Aurora needs shared language, but not generic TA bloat.

---

## 3.5 Grimes provides the cleanest bridge between pattern and family

### Extracted truth
Grimes places patterns inside market structure and practical templates instead of treating them as isolated drawings.

### Aurora translation
Pattern atlas entries should prefer Grimes-style context:
- pattern within trend
- pattern within range
- pattern at transition
- pattern as failure or pullback structure

### Why this matters
This helps Aurora connect Pattern Atlas entries to Strategy Families cleanly.
