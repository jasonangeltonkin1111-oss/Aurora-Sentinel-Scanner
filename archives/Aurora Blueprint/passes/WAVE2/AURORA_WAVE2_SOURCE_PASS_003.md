# 2. DEEPER EXTRACTIONS FOR FAMILY CARDS

## 2.1 Trend Continuation needs clearer maturity discrimination

### Extracted truth
Murphy and Grimes both support trend continuation as a legitimate family, but Grimes is especially useful because he repeatedly forces trend analysis to remain tied to market structure, pullbacks, failure, and transition rather than naïve linear extrapolation.

### Aurora translation
The Trend Continuation card should continue to distinguish more sharply between:
- fresh continuation
- orderly mature continuation
- late exhausted continuation
- pseudo-continuation inside unstable overlap

### Why this matters
A continuation family is weak if it cannot tell the difference between a living trend and a dying trend.

---

## 2.2 Trend Pullback needs stronger pullback-vs-deterioration language

### Extracted truth
Grimes is especially valuable because pullback logic is not treated as “buy dip” simplistically. It is embedded inside context, trend quality, and failure awareness.

### Aurora translation
The Trend Pullback / Pullback Continuation card should continue to sharpen:
- what makes a retracement still subordinate
- what makes it structural deterioration instead
- what turns a pullback into a trap or failed continuation setup

### Why this matters
This is one of the main family-confusion zones in live chart reading.

---

## 2.3 Breakout / Compression Release needs stronger release-vs-excursion logic

### Extracted truth
Murphy, Grimes, and Bulkowski all support breakout-type structures, but they also imply that not every break has the same meaning, durability, or trust.

### Aurora translation
The Breakout / Compression Release card should keep sharpening:
- release vs weak excursion
- accepted break vs temporary escape
- breakout vs failed breakout
- fresh release vs already-mature expansion

### Why this matters
Breakout families are among the easiest to overcall if release quality is not tested structurally.

---

## 2.4 Failed Break / Trap Reversal deserves heavier emphasis than textbook systems usually give it

### Extracted truth
Grimes and Bulkowski both preserve failure structures as meaningful, not as mere “bad versions” of successful patterns.

### Aurora translation
The Failed Break / Trap Reversal card should continue to treat failed movement as a first-class family with its own:
- structure
- reclaim logic
- anti-confusions
- transition meaning

### Why this matters
Aurora’s edge may often come more from reading failure cleanly than from chasing idealized success patterns.

---

## 2.5 Balance Rotation / Range Mean-Reversion needs stronger stable-range vs unstable-overlap discrimination

### Extracted truth
Murphy and Grimes support the importance of ranges, but they also imply that not every sideways market is equally tradable or stable.

### Aurora translation
The Balance Rotation / Range Mean-Reversion card should continue to sharpen:
- stable accepted rotation
- unstable overlap masquerading as range
- early breakout pressure emerging inside the range
- clean rejection vs noisy reversal attempts

### Why this matters
Range families fail when all sideways conditions are treated as one category.

# 3. DEEPER EXTRACTIONS FOR PATTERN CARDS

## 3.1 Pattern failure logic is one of the highest-value residues from Bulkowski and Grimes

### Extracted truth
Bulkowski’s value is not only taxonomy but also failure awareness and tactical nuance. Grimes also makes failure structures practically important.

### Aurora translation
All first pattern-group cards should continue to preserve failure logic as a core field, not an appendix.

### Why this matters
Pattern meaning is often clarified more by how a pattern fails than by how it looks when idealized.

---

## 3.2 Candlestick names are useful only when subordinated to family meaning

### Extracted truth
Nison provides a rich candlestick naming system, but candlestick names alone are too local and too easy to overread if detached from context.

### Aurora translation
Candlestick knowledge should be used mainly to:
- enrich local reversal nuance
- enrich local continuation nuance
- improve pattern-card language
- strengthen anti-confusions at decision zones

It should not dominate family or pattern classification.

### Why this matters
This protects Aurora from turning into a candlestick name engine.

---

## 3.3 Pattern cards need stronger decision-zone attachment

### Extracted truth
Murphy and Grimes both make support/resistance and structural location central to chart meaning.

### Aurora translation
Pattern cards should continue to deepen their location logic, especially:
- break through resistance vs noise above resistance
- reclaim through failed support/resistance break
- rejection at meaningful boundary vs rejection inside noise

### Why this matters
Many patterns only become meaningful because of where they occur.

---

## 3.4 Pattern groups should remain broad while the source base is still being integrated

### Extracted truth
Bulkowski and Nison can explode taxonomy very quickly if imported too literally.

### Aurora translation
Aurora should keep the current first pattern groups broad until deeper extraction proves that narrower child cards add real discriminatory power.

### Why this matters
This prevents a sprawling weak atlas full of names without leverage.

# 4. HIGH-VALUE ANTI-CONFUSIONS JUSTIFIED BY THIS PASS

- breakout vs weak excursion

These anti-confusions are among the most valuable practical extractions from the uploaded books.
