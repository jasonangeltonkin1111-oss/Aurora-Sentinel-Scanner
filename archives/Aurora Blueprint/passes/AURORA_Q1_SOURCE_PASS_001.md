# 3. CORE DOCTRINE EXTRACTIONS FOR THE MARKET STATE CANON

## 3.1 Auction Process

### Extracted truth
Aurora should treat market behavior as an auction process rather than as a static “price label” system.

This upload set strongly supports:
- markets as auctions
- price as a result of competitive interaction and information, not as absolute truth
- the importance of interpreting how the auction is behaving rather than only where price currently is

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Auction Process** = the mechanism by which markets explore, test, and facilitate trade through price over time

Operational implication for Aurora:
- every state should be interpreted as part of an ongoing auction:
  - balanced auction
  - directional auction
  - failed directional auction
  - transitioning auction

### Why this matters
This becomes the root concept underneath:
- balance
- imbalance
- acceptance
- rejection
- failed break
- value migration

---

## 3.2 Market-Generated Information

### Extracted truth
The uploaded books strongly distinguish between:
- external / stale / opinion-based information
- market-generated information in the present tense

Aurora should inherit the principle that the market itself is the highest-value immediate information source when interpreting current structure.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Market-Generated Information** = information revealed by the market’s own price, time, and participation behavior as the auction unfolds

Operational implication for Aurora:
- market-state inference should be led by current structure and auction behavior
- outside narratives may inform context, but must not override present-tense structural evidence

### What this protects against
- stale opinion dominance
- lagging narrative bias
- overreliance on external explanation after the market has already shifted state

---

## 3.3 Balance / Bracket

### Extracted truth
This upload set strongly supports a condition where the market is rotating within an accepted area rather than discovering strongly in one direction.

Mind Over Markets explicitly frames:
- bracketed markets
- rotational trade
- value-centered behavior
- day structures where directional conviction is weak or absent

Markets in Profile supports:
- fair-value seeking
- balance / stability phases
- ebb and flow of balance

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Balance** = an accepted, rotational auction condition where trade is being facilitated around a relatively stable value area and directional discovery is not currently dominant

Canonical state:
- **Balance State** = rotational, accepted, bracket-like behavior with weak directional migration

Key structural markers:
- repeated return to central trade area
- poor sustained directional escape
- two-sided trade
- lower directional conviction than true discovery states

### Important synonym control
For Aurora, treat these as closely related but not always identical:
- balance
- bracket
- rotational trade
- accepted trade around value

Use “bracket” when source language is specifically about contained rotational ranges.
Use “balance” as the broader canonical concept.

---

## 3.4 Imbalance / Directional Discovery

### Extracted truth
Markets in Profile is explicit that asymmetry emerges from imbalances in evolving market structure.
The market alternates between stability and crisis, and opportunities arise when change in equilibrium becomes visible.

Mind Over Markets supports:
- trend versus bracket distinction
- attempted direction
- directional performance
- other-timeframe influence on directional movement

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Imbalance** = a structural condition in which the market is no longer simply facilitating two-way trade around value and is instead migrating directionally with stronger initiative and asymmetry

Canonical state:
- **Directional Discovery State** = a directional auction that is actively trying to establish or confirm trade away from prior balance

Key structural markers:
- directional displacement from prior balance
- stronger hold away from prior value
- evidence that the auction is seeking new value rather than just rotating

### Important nuance
Aurora should not treat every breakout print as imbalance.
Directional discovery requires more than excursion; it requires evidence that the auction is functioning directionally rather than merely probing.

---

## 3.5 Acceptance

### Extracted truth
Mind Over Markets explicitly frames the Market Profile as a way to visualize acceptance or rejection of price over time.
This is central.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Acceptance** = sustained trade quality at or beyond a reference area sufficient to indicate that price is being facilitated there rather than instantly denied

Key implications:
- acceptance is a time-and-structure event, not just a one-print event
- acceptance increases confidence that directional migration is real
- acceptance is one of Aurora’s core filters for separating discovery from noise

### Canon rule
Acceptance must be judged by:
- sustained trade behavior
- structural hold quality
- relation to prior balance / value

It must not be reduced to:
- any single close outside a level
- any momentary push beyond a boundary

---

## 3.6 Rejection

### Extracted truth
If acceptance is market facilitation at a location, rejection is the market’s failure or refusal to continue facilitating trade there.
Mind Over Markets and Markets in Profile both support this through auction-process interpretation and excess / failed directional behavior.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Rejection** = the market’s denial of sustained trade at a location after a probe or attempted migration

Key implications:
- rejection is the negative image of acceptance
- rejection frequently precedes return to balance or opposite-direction opportunity
- rejection must be interpreted in structural context, not as every pullback

---

## 3.7 Excess

### Extracted truth
Markets in Profile explicitly highlights excess as a recognizable form that occurs at the end of an auction and can reveal changing status quo.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Excess** = structural evidence that an auction has run too far relative to available participation and is exhausting rather than gaining durable acceptance

Aurora use:
- excess should be treated as an end-of-auction or terminal-auction clue
- excess is a high-value support concept for:
  - rejection
  - failed directional continuation
  - transition from trend to balance

### Important boundary
Excess is not itself a strategy signal in Aurora.
It is a structural diagnostic concept.

---

## 3.8 Value / Fair Value

### Extracted truth
Markets in Profile repeatedly ties auction behavior to fair value and the search for value.
Mind Over Markets frames rotational structure around central accepted trade.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Value Area / Accepted Value Region** = the area where the market is facilitating enough trade that price is functioning as accepted rather than exceptional

Aurora use:
- value is not an abstract fundamental “true price” in this module
- value is an auction-accepted area relevant to current structural interpretation

Why it matters:
- balance and rotation occur around value
- discovery is movement away from an old accepted value area toward another
- rejection and failed migration are best read in relation to value

---

## 3.9 Value Migration

### Extracted truth
Markets in Profile supports the idea that paradigm shifts in equilibrium and asymmetry appear when the market moves away from prior accepted structure.
This is more than price movement; it is relocation of what is being accepted.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Value Migration** = the shift of accepted trade away from a prior value area toward a newly accepted area

Aurora use:
- distinguish temporary excursion from actual migration
- migration is one of the key ways Aurora recognizes state transition from balance to directional discovery

---

## 3.10 Initiative vs Responsive Activity

### Extracted truth
Mind Over Markets explicitly names initiative versus responsive activity.
This is a major structural distinction Aurora should keep.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Initiative Activity** = behavior that pushes the auction away from accepted value toward new discovery
- **Responsive Activity** = behavior that reacts against excursions and pushes trade back toward accepted value

Aurora use:
- initiative activity aligns more with directional discovery states
- responsive activity aligns more with balance defense, rejection, and return-to-value logic

This distinction is important because it sharpens:
- breakout quality
- failed break interpretation
- range rotation interpretation

---

## 3.11 Attempted Direction vs Directional Performance

### Extracted truth
Mind Over Markets explicitly separates:
- attempted direction
- directional performance

This is extremely valuable for Aurora.

### Aurora translation
Canonical distinction:
- **Attempted Direction** = where the auction is trying to go
- **Directional Performance** = how well the market is actually doing in sustaining that directional attempt

Aurora use:
- prevents overrating weak moves
- helps separate genuine discovery from low-quality directional claims
- improves the distinction between imbalance, failed break, and unstable transition

This should become a state-diagnostic rule inside the Market State Canon.

---

## 3.12 Failed Auction / Failed Break / Trap

### Extracted truth
This upload set supports the idea that auctions can attempt directional continuation and fail.
Mind Over Markets contains multiple related structures:
- trend traders’ trap
- correction of inventory imbalances
- failed directional performance
- return from poor directional attempts

Markets in Profile supports end-of-auction and imbalance correction logic.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Failed Auction / Failed Break** = an attempted directional move that cannot gain or maintain acceptance and instead reverts back through the initiating structure

Aurora use:
- this is a first-class structural event
- it should be treated as a state transition source, not as noise

High-value links:
- rejection
- excess
- responsive activity
- return to balance
- opposite-direction opportunity later in strategy modules

---

## 3.13 Trend vs Bracket

### Extracted truth
Mind Over Markets explicitly distinguishes trending versus bracketed markets.
This is one of the cleanest structural distinctions in the uploaded set.

### Aurora translation
Canonical distinction:
- **Trending Market** = directional auction with stronger migration and directional performance
- **Bracketed Market** = rotational auction with contained trade and repeated return through accepted value

Aurora use:
- this distinction becomes one of the main parent branches for state classification
- many later families will inherit from this split

---

## 3.14 Inventory Imbalance

### Extracted truth
Mind Over Markets explicitly references inventory imbalances and their correction.
This is important because not every short-horizon directional move represents true longer-horizon discovery.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Inventory Imbalance** = short-horizon directional distortion or overextension that may require correction without implying a durable higher-timeframe regime change

Aurora use:
- this concept is important for distinguishing:
  - genuine state transition
  - short-horizon correction
  - trap / excess / liquidation behavior

This belongs partly in the Market State Canon and partly later in strategy and execution interpretation.

---

## 3.15 Other-Timeframe Control

### Extracted truth
Mind Over Markets emphasizes other-timeframe control and distinguishes its effect on extremes and inside the body of the profile.

### Aurora translation
Canonical concept:
- **Other-Timeframe Control** = structural evidence that participation from a larger or different timeframe is dominating the current auction behavior

Aurora use:
- this helps explain why some auctions have stronger directional authority
- it should become a support concept inside directional discovery, excess, and balance-break interpretation

This is important, but should remain a support-layer concept rather than becoming the primary state label by itself.
