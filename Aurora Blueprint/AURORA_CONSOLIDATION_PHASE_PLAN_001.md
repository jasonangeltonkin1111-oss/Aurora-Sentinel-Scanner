# 4. CONSOLIDATION LAWS

## Law 1 — Canon absorbs, lineage remembers
Consolidated files should absorb doctrine truth.
They do not replace source passes, manifests, strengthening files, or run logs as continuity evidence.

## Law 2 — Preserve boundary labels
If a distinction is:
- provisional
- supportive
- reference-only
- deferred

that status must remain visible.

## Law 3 — Keep operator-facing surfaces tighter than lineage files
Consolidated files should be clearer and tighter than the strengthening stack, but not less honest.

## Law 4 — No fake closure
A consolidated file can still name:
- deferred residue
- unresolved competition between models
- later consolidation hooks

## Law 5 — One consolidation target at a time
Do not attempt to unify all waves at once.
