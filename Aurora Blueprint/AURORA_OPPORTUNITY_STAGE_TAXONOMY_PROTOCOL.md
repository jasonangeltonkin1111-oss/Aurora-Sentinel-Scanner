
# AURORA OPPORTUNITY STAGE TAXONOMY PROTOCOL

## PURPOSE

This file formalizes Aurora's opportunity-stage language.

Aurora already recognized that opportunity can remain valid across changing timing conditions.
This protocol gives that truth a canonical vocabulary so family doctrine, pattern doctrine, cards, packets, reviews, and wrapper packs can stay synchronized.

---

# 1. ROOT LAW

Opportunity stage is a structural timing class, not a universal quality score.

A stage label should answer:
- where the case sits inside its family lifecycle
- how much confirmation already exists
- how much path remains
- whether continuation, re-entry, or salvage logic is required
- whether the case is already too exhausted to preserve

A later stage is not automatically worse.
An earlier stage is not automatically better.
Stage truth must be interpreted together with family logic, pattern logic, bucket context, regime context, deployability, and remaining path quality.

---

# 2. CANONICAL STAGE SET

Aurora should use the following canonical stage classes when stage expression is needed:

1. `STAGE_EMERGENT`
   - structure is beginning to form
   - family logic is plausible but still lightly confirmed

2. `STAGE_DEVELOPING`
   - the case is advancing and evidence is improving
   - the family is more than a guess but not yet strongly confirmed

3. `STAGE_CONFIRMED`
   - the family/pattern case has enough confirmation that preservation or deployment can be justified if other layers agree

4. `STAGE_MATURE`
   - structure is already established and still usable, but a meaningful portion of the move or rotation has already unfolded

5. `STAGE_LATE`
   - the case still has honest structure support, but runway is reduced and exhaustion risk is materially higher

6. `STAGE_CONTINUATION`
   - the opportunity exists specifically as a continuation-through-progress or post-confirmation continuation path rather than an initial launch point

7. `STAGE_REENTRY`
   - the original move already existed, but a structurally valid re-engagement path has reopened without resetting doctrine back to emergent

8. `STAGE_SALVAGE`
   - prior execution geometry or initial timing failed, yet enough family structure remains that a recovery-style opportunity may still be preserved

9. `STAGE_EXHAUSTED`
   - the family may explain what happened, but the opportunity itself is no longer valid for preservation or deployment

---

# 3. STAGE INTERPRETATION LAW

These stages are not required to appear in every family in identical ways.

Aurora should allow:
- some families to express rich continuation and re-entry behavior
- some families to move quickly from confirmed to exhausted
- some families to rarely use salvage
- some families to treat mature or late stages as more legitimate than emergent stages

The stage taxonomy is shared vocabulary, not forced symmetry.

---

# 4. FAMILY-AWARE STAGE LAW

Each family should eventually define a stage profile covering:
- which stages are native
- which stages are legitimate but degraded
- which stages are usually invalid or exhausted
- what transitions tend to create confusion with competing families

Examples:
- trend continuation families often legitimize continuation, mature, late, and re-entry stages
- breakout release families often care heavily about emergent, developing, confirmed, and early-continuation stages
- failed-break reversal families often become clearer only after developing or confirmed failure evidence exists
- balance-rotation families may become most usable after the range is already well established rather than at first touch

---

# 5. PATTERN-AWARE STAGE LAW

Patterns should inherit stage meaning from their parent family neighborhood.

Aurora should not use a pattern label to erase stage truth.
A pattern may therefore be:
- early inside one family
- mature inside another
- invalid if the family stage has already crossed into exhaustion

Pattern cards and wrapper packs should preserve stage fit or stage tension where it matters.

---

# 6. BUCKET / REGIME CONTEXT LAW

Stage legitimacy is not bucket-blind.

Aurora should preserve that:
- some buckets allow cleaner late-stage continuation than others
- some hostile environments compress the useful life of a stage dramatically
- some rotation families need calmer or more orderly conditions before mature-stage participation stays honest
- some re-entry opportunities are only acceptable when execution burden remains bounded for the bucket

---

# 7. LATE-JOIN LEGITIMACY LAW

Aurora explicitly recognizes that:
- some good trades begin after the first move
- some structures are easier to trust after additional evidence appears
- a cleaner mid-progress entry may be preferable to a noisier early guess
- "not first" is not the same as "too late"

Late-stage legitimacy requires honest accounting of:
- remaining path quality
- exhaustion pressure
- degraded geometry freedom
- tighter invalidation needs
- family-specific stage rules

---

# 8. STAGE FAILURE LAW

If the best stage label is `STAGE_EXHAUSTED`, Aurora should preserve that truth even if the family diagnosis is still correct.

A correct family read does not guarantee a live opportunity.
Stage exhaustion is one of the valid pathways into:
- `OBSERVE_ONLY`
- `EXECUTION_INVALID`
- `STRUCTURE_INVALID`
- card blocking

---

# 9. REQUIRED DOWNSTREAM USAGE

When relevant, downstream surfaces should preserve:
- `opportunity_stage`
- `stage_justification`
- `stage_risks`
- `stage_transition_watchpoints`

Family cards, pattern cards, generated strategy cards, packets, and review packets may add more detail, but they should not contradict this stage vocabulary.

---

# 10. CURRENT JUDGMENT

Aurora now has a canonical stage taxonomy that legitimizes early, mid-progress, late-join, continuation, re-entry, salvage, and exhausted states without flattening all families into one timing template.
