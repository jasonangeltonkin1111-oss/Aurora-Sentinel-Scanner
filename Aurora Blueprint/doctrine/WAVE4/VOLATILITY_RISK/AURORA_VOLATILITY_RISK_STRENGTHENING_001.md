# 2. VOLATILITY / RISK STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Volatility-state rule

### Strengthening statement
Volatility should be treated as a structural state condition that changes the meaning and trustworthiness of market behavior.

### Why this is justified
Option volatility texts and volatility-trading texts treat volatility as a direct determinant of pricing, behavior, and strategy viability.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should not treat volatility as a decorative overlay. It should alter interpretation of patterns, states, and opportunities.

---

## 2.2 Nonlinearity rule

### Strengthening statement
Risk and exposure are often nonlinear, especially under stress, and should not be approximated as safely linear by default.

### Why this is justified
Dynamic Hedging emphasizes convexity, nonlinear exposure, and path dependence.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve explicit caution against assuming that market conditions, exposures, or losses scale smoothly.

---

## 2.3 Path-dependence rule

### Strengthening statement
How the market gets somewhere matters, not just where it ends up.

### Why this is justified
Taleb’s framework highlights path dependence and dynamic hedging behavior under changing conditions.

### Canonical implication
Risk doctrine should include the possibility that identical endpoints can conceal very different risk paths.

---

## 2.4 Implied-vs-realized risk rule

### Strengthening statement
Expected or implied risk and realized movement must be treated as related but distinct layers of information.

### Why this is justified
Option volatility literature is built around distinguishing implied volatility from realized volatility.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should remain sensitive to the difference between current realized behavior and the broader risk regime implied by the environment.

---

## 2.5 Volatility-regime rule

### Strengthening statement
Volatility should be understood in regime-like terms rather than as a constant background property.

### Why this is justified
Volatility Trading frames volatility as behavior that changes across conditions rather than staying uniform.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve regime distinctions such as:
- compressed / quiet volatility
- expanding / unstable volatility
- stressed / hostile volatility

---

## 2.6 Tail-risk rule

### Strengthening statement
Rare, extreme events can matter more than ordinary variation suggests and should not be treated as negligible residue.

### Why this is justified
The Black Swan strongly attacks naïve normality assumptions and underestimation of extreme events.

### Canonical implication
Aurora risk doctrine should keep explicit space for tail exposure even when recent conditions appear calm.

---

## 2.7 Uncertainty-humility rule

### Strengthening statement
Risk doctrine should preserve humility under uncertainty rather than pretending to fully domesticate risk with elegant formulas.

### Why this is justified
Against the Gods treats risk as a broader probabilistic worldview and discipline of uncertainty.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should frame risk as something to manage and respect, not something fully conquered by neat modeling.
