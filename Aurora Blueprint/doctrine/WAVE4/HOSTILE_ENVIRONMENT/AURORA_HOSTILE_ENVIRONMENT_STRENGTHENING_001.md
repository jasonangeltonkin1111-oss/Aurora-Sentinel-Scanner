# 2. HOSTILE-ENVIRONMENT STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Discontinuity rule

### Strengthening statement
Some environments are dangerous specifically because smooth-condition assumptions break under discontinuity, jumps, or market breaks.

### Why this is justified
Dynamic Hedging strongly emphasizes breakdown under discontinuity and imperfect real-world conditions.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve hostile-environment states where continuity assumptions are weak and normal-state extrapolation is dangerous.

---

## 2.2 Fragility rule

### Strengthening statement
A model, strategy, or interpretation that performs well only under smooth assumptions may be dangerously fragile in hostile conditions.

### Why this is justified
Taleb and Black Swan both strongly imply that elegance often hides fragility.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should prefer robustness and exposure-awareness over theoretical neatness.

---

## 2.3 Anti-naive-normality rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should not assume that normal-looking periods imply a negligible probability or impact of extreme events.

### Why this is justified
The Black Swan directly attacks the underestimation of rare extremes by standard comfort models.

### Canonical implication
Hostile-environment doctrine should preserve explicit skepticism toward complacency in quiet periods.

---

## 2.4 Hostile volatility rule

### Strengthening statement
Some volatility regimes are not just “higher volatility” but structurally hostile environments that degrade normal interpretation and deployment assumptions.

### Why this is justified
Volatility Trading and Taleb together support the idea that regime changes alter the meaning of strategy and interpretation quality.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve a distinction between:
- normal volatility variation
- hostile volatility regime

---

## 2.5 Robustness-over-elegance rule

### Strengthening statement
Under hostile conditions, a less elegant but more robust approach is superior to a beautiful but fragile one.

### Why this is justified
Black Swan and Taleb both imply that theoretical elegance without robustness can be catastrophic.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should bias its hostile-environment doctrine toward survivability, not toward aesthetic model purity.

---

## 2.6 Tail-dominance rule

### Strengthening statement
Rare events can dominate outcomes enough that they should influence doctrine even when they are infrequent.

### Why this is justified
Black Swan explicitly emphasizes that rare extremes dominate more than standard narratives admit.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve hostile-environment caution as a structural doctrine layer rather than an occasional footnote.
