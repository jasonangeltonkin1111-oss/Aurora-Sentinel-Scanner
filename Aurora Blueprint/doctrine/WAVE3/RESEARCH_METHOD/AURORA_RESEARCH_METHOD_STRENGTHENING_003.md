# 2. RESEARCH-METHOD STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Quantity is part of the strategy hypothesis, not just trade administration

### Strengthening statement
Research should treat position size as part of the strategy design problem rather than as a detached administrative afterthought.

### Why this is justified
Vince explicitly frames every trade as containing both a directional decision and a quantity decision tied to equity and worst-case loss assumptions.

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s research method should preserve explicit prompts such as:
- what size logic is assumed?
- what equity fraction is implicitly at risk?
- how sensitive is the edge to quantity choice?

### What this changes
This makes sizing part of strategy realism rather than an external overlay.

---

## 2.2 Geometric growth should be weighed against drawdown pain explicitly

### Strengthening statement
Research should preserve the nonlinear tradeoff between maximizing long-run growth and tolerating drawdown severity.

### Why this is justified
Vince frames optimal f and fixed-fraction thinking around geometric growth, drawdown risk, and the destructive effect of overbetting.

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s research method should preserve explicit risk questions such as:
- what drawdown profile does this sizing logic imply?
- does the growth logic become fragile under realistic loss sequences?
- is the edge only attractive because it assumes intolerable drawdown?

### What this changes
This strengthens risk discipline inside research rather than leaving it for later portfolio cleanup.

---

## 2.3 Worst-case-first design should outrank optimistic average-case thinking

### Strengthening statement
Research should begin from catastrophic and discontinuous loss possibilities rather than assuming orderly average-case behavior.

### Why this is justified
Vince repeatedly emphasizes worst-case scenario thinking and rejects simplistic continuity assumptions in market behavior.

### Canonical implication
Aurora’s research method should preserve a worst-case-first sequence:
1. define catastrophic path
2. test fragility under adverse sequences
3. only then consider average-case growth appeal

### What this changes
This makes research harder to fool with attractive but fragile expectancy logic.

---

## 2.4 Naive money-management myths should be explicitly rejected

### Strengthening statement
Research should explicitly resist common myths such as:
- more size means linearly more reward
- diversification automatically solves drawdown
- price and loss realization behave smoothly enough to justify naive sizing assumptions

### Why this is justified
Vince explicitly attacks these simplistic assumptions and frames money management as a mathematically sensitive design problem.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve anti-naive money-management warnings inside research and evidence work.

### What this changes
This strengthens the anti-handwavy realism of the Wave 3 method backbone.
