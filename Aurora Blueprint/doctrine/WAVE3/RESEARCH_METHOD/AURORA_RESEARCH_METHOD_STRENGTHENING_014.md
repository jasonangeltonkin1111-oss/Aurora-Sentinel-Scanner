# 2. RESEARCH-METHOD STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Field-research rule

### Strengthening statement
Research should remain open to field-reality checks from customers, suppliers, competitors, and other informed participants when those checks materially improve understanding.

### Why this is justified
Kenneth Fisher’s preface explicitly frames Philip Fisher’s scuttlebutt method as learning about a firm through real-world information gathered from its ecosystem rather than through rumor or Wall Street noise. fileciteturn119file0

### Canonical implication
Wave 3 should preserve bounded respect for field-reality research rather than treating all research as screen-only or chart-only.

---

## 2.2 Quality-plus-cheapness rule

### Strengthening statement
Research should avoid treating cheapness alone as a sufficient conclusion when durable quality is missing.

### Why this is justified
Common Stocks frames the fifteen points as tests of durable business quality, while Greenblatt’s core framing is “good businesses at bargain prices,” not cheapness alone. fileciteturn119file0turn119file1

### Canonical implication
Wave 3 should preserve stronger pressure to combine quality and bargain logic rather than flattening them into one-dimensional value claims.

---

## 2.3 Simplicity-over-theater rule

### Strengthening statement
Simple methods with coherent rationale should often be preferred over sophistication theater when robustness is the real objective.

### Why this is justified
Greenblatt explicitly argues that sophisticated formulas are not what make the difference and that a simple process can outperform. fileciteturn119file1

### Canonical implication
Wave 3 should preserve stronger caution against mistaking complexity for rigor.

---

## 2.4 Active-versus-baseline rule

### Strengthening statement
Active approaches should be judged relative to meaningful baselines, including safe alternatives and challenge frameworks.

### Why this is justified
Greenblatt explicitly builds his early argument by comparing risky investments with safe long-term government-bond returns. fileciteturn119file1

### Canonical implication
Wave 3 should preserve stronger baseline discipline whenever active claims are evaluated.
