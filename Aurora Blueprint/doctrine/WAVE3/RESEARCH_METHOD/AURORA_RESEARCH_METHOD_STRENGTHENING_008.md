## 2.1 Archetypal-template rule

### Strengthening statement
Research should respect repeated-case templates and archetypes when a phenomenon recurs through stable mechanism chains.

### Why this is justified

### Canonical implication

## 2.2 Mechanism-chain rule

### Strengthening statement
Macro stress should be evaluated through mechanism chains rather than through label-only narrative.

### Why this is justified
Dalio repeatedly explains debt crises as linked sequences: credit expansion, spending, income, asset-price inflation, debt-servicing strain, reversal, and deleveraging. fileciteturn83file0

### Canonical implication
Wave 3 should preserve cause-effect chain reasoning when handling macro and liquidity context.

---

## 2.3 Macro-context-aware active-evaluation rule

### Strengthening statement
Active-trading or active-selection claims should be evaluated with awareness of broader debt/liquidity context when that context is load-bearing.

### Why this is justified
Dalio’s framework is explicitly built from the perspective of an investor forced to navigate market changes in real time by understanding macro debt dynamics. fileciteturn83file0

### Canonical implication
Aurora research should preserve the rule that macro liquidity and debt context can be relevant to evaluating active claims, not only to narrating them after the fact.
