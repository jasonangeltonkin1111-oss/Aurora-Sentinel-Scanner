# 2. REVIEW-PROTOCOL STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Anti-resulting rule

### Strengthening statement
A good result is not proof of a good process, and a bad result is not proof of a bad process.

### Why this is justified
Thinking in Bets is built around the distinction between decision quality and outcome quality under uncertainty.

### Canonical implication
Every review should ask separately:
- Was the process sound?
- Was the result mainly luck/noise?
- Did luck reward a bad process or punish a good one?

---

## 2.2 Calibration rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora review should compare expected confidence with actual outcomes and learn from mismatches.

### Why this is justified
Superforecasting emphasizes keeping score and improving calibration rather than trusting subjective certainty.

### Canonical implication
Review logs should preserve:
- prior expectation
- confidence level
- actual outcome
- degree of calibration error

---

## 2.3 Scorekeeping rule

### Strengthening statement
Review quality improves when forecasts, expectations, and decisions are explicitly scored rather than remembered impressionistically.

### Why this is justified
Superforecasting treats scorekeeping as a central path to better judgment.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve structured review evidence rather than rely on narrative hindsight.

---

## 2.4 Illusion-of-validity rule

### Strengthening statement
Review must test whether confidence was driven by genuine evidence or by illusion, invalid subjectivity, and untested assumptions.

### Why this is justified
EBTA attacks invalid subjective technical analysis and emphasizes disciplined evaluation.

### Canonical implication
Review should include explicit tests for:
- illusion of validity
- untested assumptions
- narrative rescue after the fact

---

## 2.5 Framework-drift rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora review must check whether execution and evaluation stayed inside the designed framework or drifted into ad hoc discretion.

### Why this is justified
Systematic Trading emphasizes systematized decision process; Chan’s workflow and system-building logic reinforce this.

### Canonical implication
A review should ask:
- Did the process follow the intended framework?
- If not, where did drift enter?
- Was the drift justified or just impulsive?

---

## 2.6 Overfitting and backtest-seduction rule

### Strengthening statement
Review should explicitly test whether apparent confidence came from backtest seduction, data-snooping, or overfitting rather than robust process.

### Why this is justified
EBTA and Chan both foreground data-mining and backtest pitfalls.

### Canonical implication
Post-strategy review should preserve anti-overfitting checkpoints, not only performance summaries.

---

## 2.7 Process-over-outcome learning rule

### Strengthening statement
The main purpose of review is to improve future judgment and process, not just to celebrate wins or explain losses.

### Why this is justified
Thinking in Bets and Superforecasting both support iterative learning under uncertainty.

### Canonical implication
Review outputs should emphasize learning leverage rather than storytelling.
