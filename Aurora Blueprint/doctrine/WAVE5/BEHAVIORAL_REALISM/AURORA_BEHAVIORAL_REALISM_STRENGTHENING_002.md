# 2. BEHAVIORAL-REALISM STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Adaptive-ecology rule

### Strengthening statement
Behavior and edge quality should be viewed inside an adaptive market ecology rather than as fixed traits expressed in static conditions.

### Why this is justified
Lo explicitly frames market behavior through competition, adaptation, and natural selection. fileciteturn115file1

### Canonical implication
Wave 5 should preserve ecological adaptation language when diagnosing why once-valid behaviors or strategies decay.

---

## 2.2 Explicit-bias-language rule

### Strengthening statement
Behavioral review should use explicit bias labels whenever they materially improve diagnosis.

### Why this is justified
Kahneman explicitly argues for a richer vocabulary to identify systematic judgment errors. fileciteturn115file3

### Canonical implication
Wave 5 should preserve stronger bias-label discipline instead of generic “emotion” or “discipline” talk.

---

## 2.3 Fast-slow-cognition rule

### Strengthening statement
Behavioral failure can arise from fast automatic substitution or from weak slow oversight, and review should distinguish them.

### Why this is justified
Kahneman defines System 1 as fast and automatic, and System 2 as slower and deliberate. fileciteturn115file3

### Canonical implication
Wave 5 should preserve better separation between impulse failure and oversight failure.

---

## 2.4 Physiology-of-risk comparison rule

### Strengthening statement
Risk appetite should be treated as partly state-dependent and physiologically variable rather than as a constant trait.

### Why this is justified
The uploaded Dog/Wolf summary explicitly describes testosterone and cortisol shifts affecting risk taking and stress response. fileciteturn115file0

### Canonical implication
Wave 5 should preserve bounded physiology-of-risk comparison prompts, especially after winning or losing streaks.

---

## 2.5 Mental-accounting / break-even rule

### Strengthening statement
Review should explicitly test for mental-accounting distortions, sunk-cost persistence, house-money behavior, and break-even chasing.

### Why this is justified
The uploaded Misbehaving review explicitly highlights those effects as recurring behavioral distortions. fileciteturn115file2

### Canonical implication
Wave 5 should preserve those patterns as standard review checks rather than optional curiosities.
