# 1. ROOT LAW

Wave 5 exists to stop Aurora from confusing analysis quality with operator quality.

A good framework can still fail when:
- the environment changes
- the operator becomes biased
- physiology shifts risk appetite
- hindsight rewrites the story
- prior wins or losses distort current judgment

Wave 5 is not allowed to become:
- vague psychology talk
- self-help language
- motivational filler
- excuses for bad structure or bad research

A valid Wave 5 reading must ask:
- did the environment change?
- did the method lose fit?
- did review become contaminated by hindsight or narrative rescue?
- did bias, stress, or body state distort the reading?
- what concrete correction should follow?

---

# 2. WHAT WAVE 5 IS FOR

Wave 5 exists to make Aurora:
- adaptation-aware
- review-disciplined
- bias-diagnostic
- physiology-aware under pressure
- less naive about human judgment
- capable of updating without becoming unstable

It is the bridge between:
- Wave 3 research/review discipline
- Wave 4 hostile-environment realism
- future wrapper/operator prompts and self-audit logic

If Wave 5 is weak, the system will misdiagnose failures, cling to stale methods, and protect false confidence.

# 3. CANONICAL WAVE 5 OBJECTS

Every serious review/adaptation item should preserve these objects explicitly.

## 3.1 Fit
Was the method fit for the environment it encountered?

## 3.2 Bias pattern
What recurring judgment distortion may have been present?
Examples:
- hindsight contamination
- illusion of validity
- anchoring
- framing distortion
- availability bias
- house-money looseness
- break-even escalation

## 3.3 Operator state
What physiological or emotional state may have influenced decision quality?
Examples:
- overconfidence after success
- fear contraction after losses
- cortisol-heavy stress state
- biochemically inflated conviction

## 3.4 Review contamination
What part of the post-event review may be corrupted by:
- coherent-story reconstruction
- selective memory
- false certainty after noisy outcomes

## 3.5 Corrective boundary
What concrete correction follows?
Examples:
- revise habitat assumptions
- tighten evidence requirements
- mark a recurring bias trigger
- require stronger operator-state gating
- reclassify the method as environment-sensitive

---

# 4. WAVE 5 LAWS

## Law 1 — Fit before blame
When something fails, Aurora should ask whether the method was fit for the environment before assigning blame to structure, execution, or operator.

## Law 2 — Bias patterns repeat
Judgment errors should be treated as recurring, diagnosable families rather than isolated accidents.

## Law 3 — Review must resist hindsight
Post-event review should explicitly defend against hindsight contamination, narrative rescue, and false certainty.

## Law 4 — Humans are not neutral optimizers
Aurora should not model the operator as a perfectly rational, stable decision engine.

## Law 5 — Physiology matters under pressure
Body state is part of decision quality, especially under stress, success streaks, loss pressure, and high-stakes conditions.

## Law 6 — Gains and losses distort behavior asymmetrically
House-money looseness, break-even escalation, and sunk-cost attachment should remain explicit review hazards.

## Law 7 — Adaptation beats dogma
A method earns persistence through environmental fit and evidence, not tradition or prior success.

# 5. CANONICAL WAVE 5 WORKFLOW

## Step 1 — Check fit
Ask:
- what environment was present?
- was the method fit for it?
- did failure reflect edge decay, environmental change, or misuse?

## Step 2 — Check review contamination
Ask:
- is hindsight rewriting the event?
- is a coherent story standing in for evidence?
- is confidence inflated because the explanation feels clean?

## Step 3 — Check bias family
Ask:
- what recurring bias class may have been triggered?
- what situation triggered it?
- has this pattern happened before?

## Step 4 — Check operator state
Ask:
- was stress elevated?
- was caution suppressed or exaggerated?
- did prior wins or losses distort risk appetite?
- did body state likely change conviction quality?

## Step 5 — Set corrective boundary
Wave 5 should not stop at diagnosis.
It should define the correction:
- what is now disallowed?
- what requires stronger evidence?
- what review prompt should be added?
- what context sensitivity should be logged?

# 6. ADAPTATION DOCTRINE

## 6.1 Markets and methods are environment-dependent
A method can be valid in one environment and weak in another.
Adaptation is central survival logic, not optional maintenance.

## 6.2 Review should test fit, not only correctness
Aurora review should ask not only whether a method was right or wrong, but whether it was fit or unfit for the ecology it encountered.

## 6.3 Persistence is earned
A method should persist because it continues to fit and continues to earn evidence, not because it once worked.

## 6.4 Adaptation must remain bounded
Adaptation is not random drift.
It should remain tied to evidence, recurring failure modes, and explicit environmental change.

---

# 7. BEHAVIORAL REALISM DOCTRINE

## 7.1 Two-systems realism
Human judgment should be modeled as the interaction of fast intuition and slower checking, not as a single seamless rational engine.

## 7.2 Bias families matter
Recurring decision errors should be named and tracked as patterned bias families.

## 7.3 Mental-accounting distortions matter
Aurora should preserve warnings around:
- sunk-cost attachment
- house-money looseness
- break-even escalation
- transaction-utility seduction

## 7.4 People are not Homo Economicus
Aurora should begin from realistic human behavior, not idealized rational fiction.

---

# 8. PHYSIOLOGY-OF-RISK DOCTRINE

## 8.1 Biology is part of decision quality
Stress, body chemistry, confidence loops, and fatigue-like pressure effects are not background noise.
They can alter risk appetite and judgment quality materially.

## 8.2 Success and failure change state differently
Success can inflate conviction and loosen caution.
Losses can create contraction, revenge risk, or break-even fixation.

## 8.3 State awareness should feed review
Wave 5 should preserve operator-state prompts that help distinguish:
- structural failure
- context failure
- state-driven judgment distortion
