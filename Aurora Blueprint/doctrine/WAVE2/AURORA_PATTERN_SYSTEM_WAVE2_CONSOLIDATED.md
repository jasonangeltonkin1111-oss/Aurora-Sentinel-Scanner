---

# 1. ROOT LAW

Patterns are not strategy families.

Patterns are local structural expressions that may support, weaken, or disambiguate a family thesis.
They should never be allowed to replace family identity, habitat logic, or hostility/review filters.

Wave 2 pattern doctrine must not become:
- a pattern encyclopedia
- a picture-matching engine
- a candlestick naming ritual
- a generic trigger library detached from family context

A valid pattern reading must answer:
- what family is this pattern supporting or competing with?
- what habitat makes this pattern meaningful?
- what anti-pattern or false-positive risk is present?
- what later measurable hypothesis could express it?

---

# 2. WHAT THE PATTERN LAYER IS FOR

Wave 2 pattern doctrine exists to make Aurora:
- better at local structural recognition
- less naive about false positives
- better at linking local expressions to higher-order families
- easier to translate into evidence notes and measurable hypotheses later

The pattern layer sits beneath the family layer.
If family and pattern disagree, the conflict must be made explicit rather than silently flattening one into the other.

---

# 3. CANONICAL PATTERN OBJECTS

Every serious pattern should preserve these objects explicitly.

## 3.1 Pattern identity
What local structural expression is being seen?

## 3.2 Family linkage
Which family or families does this pattern most naturally support?
Which family interpretations does it commonly get mistaken for?

## 3.3 Habitat
Where does this pattern become meaningful rather than decorative?

## 3.4 Anti-pattern / false-positive risk
What nearby lookalike, weak variant, or hostile-context version should not be trusted?

## 3.5 Failure path
How does this pattern usually fail when misread, late, or taken in the wrong habitat?

## 3.6 Measurable hypothesis hook
What later proxy fields or evidence questions could express it without pretending the pattern is already fully formalized?

---

# 4. PRIMARY PATTERN GROUPS

Aurora should preserve patterns as grouped local expressions rather than as one flat list.

## 4.1 Continuation-support patterns

### Core role
These patterns support the idea that an existing family thesis of continuation or re-entry is still structurally alive.

### Natural family linkage
- trend continuation
- pullback continuation

### Common local forms
- orderly compression inside dominant direction
- healthy pause / reset structures
- constructive retracement organization

### Anti-pattern risk
- late drift mistaken for constructive continuation
- weak pauses that actually show exhaustion
- “small pullback” labels used on structurally damaged retracements

### Measurable-hypothesis hook
Later evidence should ask whether the local pause/retracement preserves directional structure or degrades it.

---

## 4.2 Compression / breakout-support patterns

### Core role
These patterns support the idea that containment, pressure, or compression is building toward release.

### Natural family linkage
- breakout / compression release

### Common local forms
- tightening range structures
- boundary pressure clusters
- coiling / narrowing expressions

### Anti-pattern risk
- ordinary noise compression mistaken for meaningful release pressure
- structures that look tight but lack asymmetry or release potential
- already-spent expansion misread as fresh breakout opportunity

### Measurable-hypothesis hook
Later evidence should ask whether containment quality and release quality are both present, not only visual tightness.

---

## 4.3 Rejection / trap-support patterns

### Core role
These patterns support the idea that a directional attempt is being rejected, trapped, or failing to sustain discovery.

### Natural family linkage
- trap / failed-break reversal

### Common local forms
- failed escape and return
- rejection tails or rejection clusters in context
- unsustained break expressions

### Anti-pattern risk
- minor hesitation mislabeled as rejection
- reversal desire outrunning actual failure evidence
- post-break consolidation mistaken for failed break

### Measurable-hypothesis hook
Later evidence should ask whether the attempted directional move genuinely lost sponsorship or only paused.

---

## 4.4 Rotational / boundedness-support patterns

### Core role
These patterns support the idea that the environment remains bounded, traversable, or value-seeking rather than discovery-driven.

### Natural family linkage
- range rotation / mean reversion

### Common local forms
- repeated rejection near boundaries
- internal rotational structures
- lack of convincing directional discovery follow-through

### Anti-pattern risk
- stale boundedness assumption after genuine breakout pressure has started
- repeated range behavior mistaken for continued safety even as structure destabilizes

### Measurable-hypothesis hook
Later evidence should ask what local signs still preserve boundedness and what signs imply imminent escape.

---

## 4.5 Exhaustion / late-stage warning patterns

### Core role
These patterns do not automatically create a new family thesis.
They act as warning structures that a current family may be late, degraded, or vulnerable.

### Natural family linkage
- can pressure continuation families
- can support trap/reversal only when failure evidence matures

### Common local forms
- weakening follow-through after repeated extension
- unstable final push structures
- late-stage overextension without clean reset

### Anti-pattern risk
- calling every mature move “exhausted” too early
- using warning patterns to front-run reversal without failure confirmation

### Measurable-hypothesis hook
Later evidence should ask whether the pattern is only warning of degradation or actually confirming transition.

---

## 4.6 Relationship / spread distortion patterns

### Core role
These patterns support the idea that relationship structure across instruments or legs is distorted in a way that may restore.

### Natural family linkage
- relative-value / stat-arb

### Common local forms
- spread widening/narrowing anomalies
- divergence clusters across linked instruments
- local dislocations within a broader relation structure

### Anti-pattern risk
- treating loose correlation drift as meaningful spread distortion
- ignoring legging risk, costs, or relationship instability

### Measurable-hypothesis hook
Later evidence should ask what relation is being modeled and whether local distortion is large enough to matter after costs.

# 5. FAMILY-TO-PATTERN LINKAGE RULES

## 5.1 Patterns are subordinate to families
A pattern should strengthen or weaken a family reading, not silently define strategy by itself.

## 5.2 One pattern can support multiple families
A local structure can point to more than one family interpretation.
That ambiguity must remain visible until disambiguated by habitat, hostility, or failure evidence.

## 5.3 Pattern without habitat is decoration
A visually named pattern that lacks habitat context should not be treated as strategy information.

## 5.4 Candlestick language is optional, not sovereign
Pattern naming may use candlestick or classical chart vocabulary where useful, but naming must not replace structural meaning.

---

# 6. ANTI-PATTERN / FALSE-POSITIVE DOCTRINE

Every serious pattern group should preserve anti-pattern logic.

## 6.1 Lookalike risk
Some weak, noisy, or late-stage structures visually resemble real patterns but do not deserve the same interpretation.

## 6.2 Context failure risk
A pattern that works in one habitat may be decorative or dangerous in another.

## 6.3 Timing failure risk
A valid pattern seen too late can still be unusable.

## 6.4 Hostility contamination risk
A pattern that is locally recognizable may still be suppressed because execution, volatility, macro, or cross-asset hostility erodes its deployability.

---

# 7. PATTERN FAILURE LAYER

Patterns should usually fail through one or more of:
- wrong family linkage
- wrong habitat
- false-positive visual similarity
- late recognition
- hostile execution surface
- hostile backdrop or regime transition

A pattern is not mature until its false-positive map is clearer than its marketing label.

---

# 8. MEASURABLE-HYPOTHESIS DOCTRINE

Patterns are allowed to remain partly descriptive at this stage, but they should expose later measurable hooks.

These hooks should ask:
- what structural features define the pattern?
- what would count as failure?
- what context variables preserve or weaken it?
- what evidence layer questions does it generate?

Pattern doctrine should not pretend full formalization is already complete.
It should preserve the translation path honestly.

---

# 9. WRAPPER / EA-SAFE TRANSLATION HOOKS

The pattern layer should later feed safer classification objects such as:
- `pattern_group`
- `family_linkage`
- `pattern_confidence`
- `anti_pattern_risk`
- `hostility_contamination_flag`
- `translation_maturity`

These are safer than jumping directly from a named pattern to a live action.

---

# 10. WHAT THE PATTERN LAYER MUST NOT BECOME

Wave 2 pattern doctrine must not become:
- a flat catalog of names
- a trigger engine without family context
- a candlestick-first superstition layer
- a pseudo-quant geometry game detached from structure
- a wrapper-ready decision engine by itself

If the pattern layer loses its subordinate role to family logic, it starts manufacturing false edge.
