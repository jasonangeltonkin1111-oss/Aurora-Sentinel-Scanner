# AURORA STRATEGY FAMILY SYSTEM DEEPENING 001

## PURPOSE

This file records the first deeper-pass sharpening run applied to the consolidated Wave 2 strategy-family doctrine surface.

Its job is not to restate family definitions.
Its job is to make the family system harder to misuse by adding:
- escalation boundaries
- edge-decay and lateness markers
- family-to-hostility interaction rules
- family-to-review/adaptation interaction rules
- stronger suppression guidance

This file should be read after:
- `AURORA_STRATEGY_FAMILY_SYSTEM_WAVE2_CONSOLIDATED.md`

It does not replace that consolidated surface.
It deepens it.

---

# 1. ROOT LAW

A family is not mature just because its identity is clear.

A mature family also needs to answer:
- when it is too late
- when it is being degraded by hostility
- when repeated review evidence should downgrade it
- when apparent opportunity should be suppressed rather than merely scored lower

If a family system cannot express degradation and suppression clearly, it is still too easy to misuse.

---

# 2. ESCALATION BOUNDARIES

## 2.1 Valid -> degraded -> suppress -> invalid

Aurora should not jump from “valid” to “wrong” too quickly.
Each family should be capable of moving through at least four states:

### Valid
Habitat is intact.
No major hostility or review-damage evidence is active.

### Degraded
Core family logic may still be intact, but one or more of the following is weakening edge quality:
- worsening execution surface
- rising hostility
- worse asymmetry
- late-stage extension
- backdrop strain

### Suppress
Do not treat the family as deployable even if structurally recognizable.
Suppression becomes appropriate when degradation is strong enough that:
- follow-through reliability is materially impaired
- implementation burden is too high
- family confusion with a rival interpretation is unresolved
- repeated review evidence shows low-quality outcomes in this context

### Invalid
The family thesis itself no longer fits the environment or structure.
This is not just “hard to trade.”
It is “wrong family for the current reality.”

---

# 3. LATE-STAGE / EDGE-DECAY MARKERS

## 3.1 Trend continuation late-stage markers
Trend continuation should be downgraded or suppressed when:
- extension is already mature enough that asymmetry collapses
- continuation depends on chasing rather than structured persistence
- hostility rises while marginal continuation reward falls

## 3.2 Pullback continuation decay markers
Pullback continuation should be downgraded or suppressed when:
- the retracement is no longer a reset but a genuine structural wound
- re-entry quality deteriorates through repeated weak continuation attempts
- the pullback becomes ambiguous between continuation and reversal

## 3.3 Breakout decay markers
Breakout should be downgraded or suppressed when:
- the “release” is late and already partially spent
- containment quality was weak to begin with
- breakout conditions repeatedly fail under current surface or backdrop conditions

## 3.4 Trap / failed-break decay markers
Trap/reversal should be downgraded or suppressed when:
- rejection evidence is thin, late, or already consumed
- reversal desire is doing more work than failure evidence
- the original directional logic is still structurally healthier than the reversal thesis

## 3.5 Range rotation decay markers
Range rotation should be downgraded or suppressed when:
- boundedness is becoming unstable
- repeated traversals are no longer paying cleanly
- breakout pressure is increasing while mean-reversion assumptions weaken

## 3.6 Relative-value / stat-arb decay markers
Relative-value should be downgraded or suppressed when:
- relationship stability is weakening
- event risk or structural break threatens restoration logic
- implementation burden rises enough to overwhelm expected restoration edge

# 4. FAMILY-TO-HOSTILITY INTERACTION RULES

## 4.1 Continuation families are especially sensitive to late hostility
Trend continuation and pullback continuation should be treated as more fragile when:
- execution burden is rising
- macro or cross-asset backdrop is compressing follow-through
- asymmetry is already thin before hostility is applied

## 4.2 Breakout families are especially sensitive to false-release environments
Breakout families should be suppressed faster when:
- breakout conditions are occurring in thin, trap-prone, or unstable environments
- volatility expansion is disorderly rather than directional
- macro stress is producing noise bursts rather than clean discovery

## 4.3 Range families are especially sensitive to breakout pressure
Range rotation should be downgraded quickly when hostility appears as rising instability or boundary pressure rather than normal internal rotation.

## 4.4 Trap/reversal families are especially sensitive to overinterpretation
Trap/reversal families should be suppressed when hostile context is being used to justify reversal desire without enough actual rejection evidence.

## 4.5 Relative-value families are especially sensitive to hidden implementation hostility
Relative-value logic should be suppressed earlier when:
- relationship logic is still plausible but cost, legging risk, or portfolio interaction degrades deployability

---

# 5. FAMILY-TO-REVIEW / ADAPTATION INTERACTION RULES

## 5.1 Repeated context-specific underperformance should change family status
If a family repeatedly underperforms in the same habitat slice, Aurora should not keep calling it “valid but unlucky.”
It should consider:
- downgrading habitat quality
- tightening evidence standards
- creating a suppression rule for that context

## 5.2 Repeated bias patterns should change how families are read
If review shows that a specific family repeatedly attracts:
- chasing behavior
- premature reversal desire
- break-even escalation
- overconfidence after prior wins

then the family should gain a review-bound caution tag rather than leaving the problem only at the operator level.

## 5.3 Review should distinguish family failure from reader failure
A bad outcome should trigger a split question:
- did the family logic fail?
- or did the operator misread habitat, timing, or hostility?

Without this split, Wave 5 will either unfairly kill good families or protect bad readings.

---

# 6. STRONGER SUPPRESSION GUIDANCE

A family should usually move to suppression when at least one of these becomes load-bearing:
- unresolved family confusion
- hostile context that directly attacks the family’s payoff shape
- repeated negative evidence in the same habitat slice
- implementation burden that erases the family’s expected edge
- late-stage decay that collapses asymmetry

Suppression is not a declaration that the family is false forever.
It is a declaration that the family is not currently fit enough to treat as deployable.

---

# 7. WRAPPER / EA-SAFE DEEPENING HOOKS

The family system should later expose fields such as:
- `family_state`: valid / degraded / suppress / invalid
- `family_confusion_risk`
- `late_stage_decay_flag`
- `hostility_conflict_flag`
- `review_caution_tag`
- `translation_confidence`

These are safer than trying to jump directly from family label to live action.
