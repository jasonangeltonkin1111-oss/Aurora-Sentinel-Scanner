# 4. PRIMARY FAMILY CLASSES

Aurora should preserve these as distinct primary classes.

## 4.1 Trend continuation family

### Core identity
This family captures continuation within an already-advancing directional structure where the dominant condition is persistence, not fresh discovery from neutral equilibrium.

### Habitat
- directional structure already present
- evidence of continuation pressure
- no strong signs of exhaustion or hostile reversal trap
- execution surface still supportive enough for continuation to matter

### Anti-habitat
- late exhausted move with poor asymmetry
- range-dominant conditions pretending to trend
- high hostility that compresses continuation reliability

### Common confusion
Often confused with:
- breakout family
- pullback continuation family

### Anti-confusion rule
A trend continuation setup is not the same as a breakout.
If the edge depends on fresh release from compression or boundary escape, it belongs nearer breakout logic.
If the edge depends on re-entry after retracement, it belongs nearer pullback continuation logic.

### Failure / misuse mapping
Naive misuse happens when ordinary drift is labeled as strong continuation or when late trend exhaustion is mistaken for clean persistence.

### Translation hook
Later evidence should ask:
- how much directional persistence is already present?
- what continuation-quality evidence survives hostile/context filters?

---

## 4.2 Pullback continuation family

### Core identity
This family captures re-entry into an existing directional structure after temporary retracement, pause, or local reset.

### Habitat
- directional structure intact
- retracement does not destroy dominant thesis
- re-entry asymmetry improves relative to chasing the extended move

### Anti-habitat
- retracement that is actually transition or reversal
- environments where the retracement reflects genuine structural failure
- unstable execution surface that erases re-entry advantage

### Common confusion
Often confused with:
- trend continuation family
- trap/reversal family

### Anti-confusion rule
A pullback continuation setup requires a meaningful reset or retracement.
If there is no real reset, it is closer to direct continuation.
If the retracement reflects deeper structural damage, it may be reversal logic instead.

### Failure / misuse mapping
Naive misuse happens when every pause is treated as healthy pullback or when a regime transition is mislabeled as “just a pullback.”

### Translation hook
Later evidence should ask:
- what defines a healthy versus destructive retracement?
- what context variables preserve re-entry quality?

---

## 4.3 Breakout / compression release family

### Core identity
This family captures movement that depends on release from compression, containment, or boundary pressure into a new impulse phase.

### Habitat
- visible containment, compression, or pressure build-up
- credible release mechanism
- enough asymmetry that fresh participation is still worthwhile

### Anti-habitat
- already-mature move dressed up as breakout
- false release into immediate hostile backdrop
- thin or hostile surface where breakout becomes trap-prone

### Common confusion
Often confused with:
- trend continuation family
- trap / failed-break reversal family

### Anti-confusion rule
Breakout logic is not “any move in the same direction.”
It is a family whose edge depends on release from prior containment or compression.

### Failure / misuse mapping
Naive misuse happens when traders chase random expansion without proving real containment existed first.

### Translation hook
Later evidence should ask:
- what measurable conditions define containment?
- what constitutes real release versus noise burst?

---

## 4.4 Trap / failed-break reversal family

### Core identity
This family captures reversal or directional rejection following false escape, failed break, trap behavior, or exhausted continuation that cannot sustain itself.

### Habitat
- false break or unsustained release
- evidence of rejection, trapping, or failed discovery
- asymmetry improves because prior directional commitment is now vulnerable

### Anti-habitat
- strong genuine continuation with no meaningful rejection
- environments where failure evidence is too thin or too late
- reversal attempts made against intact strong continuation without degradation evidence

### Common confusion
Often confused with:
- breakout family
- range rotation family

### Anti-confusion rule
This family requires evidence that the attempted directional escape or continuation is failing.
Without failure evidence, it does not belong here.

### Failure / misuse mapping
Naive misuse happens when every hesitation after expansion is labeled a trap or when reversal desire outruns rejection evidence.

### Translation hook
Later evidence should ask:
- what marks genuine failed discovery?
- what rejection signals survive hostile context and execution filters?

---

## 4.5 Range rotation / mean-reversion family

### Core identity
This family captures rotation or reversion behavior inside bounded, non-trending, or equilibrium-seeking environments where extension is less rewarded than return toward internal value.

### Habitat
- bounded or rotational structure
- no strong evidence of fresh directional discovery
- conditions favoring reversion or internal traversal over sustained breakout

### Anti-habitat
- genuine directional discovery
- compression about to resolve violently
- hostile execution or volatility context that destroys mean-reversion assumptions

### Common confusion
Often confused with:
- pullback continuation family
- trap/reversal family

### Anti-confusion rule
Range rotation assumes boundedness remains meaningful.
If the market is escaping the range for real, the family is wrong.
If the move is a failed escape rather than normal rotation, trap/reversal logic may dominate instead.

### Failure / misuse mapping
Naive misuse happens when stale range assumptions persist after genuine regime transition.

### Translation hook
Later evidence should ask:
- what conditions preserve boundedness?
- what signals indicate range logic is decaying into breakout logic?

---

## 4.6 Relative-value / stat-arb family

### Core identity
This family captures opportunity arising from relationship distortion, spread behavior, or cross-instrument reversion rather than from a single-chart directional story.

### Habitat
- meaningful relation or spread structure exists
- divergence or dislocation has context
- execution and portfolio handling are good enough for relationship logic to matter

### Anti-habitat
- weak or unstable relationship structure
- event-driven breaks in the relation
- costs or portfolio interaction destroy the expected restoration edge

### Common confusion
Often confused with:
- simple mean reversion on one chart
- generic quant category with no family identity

### Anti-confusion rule
Relative-value logic is not just “mean reversion with math words.”
Its edge depends on relationship structure between instruments, legs, or spreads.

### Failure / misuse mapping
Naive misuse happens when loose correlation is mistaken for strong relationship structure or when implementation burden is ignored.

### Translation hook
Later evidence should ask:
- what relation is being modeled?
- what spread behavior or portfolio interaction matters?
- what costs threaten restoration logic?

# 5. FAMILY-TO-FAMILY ANTI-CONFUSION MAP

## Continuation vs breakout
Continuation depends on persistence in an already directional context.
Breakout depends on release from containment or compression.

## Pullback continuation vs trap/reversal
Pullback continuation assumes dominant structure survives the retracement.
Trap/reversal assumes the attempted continuation or break is failing.

## Range rotation vs trap/reversal
Range rotation assumes internal boundedness remains valid.
Trap/reversal assumes a directional attempt has failed and created asymmetric rejection.

## Range rotation vs breakout
Range rotation assumes containment remains intact.
Breakout assumes containment is ending.

## Relative-value vs simple mean reversion
Relative-value depends on relationship structure across instruments or spreads.
Simple mean reversion may exist on a single instrument without multi-leg logic.
