# 2. STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS TO THE MARKET-STATE CANON

## 2.1 Institution-First Rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should interpret market states as outcomes of structured rules of trade and institutional design, not as free-floating geometric events.

### Why this is justified
The guided tour emphasizes auction theory as a way to analyze institutions and rules of trade.

### Canonical implication
State interpretation should continue moving away from pure pattern geometry and toward mechanism-aware structural reading.

---

## 2.2 Auction-As-Market Rule

### Strengthening statement
Aurora can treat auction theory as directly relevant to markets because the framework applies to two-sided interaction and market-like institutions, not only isolated sale events.

### Why this is justified
The guided tour explicitly broadens auction theory toward markets and double-auction logic.

### Canonical implication
The root Wave 1 law that markets are auctions in motion gains stronger theoretical support.

---

## 2.3 Feasibility Caution for State Interpretation

### Strengthening statement
Not every elegant theoretical or visible outcome corresponds to a feasible, implementable, or behaviorally stable market reality.

### Why this is justified
The paper emphasizes incentive compatibility and individual rationality as feasibility constraints.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should keep a stronger barrier against over-reading visible movement as if it implied realizable or stable outcomes automatically.

---

## 2.4 Participation-Condition Sensitivity

### Strengthening statement
State quality and trust can depend materially on who chooses to participate and under what incentives.

### Why this is justified
The paper treats entry and participation as endogenous and outcome-relevant.

### Canonical implication
Ambiguous or unstable states should remain open to the possibility that participation conditions are shaping the visible structure.

---

## 2.5 Information-Structure Caution

### Strengthening statement
Visible state clarity may still be low-trust when correlated information, asymmetry, or uneven informational conditions shape outcomes.

### Why this is justified
The paper highlights richer information environments and asymmetry as central complications.

### Canonical implication
The difference between apparent discovery and trustworthy discovery should remain central in the canon.

---

# 3. STATE-SPECIFIC STRENGTHENING

## 3.1 Directional Discovery State

### Strengthening
Directional Discovery State should be strengthened with an explicit reminder that discovery credibility depends not only on visible displacement, but also on the underlying institution and information environment.

### Upgraded rule
A move may be structurally directional while still remaining lower-trust because of participation and information asymmetry.

---

## 3.2 Transitional / Unstable State

### Strengthening
This state gains stronger theoretical support because mixed structure may reflect genuine institutional and participation complexity rather than mere labeling weakness.

### Upgraded rule
Aurora should remain more tolerant of unresolved ambiguity where information and participation conditions are plausibly shaping outcomes.

---

## 3.3 Failed Break / Trap State

### Strengthening
This state gains added support from the idea that apparent opportunities can fail because their underlying feasibility or information quality was weak from the start.

### Upgraded rule
Failed directional performance should be interpreted with stronger respect for institution-sensitive weakness rather than dismissed as noise.
