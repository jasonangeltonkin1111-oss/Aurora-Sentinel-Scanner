# 3. ROOT EXECUTION-CONTEXT LAW

Aurora must separate:
- **structural validity**
from
- **practical deployability**

A market can be structurally interesting and still be poor deployment terrain.

Practical deployability is shaped not only by visible chart opportunity, but also by:
- location quality
- conviction quality
- facilitation quality
- access urgency
- friction burden
- inventory and order-flow conditions
- mechanism and venue dependence
- orderability under the current market setting

This uploaded Wave 1 stack strongly supports that not every move, not every location, and not every apparent opportunity is equal.

# 4. PRIMARY WAVE 1 EXECUTION-CONTEXT CONCEPTS

## 4.1 Practical Opportunity vs Visible Opportunity

### Canonical definition
Visible opportunity is what the chart appears to offer.
Practical opportunity is what the current auction condition can actually support with credible deployability.

### Why Aurora needs it
Not every visually attractive move is equally actionable.
The present-tense auction condition determines whether apparent opportunity is meaningful.

### Canon law
Aurora must not treat visual pattern quality as sufficient evidence of deployability.

---

## 4.2 Location Quality

### Canonical definition
Location quality is the structural significance of where trade is occurring relative to accepted value, excess, extremes, and the current auction context.

### Why Aurora needs it
Trade location matters enormously.
Moves at the center of accepted trade do not carry the same meaning as moves at meaningful auction extremes or transitions.

### Aurora use
Location quality should inform:
- trust in fade logic
- trust in continuation logic
- confidence in rejection interpretation
- skepticism toward middle-of-range noise

### Canon law
Aurora should not evaluate all price locations equally.

---

## 4.3 Nonconviction / Low-Quality Auction Context

### Canonical definition
Nonconviction is a condition where the market is active enough to move, but not committed enough to provide trustworthy directional performance or clean structural implication.

### Why Aurora needs it
Weak, low-conviction, or rotational conditions often tempt action without supporting reliable deployment.

### Aurora use
This becomes a support concept for:
- degraded but tradable surface
- stay-out context
- weak directional claims
- session/context caution later

### Canon law
Movement alone is not conviction.
Aurora must distinguish active noise from meaningful commitment.

---

## 4.4 Present-Tense Context Superiority

### Canonical definition
Present-tense context superiority is the rule that current market-generated information outranks stale outside interpretation when evaluating deployment quality.

### Why Aurora needs it
A trade that looked attractive earlier may be materially worse if present auction behavior has changed.

### Aurora use
Execution-context evaluation must always be based on current condition, not just on how a setup looked earlier.

---

## 4.5 Trade Facilitation Quality

### Canonical definition
Trade facilitation quality is the degree to which the auction is currently facilitating orderly participation rather than merely printing movement.

### Why Aurora needs it
When facilitation quality is weak, visible movement is less trustworthy.

### Aurora use
This is one of the strongest bridges between the market-state canon and execution-context doctrine.

### Canon law
Aurora should ask not only where trade is happening, but how well the market is currently facilitating trade there.

---

## 4.6 Directional Trust Quality

### Canonical definition
Directional trust quality is the degree to which a directional move deserves confidence as a deployable directional auction rather than a low-quality directional attempt.

### Why Aurora needs it
The distinction between attempted direction and directional performance is not only structural; it also affects deployment confidence.

### Aurora use
Low directional trust quality should down-rank:
- breakout chasing
- continuation assumptions
- late directional commitment

High directional trust quality should require:
- stronger directional performance
- signs of acceptance
- better location/context than mere breakout print

---

## 4.7 Inventory Distortion Risk

### Canonical definition
Inventory distortion risk is the danger that current movement reflects short-horizon inventory correction or imbalance rather than durable structural opportunity.

### Why Aurora needs it
Not every move should be trusted as a state change.
Some moves are inventory-driven distortions.

### Aurora use
This concept protects Aurora from overrating:
- liquidation spikes
- inventory-correction moves
- short-horizon distortions mistaken for durable authority

---

## 4.8 Immediacy Cost

### Canonical definition
Immediacy cost is the degradation in deployability that appears when access to the opportunity requires urgent execution under less favorable conditions.

### Why Aurora needs it
A trade that requires immediacy is inherently more fragile, costly, or degraded than one that does not.

### Aurora use
Execution-context doctrine should downgrade visible opportunities that depend on urgent access in weak conditions.

### Canon law
Aurora should not assume that a setup is equally deployable regardless of urgency.

---

## 4.9 Spread Is Structural, Not Cosmetic

### Canonical definition
Spread burden is a structural expression of market conditions, not a minor nuisance layered on top of otherwise pure opportunity.

### Why Aurora needs it
Spread reflects risk, order arrival, inventory pressure, and market viability rather than incidental noise.

### Aurora use
Spread belongs in the execution-context layer as a first-class deployment condition.

### Canon law
Aurora should treat spread burden as meaningful context, not as a cosmetic afterthought.

---

## 4.10 Order-Flow and Inventory Distortion

### Canonical definition
Visible movement can reflect order-flow pressure or inventory adjustment rather than durable opportunity.

### Why Aurora needs it
Low-trust execution environments often arise from flow and inventory conditions, not just from visible chart messiness.

### Aurora use
This sharpens:
- inventory distortion risk
- low-trust discovery warnings
- caution in one-sided or stressed conditions

### Canon law
Aurora should not interpret all directional movement as equally deployable when underlying flow conditions are likely distorting practical quality.

---

## 4.11 Trading Rules Shape Deployability

### Canonical definition
Deployability depends in part on the explicit or implicit rules of the trading mechanism that produced the opportunity.

### Why Aurora needs it
The mechanism is not a neutral pipe.
Trading rules shape prices and therefore shape what price movement means for deployment.

### Aurora use
Execution-context doctrine should remain mechanism-aware and venue-aware at the conceptual level.

### Canon law
Similar-looking chart conditions are not automatically equally tradable across different structures.

---

## 4.12 Orderability

### Canonical definition
Orderability is the degree to which an apparent opportunity can actually be expressed through workable order instructions under the current market setting.

### Why Aurora needs it
Practical opportunity depends partly on whether the opportunity can be expressed through orders that fit the current environment.

### Aurora use
This supports a stronger distinction between:
- visually attractive setup
- executable setup

### Canon law
Practical opportunity is inseparable from the ability to express that opportunity with workable order instructions.

---

## 4.13 Trader Role / Venue Dependence

### Canonical definition
Execution quality depends in part on who is trading, where they are trading, and under what venue rules they interact.

### Why Aurora needs it
Market quality is not only internal chart structure; it is also a function of the surrounding trading environment.

### Aurora use
Execution-context doctrine should remain conceptually aware that market quality depends partly on trader roles and venue organization.

### Canon law
Aurora should avoid treating every visible move as equally meaningful across all market settings.

---

## 4.14 Information-Sensitive Trust Caution

### Canonical definition
A visibly strong move may still deserve lower deployment trust if the environment likely concentrates better-informed participation or adverse-selection pressure.

### Why Aurora needs it
Visible strength is not automatically deployable trustworthy strength.

### Aurora use
This sharpens the low-trust side of directional opportunity evaluation.

### Canon law
Aurora must continue to separate apparent strength from deployable trustworthy strength.

# 5. PRIMARY WAVE 1 SURFACE STATES — CONSOLIDATED VERSION

## 5.1 Structurally Valid but Low-Trust Surface

### Canonical definition
This is a condition where the market-state read may be valid, but present-tense conviction, facilitation quality, access quality, or directional trust are too weak to justify normal confidence in deployability.

### Structural markers
- visible movement without strong directional performance
- location not supportive enough
- weak commitment around the move
- elevated risk that the move is inventory correction or low-conviction noise
- friction burden or urgency degradation

### Main risks
- forcing trades because the setup “exists”
- confusing technical possibility with practical quality

### Strengthened rule
This surface should be used more aggressively whenever visible opportunity exists but the practical cost/quality environment is clearly degraded.

---

## 5.2 Return-to-Value / Responsive Surface

### Canonical definition
This is a condition where responsive activity is dominant enough that apparent opportunity away from value should be treated skeptically unless stronger signs of acceptance emerge.

### Structural markers
- excursion from value without sustained facilitation there
- market behavior favoring return rather than migration
- rejection more plausible than continuation

### Main risks
- chasing weak continuation away from value
- underestimating responsive activity

---

## 5.3 Discovery-with-Trust Surface

### Canonical definition
This is a condition where directional discovery is not only visible structurally, but also supported by enough acceptance, location quality, directional performance, access quality, and deployability realism to justify stronger deployment confidence.

### Structural markers
- directional movement with credible performance
- evidence of acceptance rather than momentary excursion
- stronger migration away from prior accepted trade
- less evidence that the move is merely inventory correction
- no major immediacy, spread, or orderability degradation undermining access

### Main risks
- late entry after clean discovery is already spent
- confusing high trust with infinite follow-through

### Strengthened rule
A move can be structurally directional and still fail to qualify as high-trust deployment terrain if immediacy cost, spread burden, adverse-selection-like context, or orderability problems are too severe.

---

## 5.4 Excess / Exhaustion Surface

### Canonical definition
This is a condition where excess or end-of-auction behavior reduces continuation trust and increases the relevance of rejection, responsive activity, or transition.

### Structural markers
- signs of end-of-auction behavior
- weaker facilitation at the extension
- growing mismatch between extension and acceptance quality
- worsening practical deployment conditions into the extension

### Main risks
- deploying continuation logic into auction termination
- underweighting the risk of rejection or reversal

### Strengthened rule
Continuation trust should fall faster when structural extension combines with worsening practical deployment conditions.

---

## 5.5 Nonconviction / Stay-Out Surface

### Canonical definition
This is a condition where the market is active enough to tempt action but not trustworthy enough to justify strong confidence in deployment.

### Structural markers
- mixed directional evidence
- weak conviction
- poor clarity about whether the market is discovering or rotating
- insufficient quality of facilitation for strong trust
- uncertain access quality or degraded orderability

### Main risks
- forcing opportunity
- overtrading low-information conditions

### Strengthened rule
A market can be active enough to tempt and still be poor enough in execution-context terms that staying out is the higher-quality interpretation.

# 6. EXECUTION-CONTEXT RULES — CONSOLIDATED VERSION

## Rule 1 — Not all opportunities are equal
A visible structural event may still have poor deployment quality.

## Rule 2 — Location matters
Opportunity at extremes, transitions, and meaningful value relationships is different from opportunity in structurally poor locations.

## Rule 3 — Conviction matters
Directional movement without directional performance should not be trusted like clean discovery.

## Rule 4 — Responsive environments degrade continuation trust
When responsive activity dominates, continuation quality falls and return-to-value logic becomes more relevant.

## Rule 5 — Inventory distortion can mimic structural opportunity
Short-horizon correction and imbalance can create misleading movement.

## Rule 6 — Present-tense condition outranks stale setup memory
Current market-generated information should determine deployment confidence.

## Rule 7 — Excess reduces continuation trust
End-of-auction behavior increases the risk that continuation logic is late or degraded.

## Rule 8 — Immediacy degrades deployability
A move that requires urgent access under weak conditions deserves lower trust.

## Rule 9 — Spread is structural, not cosmetic
Spread burden reflects market conditions and must be treated as a first-class part of deployment quality.

## Rule 10 — Order-flow and inventory can degrade trust
Visible movement may reflect flow pressure or inventory adjustment rather than durable opportunity.

## Rule 11 — Trading mechanism matters
Deployability depends partly on how the market is organized and how price is being formed.

## Rule 12 — Orderability matters
A visually attractive move is not automatically executable in a workable way.

## Rule 13 — Trader role and venue matter
Market quality depends partly on who is interacting and under what venue rules.

## Rule 14 — Apparent strength is not automatically trustworthy strength
Information-sensitive environments can produce visible strength that deserves lower deployment trust.
