# 2. STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS TO EXECUTION-CONTEXT DOCTRINE

## 2.1 Immediacy Cost Rule

### Strengthening statement
A trade that requires immediacy should be treated as inherently more fragile, costly, or degraded than one that does not.

### Why this is justified
Market Microstructure Theory explicitly ties transaction cost and price formation to immediacy.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should downgrade visible opportunities that depend on urgent access in weak conditions.

### What this changes
This strengthens caution around:
- chasing late movement
- trusting narrow geometry under poor access conditions
- assuming a setup is equally deployable regardless of urgency

---

## 2.2 Spread Is Structural, Not Cosmetic

### Strengthening statement
Spread burden is a structural expression of market conditions, not a minor nuisance layered on top of otherwise pure opportunity.

### Why this is justified
Market-maker and inventory models tie spread directly to risk, order arrival, and market viability.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should continue to treat spread as a first-class deployment condition.

### What this changes
This strengthens the existing Q1 rule that practical opportunity can be materially worse than visible opportunity.

---

## 2.3 Inventory and Order-Flow Distortion Rule

### Strengthening statement
Visible movement can reflect order-flow pressure or inventory adjustment rather than durable opportunity.

### Why this is justified
Market Microstructure Theory explicitly links quote setting, spread, and dealer behavior to order arrival and inventory management.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should treat some moves as low-trust not because they are structurally impossible, but because their practical quality is weakened by underlying flow conditions.

### What this changes
This sharpens:
- inventory distortion risk
- low-trust discovery warnings
- caution in one-sided or stressed conditions

---

## 2.4 Trading Rules Shape Deployability

### Strengthening statement
Deployability depends in part on the explicit or implicit rules of the trading mechanism that produced the opportunity.

### Why this is justified
Market Microstructure Theory says trading rules shape prices. Trading and Exchanges emphasizes how orders, venues, and market organization affect execution.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should remain mechanism-aware and venue-aware at the conceptual level.

### What this changes
This strengthens caution against assuming that similar-looking chart conditions are equally tradable across different structures.

---

## 2.5 Orderability Rule

### Strengthening statement
Practical opportunity depends partly on whether the opportunity can be expressed through workable order instructions under the current market setting.

### Why this is justified
Trading and Exchanges treats orders as the fundamental building blocks of execution and trading strategy.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should later connect surface quality with orderability and order-property fit.

### What this changes
This supports a future stronger distinction between:
- visually attractive setup
- executable setup

---

## 2.6 Trader Role / Venue Dependence

### Strengthening statement
Execution quality depends in part on who is trading, where they are trading, and under what venue rules they interact.

### Why this is justified
Trading and Exchanges explains that trader roles and venue organization materially affect market behavior.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should remain conceptually aware that market quality is not only internal chart structure; it is also a function of the surrounding trading environment.

### What this changes
This reinforces the need to avoid treating every visible move as equally meaningful across all contexts.

---

## 2.7 Information-Sensitive Trust Caution

### Strengthening statement
A visibly strong move may still deserve lower deployment trust if the environment likely concentrates better-informed participation or adverse-selection pressure.

### Why this is justified
Auction Theory2’s winner's-curse logic supports stronger caution around information asymmetry and trust.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should strengthen the difference between:
- apparent strength
- deployable trustworthy strength

### What this changes
This sharpens the low-trust side of directional opportunity evaluation.

# 3. SURFACE-STATE STRENGTHENING

## 3.1 Discovery-with-Trust Surface

### Strengthening
This surface should now require not only visible directional discovery, but also stronger confidence that the move is not being materially degraded by immediacy cost, spread burden, or adverse-selection-like context.

### Upgraded rule
A move can be structurally directional and still fail to qualify as high-trust deployment terrain if the access conditions are too poor.

---

## 3.2 Structurally Valid but Low-Trust Surface

### Strengthening
This state gains stronger support from:
- immediacy cost
- spread burden
- order-flow distortion
- mechanism dependence
- information-sensitive trust caution

### Upgraded rule
This state should be used more aggressively whenever the market offers visible opportunity but the practical cost/quality environment is clearly degraded.

---

## 3.3 Nonconviction / Stay-Out Surface

### Strengthening
This surface now gains additional support from the idea that weak practical opportunity may arise not only from poor structure, but also from poor market-quality conditions and uncertain access quality.

### Upgraded rule
A market can be “active enough to tempt” yet still be poor enough in execution-context terms that staying out is the higher-quality interpretation.

---

## 3.4 Excess / Exhaustion Surface

### Strengthening
This surface gains added realism because late-stage movement may become increasingly expensive, fragile, or information-sensitive even before pure structural failure is fully obvious.

### Upgraded rule
Continuation trust should fall faster when structural extension combines with worsening practical deployment conditions.
