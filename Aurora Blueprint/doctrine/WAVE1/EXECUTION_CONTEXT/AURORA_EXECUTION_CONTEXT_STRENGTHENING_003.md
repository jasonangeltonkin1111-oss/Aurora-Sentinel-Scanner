# 2. EXECUTION-CONTEXT STRENGTHENING ADDITIONS

## 2.1 Visible opportunity and deployable opportunity must stay separate

### Strengthening statement
Aurora should preserve a harder distinction between:
- a structure that appears valid on chart
- an opportunity that remains practically deployable under real execution conditions

### Why this is justified
Flash Boys is explicitly planned as a source for market plumbing, fairness, latency, and deployability realism rather than chart-level pattern logic.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should preserve the rule that:
- execution hostility can invalidate practical opportunity without changing the underlying chart interpretation
- deployability loss is a separate failure mode from structural invalidity

### What this changes
This strengthens the core Wave 1 state-vs-surface boundary and gives it more real-world bite.

---

## 2.2 Venue and plumbing are part of surface hostility

### Strengthening statement
Execution context should remain sensitive to the possibility that venue structure, plumbing asymmetry, or latency-sensitive conditions create practical hostility beyond visible spread or chart cleanliness.

### Why this is justified
Flash Boys is specifically valuable as a market-plumbing and fairness/latency realism source.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve a hostile-surface branch where:
- execution degradation is caused by the market environment itself
- fairness of interaction cannot be assumed
- visible chart cleanliness does not guarantee deployability symmetry

### What this changes
This deepens hostile-surface realism inside Execution Context Surface without turning Aurora into a broker-hack playbook.

---

## 2.3 Continuity assumptions should be treated cautiously in hostile execution environments

### Strengthening statement
Execution assumptions should remain cautious about the possibility that price behavior, fills, and loss realization do not unfold in smooth, continuous steps under stress.

### Why this is justified
Ralph Vince explicitly emphasizes worst-case thinking and rejects simplistic continuity assumptions in real market behavior.

### Canonical implication
Execution-context doctrine should preserve a warning that:
- risk may jump faster than naive chart expectations imply
- continuous fill or loss assumptions can degrade in hostile conditions
- execution surface realism should resist straight-line assumptions

### What this changes
This gives Wave 1 execution-context doctrine stronger contact with later Wave 4 fragility realism.

---

## 2.4 Worst-case thinking belongs in execution-surface realism, not only in portfolio logic

### Strengthening statement
Worst-case thinking should inform not only sizing but also whether an execution environment is acceptable in the first place.

### Why this is justified
Vince frames risk design from worst-case scenarios, and Flash Boys strengthens the case that market structure itself can become part of the problem.

### Canonical implication
Aurora should preserve a rule that:
- execution environment quality must be judged partly by worst-case failure behavior
- apparent opportunity should be discounted when the surface is fragile under stress

### What this changes
This strengthens Aurora’s ability to treat hostile execution as a genuine disqualifier rather than as an afterthought.
