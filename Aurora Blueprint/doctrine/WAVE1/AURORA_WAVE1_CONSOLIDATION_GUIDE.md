# 4. MERGE LAW

## Law 1 — strengthen, do not regress
Never replace grounded doctrine with flatter scaffold language.

## Law 2 — integrate only what is doctrinally active
If a strengthening point is clearly framed as later residue for research, volatility, review, family, or pattern work, do not force it into the consolidated Wave 1 core.

## Law 3 — preserve distinctions
Do not collapse these distinctions during merge:
- state vs surface
- visible opportunity vs practical opportunity
- attempted direction vs directional performance
- apparent strength vs trustworthy strength
- structural validity vs deployability
- discovery vs mechanism-sensitive weak-trust movement

## Law 4 — keep lineage explicit
The consolidated files should name the files they were merged from.

## Law 5 — preserve provisionality honestly
If a concept is stronger after Source Set 002 but still not fully universalized, mark it as strengthened rather than pretending all ambiguity is gone.
