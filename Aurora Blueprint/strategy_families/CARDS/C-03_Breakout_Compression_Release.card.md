# STRUCTURAL ENTRY LOGIC (HIGH LEVEL ONLY)
At a high level, the family becomes plausible when:
- prior compression or balance is clear
- break beyond reference structure becomes more than momentary excursion
- continuation trust becomes stronger than fake-break or trap risk

# STRUCTURAL INVALIDATION LOGIC (HIGH LEVEL ONLY)
At a high level, the family weakens or invalidates when:
- the break fails to hold or gain acceptance
- trap / failed-break logic becomes stronger than continuation
- overlap / instability remains dominant after the attempted release

# MAIN FAMILY COMPETITORS
- Trend Continuation
- Failed Break / Trap Reversal
- Balance Rotation / Range Mean-Reversion

# FAMILY REJECTION CONDITIONS
Reject or suppress the family when:
- the move is only wick-only excursion
- commitment beyond the break is unresolved
- trap/failure logic is stronger than continuation logic
- low-trust expansion remains inside unstable overlap

# FUTURE PATTERN CHILDREN
- compression release structures
- balance break and hold patterns
- post-break acceptance structures
- false release rejection patterns

# OPPORTUNITY STAGE PROFILE
- Native stages: `STAGE_EMERGENT`, `STAGE_DEVELOPING`, `STAGE_CONFIRMED`
- Legitimate but more sensitive: `STAGE_CONTINUATION` when release remains alive after acceptance
- Usually weak or invalid: `STAGE_LATE`, `STAGE_EXHAUSTED` once the release becomes chased, overextended, or failure-prone
