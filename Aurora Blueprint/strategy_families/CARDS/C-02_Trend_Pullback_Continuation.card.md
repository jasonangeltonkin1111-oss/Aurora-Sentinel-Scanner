Participate when a directional market remains structurally intact and the opportunity appears through temporary retracement rather than immediate expansion print.

- Trend Pullback
- retracement within intact Directional Discovery

# STRUCTURAL ENTRY LOGIC (HIGH LEVEL ONLY)
At a high level, the family becomes plausible when:
- prior directional structure is already established
- retracement does not fully destroy the prior directional advantage
- continuation still dominates over failure or balance re-formation logic

# STRUCTURAL INVALIDATION LOGIC (HIGH LEVEL ONLY)
At a high level, the family weakens or invalidates when:
- retracement becomes structural deterioration rather than orderly pullback
- opposing acceptance becomes stronger than continuation trust
- balance re-formation or failed-state logic overtakes continuation logic

# MAIN FAMILY COMPETITORS
- Trend Continuation
- Failed Break / Trap Reversal
- Balance Rotation / Range Mean-Reversion

# FAMILY REJECTION CONDITIONS
Reject or suppress the family when:
- pullback is actually a failed state
- balance is re-forming
- overlap / compression is growing instead of resolving
- reversal logic is stronger than continuation logic

# FUTURE PATTERN CHILDREN
- orderly pullback continuation structures
- post-break retracement hold patterns
- trend resumption structures

# OPPORTUNITY STAGE PROFILE
- Native stages: `STAGE_DEVELOPING`, `STAGE_CONFIRMED`, `STAGE_CONTINUATION`
- Legitimate but more sensitive: `STAGE_REENTRY`, `STAGE_MATURE`
- Usually weak or invalid: `STAGE_LATE` when pullback quality is actually deterioration, `STAGE_EXHAUSTED` when trend repair is no longer credible

# BUCKET / REGIME FIT NOTES
- Strongest where directional structure survives retracement cleanly and execution burden remains bounded
- Can thin out in hostile buckets where pullbacks mutate into failed trend state too quickly
