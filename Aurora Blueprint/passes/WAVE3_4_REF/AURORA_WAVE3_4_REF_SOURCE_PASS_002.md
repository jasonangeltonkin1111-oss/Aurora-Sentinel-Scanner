# 2. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 4 HOSTILE-ENVIRONMENT REALISM

## 2.1 Credit-flow stress is a real hostile-environment driver

### Extracted truth
Dalio explicitly frames credit as purchasing power and debt as the future obligation created by that purchasing power. He then describes debt cycles as cause-effect sequences in which credit expansion lifts spending, incomes, and asset prices until debt servicing outruns income capacity, at which point the cycle reverses into contraction. fileciteturn83file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 4 should preserve the rule that hostile environments are not only local volatility or execution-friction problems. They can also be driven by credit-flow contraction and debt-service stress that degrade follow-through, liquidity, and payoff quality.

### Why this matters
This gives Aurora a deeper macro hostile-context layer that is tied to credit mechanics rather than only market mood.

---

## 2.2 Bubble / top / depression / deleveraging phases matter

### Extracted truth
Dalio lays out the archetypal big debt cycle as phase logic, including early cycle, bubble, top, depression, beautiful deleveraging, and normalization, and shows that long-term debt cycles are driven by the accumulation of short-term cycles until interest-rate relief can no longer continue. fileciteturn83file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 4 should preserve the rule that hostile backdrop conditions often differ by debt-cycle phase. Not all stress environments are the same. Bubble-top fragility, depression liquidity stress, and deleveraging recovery all create different hostile-context profiles.

---

## 2.3 Deflationary and inflationary crises are different environments

### Extracted truth
Dalio distinguishes deflationary depressions from inflationary depressions, largely based on whether debt is denominated in one’s own currency or in foreign currency, and explains that foreign-currency debt makes policy response harder and the crisis more inflationary and currency-sensitive. fileciteturn83file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 4 should preserve the rule that hostile environments differ materially when a system is facing deflationary debt compression versus inflationary / currency-stress debt compression.

### Why this matters
This is especially relevant to later FX, macro-overlay, and cross-asset context work.

---

## 2.4 Policy response changes the hostility shape

### Extracted truth
Dalio identifies four major policy levers for handling debt crises: austerity, defaults/restructurings, central-bank money creation / purchases, and transfers of money and credit. fileciteturn83file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 4 should preserve the rule that macro hostility is partly defined by the response regime. The same debt stress can produce different market backdrops depending on which policy levers dominate.

# 3. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 3 RESEARCH / EVALUATION DISCIPLINE

## 3.1 Templates built from many cases are stronger than one-off narratives

### Extracted truth
Dalio says the archetypal template was built from many historical debt crises, including a compendium of dozens of cases, in order to identify the repeated cause-effect relationships behind debt crises. fileciteturn83file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that robust market understanding is strengthened by repeated case study comparison and archetypal templates, not by single dramatic case narratives.

---

## 3.2 Big-picture context matters for evaluating active claims

### Extracted truth
Dalio repeatedly frames his perspective as that of an investor forced to bet on macroeconomic shifts in real time, using historical templates to prepare for storms not personally experienced. fileciteturn83file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that active claims should be evaluated inside broader macro/liquidity context when that context is load-bearing, rather than in isolated setup space only.

---

## 3.3 Debt crises should be viewed as mechanism chains, not as random events

### Extracted truth
Dalio presents debt crises as logically driven sequences in which borrowing pulls spending forward, which later requires contraction, making the cycle structurally recurrent rather than mysterious. fileciteturn83file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that macro stress should be evaluated through mechanism chains and causal structure, not only through label-based storytelling.
