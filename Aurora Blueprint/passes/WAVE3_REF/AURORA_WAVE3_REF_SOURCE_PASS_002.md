# 2. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR WAVE 3 RESEARCH / METHOD DISCIPLINE

## 2.1 Research should include field reality, not only market gossip

### Extracted truth
Kenneth Fisher’s preface explicitly presents Philip Fisher’s scuttlebutt approach as studying whether a firm is strong or weak by drawing out information from competitors, customers, suppliers, and other informed sources rather than relying on rumor and Wall Street noise. It also frames the famous fifteen points as universal business-quality tests, not as formulaic screen outputs. fileciteturn119file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that real research can require external reality checks and domain-specific inquiry rather than only price screens, narratives, or secondhand opinion.

---

## 2.2 Qualitative quality matters alongside numerical cheapness

### Extracted truth
The preface to Common Stocks explicitly frames the fifteen points as describing firms with huge product and market potential, effective research, strong sales organization, healthy margins, excellent management, and unusual integrity. fileciteturn119file0

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that “cheapness” without durable quality can be a weak research conclusion.

---

## 2.3 Simplicity can outperform sophistication theater

### Extracted truth
Greenblatt explicitly frames his method as a simple, step-by-step process that can beat the market by combining good businesses with bargain prices and insists that sophisticated formulas are not what make the difference. fileciteturn119file1

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that simple procedures with coherent logic can deserve priority over complexity theater when robustness is the true target.

---

## 2.4 Any active method must beat a safe baseline and justify its risk

### Extracted truth
Early in Greenblatt’s book, the argument is built from comparing any active opportunity with a no-risk government bond baseline, explicitly requiring other investments to justify themselves by offering more than the risk-free alternative. fileciteturn119file1

### Aurora translation
Wave 3 should preserve the rule that active claims should be evaluated relative to meaningful baselines rather than only in self-referential performance language.
