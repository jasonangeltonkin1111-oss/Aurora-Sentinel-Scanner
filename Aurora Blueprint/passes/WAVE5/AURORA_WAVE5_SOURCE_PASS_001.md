## 2.2 Review must test fit, not only correctness

### Extracted truth
Adaptive Markets suggests that behavioral biases and market behavior can be consistent with adaptation to changing environments rather than static irrationality. ŅłĆfileciteŅłéturn101file1Ņłü

### Aurora translation
Review should ask not only:
- was the method right or wrong?

but also:
- was it fit or unfit for the environment it encountered?

### Why this matters
This prevents false pruning of methods that failed because the ecology changed.

# 3. CORE EXTRACTIONS FOR BEHAVIORAL REALISM

## 3.1 System 1 / System 2 rule

### Extracted truth
Thinking, Fast and Slow introduces the two-systems framework: fast, automatic processing versus slower, effortful control. ŅłĆfileciteŅłéturn101file2Ņłü

### Aurora translation
Aurora should preserve a behavioral realism layer in which:
- fast intuition can be useful but bias-prone
- slower reasoning can correct but is effortful and limited

### Why this matters
This gives Aurora a structured vocabulary for diagnosing judgment failures in analysis and review.

---

## 3.2 Heuristics and biases are recurring, not random

### Extracted truth
Thinking, Fast and Slow explicitly organizes judgment errors around recurring biases and heuristics such as availability, anchoring, framing, and small-sample error. ŅłĆfileciteŅłéturn101file2Ņłü

### Aurora translation
Behavioral realism should treat repeated judgment errors as patterned and diagnosable rather than as isolated accidents.

---

## 3.3 Humans are not Homo Economicus

### Extracted truth
Misbehaving critiques the unrealistic rational-agent model and catalogs recurring deviations such as mental accounting, endowment effect, sunk costs, house money, and break-even effects. ŅłĆfileciteŅłéturn101file3Ņłü

### Aurora translation
Aurora should preserve the rule that operator behavior and market participation are not best modeled as perfectly rational optimization.

### Why this matters
This supports more realistic assumptions in review, research, and adaptation.

---

## 3.4 Mental accounting distorts choices

### Extracted truth
Misbehaving emphasizes mental accounting, transaction utility, sunk-cost behavior, and house-money / break-even effects. ŅłĆfileciteŅłéturn101file3Ņłü

### Aurora translation
Behavioral realism should preserve warnings that:
- past gains change risk appetite
- sunk costs can trap behavior
- house-money thinking can loosen discipline
- break-even thinking can escalate bad risk

---

## 3.5 Physiology is not noise

### Extracted truth
The Hour Between Dog and Wolf frames biology as causally relevant to risk-taking and decision quality, not as background decoration. ŅłĆfileciteŅłéturn101file0Ņłü

### Aurora translation
Behavioral realism should include:
- physiology-of-risk awareness
- stress-state degradation
- overconfidence fueled by biochemical feedback
